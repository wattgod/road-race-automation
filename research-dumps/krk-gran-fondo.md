## Identity resolution

This is specifically **Krk Granfondo Falkensteiner Punat** — an island circuit based in Punat.
Confirmed distinct from:
- **Istria Gran Fondo** (Umag, Istrian peninsula, UCI Gran Fondo World Series qualifier, 105km,
  April 11 2026 — different region, different race)
- **Granfondo Pag — Cycling on the Moon** (Novalja, island of Pag, ~107km/1,210m in 2025)
- **4 Islands** (Krk/Cres/Lošinj/Rab — an MTB stage race, not this road event)

## Course

Punat → Krk town → Malinska → Njivice → Omišalj → inland to Dobrinj/Vrbnik → climb to Treskavac
Pass → descend to Baška → 7.9km return climb → Punat. All paved public roads, no gravel/off-road
sectors. Four officially categorized ascents (official race propositions PDF):
- Km 3.2: 5.8km at 1.9%, 108m gain, Category 4
- Km 36.4: 3.8km at 5.4%, 204m gain, Category 3
- Km 52.2: 3.1km at 5.8%, 178m gain, Category 3
- Km 67.0: 7.9km at 3.6%, 288m gain, Category 3

Krk is Croatia's **second**-largest Adriatic island (behind Cres), not the largest — correct
any "largest island" claim.

Post-race coverage reports extensive road control and a 190-person event operating team
(Novi list 2026 post-race report).

## History

Founded 2024 (inaugural edition April 27 2024), organized by Hoteli Punat/Falkensteiner Punat
with Punat Tourist Board support. Race directors Marin Kirin and Toni Dujmenović. Run with
Croatian Cycling Federation and Granfondo World Tour — not a UCI Gran Fondo World Series
qualifier (unlike Istria).

## Credible criticisms

- Document-control problems in the official propositions PDF: wrong year printed ("2025"
  instead of 2026), inconsistent distance rounding, a leftover Zadar-event reference
- Inconsistent field-size reporting across organizer materials (400 vs 450 vs 500)
- Aggressive relative cutoff at km 52 (40 min behind leader) — stricter than typical
  recreational-fondo pacing given the 2026 winner's time of 1:59:14 for 82km

# Research Dump: Granfondo Alpe Adria

Wave 6 / 2026-07-24. Researched via codex gpt-5.6-sol web search.

## Quick Facts
- Status: **Defunct / identity-failed.** No confirmed 2026 (or any recent) edition of a standalone race named "Granfondo Alpe Adria" exists. No formal cancellation announcement was found, so "cancelled" is too specific — but no evidence of continued operation either.
- Closest real event: **Alpe-Adria Giro**, the long road race within the **Alpe-Adria Bikefestival Villach**, last substantiated on **19 June 2016**. The festival's own site is frozen on 2016 content, its results archive covers only 2014-2016, and its homepage now carries unrelated pharmacy-spam links suggesting compromise/abandonment.
- Villach's current (2026) cycling-events listing shows Velovista, ROSA200, Carinthia200, and Enduro nationals — no Granfondo/Alpe-Adria Giro. Carinthia200 (carinthia200.com) is Villach's current large road event but explicitly calls its 2026 running a premiere; no evidence it's a rebrand/continuation of the Alpe-Adria Giro.
- The current "Alpe Adria Tour" 2026 circuit is a 6-race series; its Austrian round is the ARBÖ Kärnten Radmarathon in Bad Kleinkirchheim, NOT a Villach "Granfondo Alpe Adria."

## Identity Check — on-file profile is wrong on multiple axes
- **Wrong official site.** bike-alpeadria.eu is NOT a race site — it's the EU-funded "Bike Alpe-Adria" cross-border cycling-route network (launched 2020 per the Slovenian Tourist Board as an INTER BIKE II platform; a 2025 Interreg update calls it a route-planning site covering Adriabike, the Soča Valley, Parenzana, and the Alpe-Adria Radweg touring route). The real historical race's official info source was alpe-adria-bikefestival.com, per the event's own participation conditions.
- **Wrong name.** The real event was never documented as a standalone "Granfondo Alpe Adria" — it was the "Alpe-Adria Giro," the long-course road marathon within the broader Alpe-Adria Bikefestival Villach (which also included a Medio Giro, MTB Giro, and Dobratsch hillclimb as separate race variants, not renames).
- **Wrong route feature.** The on-file "Karawanken tunnels" claim is unsupported — the real route used Predil Pass and Vršič Pass, not the Karawanken motorway tunnel (an unrelated ~8km A11/A2 road tunnel south of Villach).
- **Wrong founding year.** On-file says 2010; verified founding is 2014 (City of Villach announcement + Austrian Alpine Club report both call the June 2014 running the premiere). The erroneous 2010 date likely traces to the opening of an Alpe-Adria cycle-TOURING-route section near Villach on 27 June 2010 — an unrelated infrastructure event, not this race.
- Confirmed as a real, physical, timed mass-participation road event when it existed (not a motorcycle race, not virtual/Zwift) — official conditions specify adult bicycle participants, helmets, chip timing, public-road rules.

## Verified historical course facts (Alpe-Adria Giro, primarily 2016 final edition)
- Distance: 165 km (2016 official spec). Prior editions varied by publication: 2014 press preview said 169km/2,430m; Villach's own 2014 material said ~160km/2,280m; final 2016 official course spec was 165km/2,280m. The on-file 160.9km/2,073m pairing matches none of these exactly and is unverified.
- Start/finish: Villach, out-and-back through Italy and Slovenia.
- Route: Villach → Tarvisio → Cave del Predil → Predil Pass → Bovec → Vršič Pass → Kranjska Gora → Tarvisio → Villach.
- Countries: Austria, Italy, Slovenia.
- Surface: road-bike marathon on public roads, only partially traffic-controlled (normal traffic rules applied on parts of the course) — not a closed-road race, not gravel.
- Principal climbs: Predil Pass (1,156m) and Vršič Pass (1,611m) — organizer specifically warned about both descents.
- Feed points: Tarvisio km29, Cave del Predil km38, Bovec km60, Trenta km80, Vršič km93, Tarvisio km118.
- Field: 2016 registration cap 1,000; 2016 results PDF shows 234 classified finishers (not necessarily the full field); a 2015 rider report describes ~330 starters.
- Entry fee (2016): tiered €49/€55/€59/€69 depending on registration date, +€20 for late entry.
- Founded: 2014 (not 2010).

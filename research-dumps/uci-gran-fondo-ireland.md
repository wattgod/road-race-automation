# UCI Gran Fondo Ireland — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Status: defunct (one edition only)

UCI's own records show only the June 29–30, 2024 Sligo edition. Ireland is absent
from the complete official 2025 and 2026 UCI Gran Fondo World Series calendars, and
no later registration or results exist. The still-live generic Sligo event page is
historical/stale, not evidence of an active race.
- UCI 2024 event record: https://www.uci.org/competition-details/2024/CPT/73033
- Official 2025-26 calendar (Ireland absent): https://ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/calendar/

## Identity check

Distinct from two other Irish events in this database:
- L'Etape Ireland — Carrick-on-Suir, ASO series, own slug/profile.
- Race Around Ireland — Trim, ultra-distance race-around, dormant since 2019.
No basis for conflating any of the three.

## Verified vitals (last & only actual edition, June 29-30 2024)

Race-week revised figures supersede the earlier promo figures inherited by the
stub (159km/1,176m, 113km/993m — those were pre-event announcements, later cut):
- Gran Fondo: 135 km / 1,050 m
- Medio Fondo: 78 km / 644 m
- Time trial: 25.6 km / 180 m
- Field: UCI reported 1,800 participants from 20 countries across the whole
  event (not necessarily 1,800 road-race starters)
Sources: https://ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/sligo-stages-irelands-first-uci-gran-fondo/ ,
https://ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/gran-fondo-ireland-christie-doubled-while-female-wins-are-shared-between-smith-and-martin/

Benbulben and Gleniff Horseshoe were NOT course features — Benbulben appears only
in the UCI page's tourism blurb, not the road-closure itinerary. No signature
climb/pass was officially identified: rolling, fast bunch-racing terrain over a
repeated ~54km inland loop through Strandhill, Ransboro, Ballysadare, Collooney,
Coolaney, Ballymote, Drumfin, Riverstown, Ballygawley, Carraroe to Doorly Park.
Source: https://www.sligococo.ie/PublicNotices/TemporaryRoadClosures-GranFondo-UPDATE/

## Organizer & history

Announced Feb 6, 2024; inaugurated June 2024 as the first Irish round of the UCI
Gran Fondo World Series. Run by Titan Experience (operator of granfondoireland.ie)
with support from Failte Ireland and Sligo County Council.
Source: https://ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/gran-fondo-ireland-added-to-the-uci-gran-fondo-calendar/

## Credible criticisms (documented, not anecdotal)

Sligo County Council's official meeting minutes record: short-notice route/road-
closure changes, inadequate communication with residents/Gardai/businesses,
unanswered queries to Titan Experience, and Tubbercurry volunteers left stranded
after that community was cut from the route. The council's chief executive said
council intervention prevented the event from being cancelled outright, and
described several promised deliverables as unfulfilled.
Source: https://www.sligococo.ie/YourCouncil/CountyCouncil/Minutes/Minutes2024/MeetingMainBody%2C65993%2Cen.html

Independently corroborated by the UCI's own route change: advertised 159/113 km
courses were cut to 135/78 km shortly before race day for unspecified "unforeseen
circumstances." Rider forum discussion (boards.ie) separately reports an outdated
website, missing email notices, start-area confusion, delayed transponder-deposit
answers — some riders praised the full road closures and on-the-day delivery.

# Research Dump — Gran Fondo Siete Lagos (editorial wave 8, 2026-07-24)

Researched via codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, live web search) for GOLD-register editorial pass.

## Key verified facts

- **Dates**: Real event runs in **November** (Patagonian spring), not the previously listed March. 2025 ran Nov 21-23 (110K on Sun Nov 23). 2026 program: Nov 20-22, with the 110K main race on Nov 22. Confirmed by Villa La Angostura municipal report (angosturainforma.gob.ar), DiarioAndino, and the official schedule (granfondosietelagos.com/cronograma/).
- **Distance**: Official full-course distance is **110 km**, confirmed by the official race page, the 2026 regulations PDF, and the UCI World Series event page. GPS tracks vary 107-109.5km due to measurement/start-finish placement.
- **Elevation**: No single authoritative organizer-published total-elevation-gain figure exists. Third-party GPS tracks range ~1,300-1,800m depending on year/direction. 1,500m retained as a defensible estimate.
- **Field size**: Approximately 3,000 riders/registrations, confirmed independently by: DiarioAndino (July 2025 registration reporting), Villa La Angostura municipal report (event-day, "more than 3,000 cyclists started"), the UCI event page ("around 3,000 participants each year"), and the organizer's own site.
- **UCI Gran Fondo World Series**: Confirmed — this is a **new 2026 UCI Gran Fondo World Series event**, the **first World Series event in Argentina**, and a qualifier for the **2027 UCI Gran Fondo World Championships**. Source: UCI's own event listing (ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/siete-lagos/) and the UCI's 2026 series-expansion announcement (ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/2026-uci-gran-fondo-world-series-expands-with-nine-new-events/). The road 110K and road time trial carry UCI qualification status; MTB/e-bike categories are explicitly classified non-UCI per the 2026 official regulations.
- **Organizer**: Gran Fondo Siete Lagos, historically operated by JAUQUE Eventos (San Martín de los Andes municipal bulletin, 2021); founders Ileana Suffern and Agustín Neiman (Aire Patagonia profile). Official site: granfondosietelagos.com.
- **Course**: Point-to-point along RN40's Ruta de los Siete Lagos, linking Villa La Angostura and San Martín de los Andes through Lanín and Nahuel Huapi national parks, past the Lácar, Machónico, Falkner, Villarino, Espejo, Correntoso, and Nahuel Huapi lakes. Direction alternates by year. Fully paved — Argentina's government tourism authority (argentina.gob.ar) documents more than 96km of the corridor as completely asphalted; no verified gravel sector despite a co-located MTB category on the same road.
- **Climbs**: UCI course description (2026 direction) identifies an opening climb of 15km at 3.5% average to the 1,175m course high point, followed by two short late climbs: 1.9km at 7.2% and 1.2km at 6.5%.
- **History/prestige**: Edition numbering implies a 2012 founding (15th edition in 2026, 14th in 2025) though no inaugural announcement was independently found. 2025 declared of Provincial Tourist Interest by Neuquén. 2024 edition drew Lionel Scaloni, Pablo Aimar, Gabriela Sabatini, and Santiago Lange as participants (La Nación). 2025 road winners: Lucas Gaday and Sofía Martelli.
- **Eligibility**: ACTIVE as of 2026-07-24 — official site shows 15th edition, registration stage 3 open, live schedule, and continued UCI Gran Fondo World Series calendar inclusion. No cancellation/defunct signal found.

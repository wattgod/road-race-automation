# Research Dump: Granfondo Nove Colli

## Quick Facts
- 200km / 124mi, 3,814m / 12,513ft climbing, 9 hills (long route)
- Route options: Long (200km/3,814m), Medium (137km/1,872m), Gravel (107km)
- Start/Finish: Colonia AGIP, Viale Carducci 181, Cesenatico, Emilia-Romagna, Italy
- Fourth Sunday in May (55th edition: May 24, 2026)
- ~12,000 registered riders, ~10,000 starters (10-15% DNS rate typical), 48+ countries
- Founded May 20, 1971 (17 riders at first edition). Oldest gran fondo in the world.
- Entry fee: EUR 120 + EUR 20 online pre-registration service fee (includes timing chip, bib, event jersey by Gobik, feed stations, pasta party)
- Grid start in 4 color-coded waves (Red/White/Blue/Yellow) departing at 4-minute intervals from 6:00 AM. Grid placement by qualifying time from prior editions.
- Meeting time: 5:00 AM. Red Grid departure: 6:00 AM.
- Organized by ASD G.C. Fausto Coppi (cycling club founded October 2, 1966)
- Maximum altitude: 791m (Monte Pugliano)
- Maximum gradient: 18% (Barbotto)
- Medical certificate required (competitive cycling certificate per Italian Ministerial Decree 18/02/82)
- 2026 special: returns to original route with Gorolo climb; also celebrates 60th anniversary of the Fausto Coppi cycling club

## The 9 Hills (Long Route, 200km — 2026 Edition)

The 2026 edition restores the original route with the Gorolo climb, after the 2023-2025 editions used a Sogliano variant. The route runs from sea level at Cesenatico into the Romagna Apennines foothills and back.

1. **Polenta** (km 37) — 8km, 227m gain, 3% avg, 13% max. Near Bertinoro. Gentle opener through vineyard country. The ancient hilltop village of Bertinoro is known as "the balcony of Romagna" for its panoramic views.

2. **Pieve di Rivoschio** (km 65) — 8km, 307m gain, 4.7% avg, 9% max. Begins around Piandispino. Sustained medium-gradient climb through wooded Apennine foothills. Road surface noted as rough in some sections.

3. **Ciola** (km 77) — 6km, 313m gain, 5.5% avg, 11% max. Begins after Linaro, near Mercato Saraceno. Harsh but regular gradient. Legs start to feel the accumulated climbing.

4. **Barbotto** (km 91) — 5.5km, 372m gain, 6.9% avg, 18% max. THE signature climb of the Nove Colli. Starts with opening 300m at 9-10%, then eases briefly before a devastating final kilometer with ramps of 14-18%. Huge crowd atmosphere with an announcer at the summit calling out every rider's name. Decisive selection point. The Barbotto is in the municipality of Mercato Saraceno. MyCols categorizes it as Cat 2.

5. **Montetiffi** (km 102) — 3km, ~175m gain, 5.8% avg, 16% max. Located in the municipality of Sogliano. Short but vicious. Views of the ancient Benedictine Abbey perched atop the hill. Punchy gradients catch fatigued riders.

6. **Perticara** (km 111) — 9km, ~370m gain, 4.1% avg, 12% max. Near Novafeltria. Long, grinding climb with irregular gradient. Historic sulphur mining village at the summit.

7. **Monte Pugliano** (km 130) — 9km, 511m gain, 5.6% avg, 12% max. Greatest elevation gain of all nine hills. The highest point on the course at 791m. MyCols categorizes it as Cat 2. Relentless, consistent gradient. This is the queen climb of the Nove Colli in terms of pure vertical.

8. **Passo delle Siepi** (km 144) — 4km, ~230m gain, 5.8% avg, 7% max. Begins after Secchiano. The most "friendly" climb — no savage ramps, just honest sustained effort. A brief respite before the final sting.

9. **Gorolo** (km 162) — 4km, ~240m gain, 6% avg, 17% max. The last effort of the Nove Colli is also one of the hardest. Restored for 2026 after years of route variations. Opens immediately at about 16% for the first kilometer, then eases mid-climb before a brutal finale touching 17-20% in the final 200 meters. ClimbFinder data for San Giovanni in Galilea via Gorolo: 5.4km, 353m gain, 6.5% avg, 13.5% steepest 100m. Seven hairpin turns.

**Note on route variation**: The route has changed over the years. The 2023-2025 editions used a Sogliano variant as the 9th climb instead of Gorolo (due to road damage from severe weather events in Romagna in 2023). For 2026, the Province of Forli-Cesena and local administrations repaired the roads, enabling the return to the original Gorolo route. Some historical sources cite slightly different distances and elevation totals depending on the edition (189-205km, 3,265-3,880m climbing).

## Medium Route (137km)
- 137km, 1,872m elevation gain, max height 507m, max slope 18%
- Covers the first four climbs: Polenta, Pieve di Rivoschio, Ciola, and Barbotto
- After Barbotto, the medium route diverges and returns directly to Cesenatico
- Same start, same finish, same atmosphere, same Barbotto

## Gravel Route (107km)
- 107km, ~1,780m elevation gain, max height 436m (Montebello)
- Runs on dirt roads, trails, and secondary paved roads
- Added in 2024 after devastating 2023 floods in Romagna
- Starts and finishes at Colonia AGIP with the road cyclists
- Held on the Saturday (May 23, 2026 for the gravel event — NOTE: the PDF regulations state May 24 for gravel, same day as the granfondo)

## Course Characteristics
- Road surface: Predominantly well-paved Italian provincial roads. Some sections noted as rough (particularly on the Pieve di Rivoschio descent). Road quality was a concern post-2023 floods but has been substantially repaired.
- Roads fully closed to motorized traffic for both long and medium routes
- 89km of ascent (including slight plateaus), 77km of downhill, 39km of flat (long route)
- First 28km from Cesenatico is flat along the Adriatic coast at very high average speed (40+ km/h), echelons and paceline riding before the hills begin
- Fast, technical descents between climbs, particularly after Barbotto and Pieve di Rivoschio (tight bends, steep gradients up to 14%)
- The climbs are not alpine — max altitude is 791m — but the relentless succession of 9 hills over 200km with 3,814m of climbing creates enormous cumulative fatigue

## Feed Zones
- Nine food stations along the route (one near each climb)
- Traditional Romagna products served: fresh pasta, pies, pizza, croissants, tarts, salted biscuits, bananas, oranges, grapefruits, apples, lemons, coffee, honey, Coca-Cola, fruit juice
- Gluten-free options available at all stations
- 46th edition data: 7,080 pies, 5,064 juices, 37.3kg coffee, 72kg sugar, 15kg honey, 2,964 bottles of Coke, 200kg apple pie, 1,400kg bananas served across all stations
- Post-race pasta party by Mare (confirmed for 2025, expected for 2026)
- Enervit is the official nutrition sponsor

## Grid / Start Format (2026 Regulations)
- **Red Grid** (bibs 1-1300): Guest/Merit riders. Long distance finishing under 7:20:00 (M) / 7:30:00 (W) in 2024/2025. Top 90 men + 10 women from Nove Colli ranking. Loyalists (10+ editions).
- **White Grid** (bibs 1301-2650): Next merit tier
- **Blue Grid** (bibs 2651-4550): Next tier
- **Yellow Grid** (bibs 4551-5550): UCI membership card holders and remaining registrants
- Additional grids beyond Yellow for remaining participants
- Grids depart at 4-minute intervals in real time starting at 6:00 AM
- "Bike Hotels Formula" grants preferred grid placement through partner hotels

## Logistics
- **Nearest Airports**:
  - Rimini Federico Fellini Airport (RMI) — 22km, ~35 min drive. Ryanair and seasonal European routes. New 2026 routes to Breslavia, Manchester, Cologne, Catania.
  - Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport (BLQ) — ~130km, 1hr 15min drive. Major international hub, best for long-haul.
  - Forli Airport (FRL) — ~30km, ~30 min drive. Limited Ryanair service (new 2026 routes to London and Cagliari).
  - Venice Marco Polo Airport (VCE) — ~250km, ~2.5hr drive. Alternative international hub.
- **Lodging**: Cesenatico is an Adriatic beach resort town with extensive hotel capacity. "Bike Hotels" are specifically affiliated with the event and offer race registration + accommodation packages. Hotels from EUR 80/night (B&B) to EUR 285/night (full board with race packages). Book early — the event transforms the entire Romagna coast.
- **Race-day transport**: Start/finish at Colonia AGIP waterfront, Viale Carducci 181. Parking available. Town is compact and walkable. Cesenatico train station connects to Bologna/Rimini rail lines.
- **Race kit collection**: Available in the days before the race at the event village. Online form required.
- **Bike rental**: Available through the official Nove Colli website ("Rent a Bike!")
- **Cutoff times**: Grid-based qualification times suggest an expected finishing range. Long distance cutoff appears to be approximately 10-11 hours based on grid qualification tiers (fastest merit tier: sub-7:20 for men).

## History & Culture

### Origin Story
- The Fausto Coppi Cycling Club was founded October 2, 1966 by eight friends who loved cycling in Cesenatico.
- The first Granfondo Nove Colli was held on May 20, 1971, starting at 5:00 AM from Bar del Corso on the Porto Canale Leonardesco (Leonardo da Vinci's canal port). Just 17 riders participated.
- The name "Nove Colli" (Nine Hills) describes the route's nine climbs through the Romagna Apennine foothills.

### Growth Milestones
- **1971**: First edition, 17 riders
- **1996**: First time the event exceeded 10,000 participants (in the "manual era" before electronic timing)
- **2010**: 40th anniversary. Participation capped at 12,000. The Giro d'Italia celebrated it by including the Nove Colli route in its final stage portion.
- **2015**: 12,000 online registration slots sold out in 4 minutes
- **2020**: Giro d'Italia Stage 12 (Cesenatico-Cesenatico) was held on the Nove Colli route as a tribute to the 50th anniversary and to Marco Pantani (won by Jhonatan Narvaez of INEOS Grenadiers)
- **2021**: 50th edition (delayed from 2020 due to COVID)
- **2024**: Tour de France Stage 2 started from Cesenatico, marking the Grand Depart from Italy. Nove Colli created a special yellow jersey to honor the occasion. New gravel route added.
- **2025**: 54th edition. Alejandro Valverde rode as a guest. Manuel Senni won the long route.
- **2026**: 55th edition, returns to original route with Gorolo climb. Also celebrates 60 years of the Fausto Coppi cycling club.

### Marco Pantani Connection
- Marco Pantani was born January 13, 1970 in Cesena and grew up in Cesenatico. The town is inextricably linked to "Il Pirata."
- The Pantani museum (Spazio Pantani) is located on Cesenatico's canal port, near the Nove Colli start.
- Andrea Agostini (now COO of UAE Team Emirates) and Gian Paolo Mondini (now Specialized team liaison) were childhood friends and amateur teammates of Pantani in Cesenatico.
- "You cannot say Cesenatico without saying Marco Pantani" — Andrea Agostini (Rouleur)
- Pantani's training roads in the Romagna hills overlap significantly with the Nove Colli route. Monte Carpegna, his most famous training climb, is near the course.
- The Giro d'Italia Stage 12 in 2020 was specifically routed through Cesenatico on the Nove Colli course as a Pantani tribute.

### Notable Champions
- **Manuel Senni**: Won the 2024 (53rd) edition, described as a "repeat victory"
- **Giulia Portaluri**: First woman to complete the 200km route (2024)
- The event also features a "Prestige" ranking system across multiple editions

### Cultural Significance
- Widely regarded as "the queen of granfondos" and "Italy's most popular gran fondo"
- CyclingHero calls it "the oldest cycling event in the world" (of the gran fondo format)
- The event earned Legambiente's "Eco Certified Event" certification (2025)
- The 2026 jersey design is described as "Inspiration Born from Genius"
- Charity initiative: #NoveColli4Children — a Dream Team rides for solidarity

## Climate (Late May)
- **Temperature**: High 22-23C (71-73F), Low 13C (55F), Average 17-18C (63-65F)
- **Precipitation**: 56-91mm for the month of May. Average ~7 rain days in May.
- **Humidity**: ~65%
- **Wind**: 11 mph average. Adriatic coastal influence means sea breezes. The flat opening 28km can feature headwinds or crosswinds.
- **Daylight**: ~14.7 hours. Sunrise ~5:44 AM, Sunset ~8:28 PM. Ample daylight for the entire race (starts 6:00 AM).
- **Conditions summary**: Late May in Romagna is generally warm and pleasant — one of the most favorable weather windows on the European gran fondo calendar. Heat can be a factor on the climbs in the inland valleys (temperatures can be higher away from the coast). Rain is possible but less frequent than in northern Alpine events. The early morning start (5:00 AM gathering, 6:00 AM start) means the opening hour can be cool, especially along the coast.
- **Water temperature**: 19C (66F) — relevant for riders considering a post-race Adriatic swim.

## Community & Culture

### Cesenatico
- Historic fishing port on the Adriatic coast of Emilia-Romagna, in the Province of Forli-Cesena
- The Porto Canale Leonardesco (canal port) was designed by Leonardo da Vinci in 1502
- Traditional colorful fishing boats (trabaccoli and bragozzi) line the canal — one of the most photographed scenes in Romagna
- Spazio Pantani museum on the canal port honors the town's greatest sporting hero
- The town transforms into a cycling festival for the entire Nove Colli weekend (Friday-Sunday)
- Cesenatico is a popular Italian summer beach resort, meaning excellent hotel and restaurant infrastructure

### Romagna Food Culture
- Piadina romagnola: the iconic local flatbread, served at every trattoria and street corner
- Sangiovese di Romagna: the local red wine, the everyday table wine of the region
- Fresh pasta traditions: tagliatelle, cappelletti, strozzapreti, passatelli
- The post-race pasta party is a major event — pasta by Mare catering, prosecco, local specialties
- Nine food stations on the course serve traditional Romagna products, making it a culinary tour as much as a cycling event
- Nearby Forlimpopoli is the birthplace of Pellegrino Artusi, father of modern Italian cuisine

### International Reach
- 48+ countries represented (2023 data: 9,200 riders from 48 countries)
- Website available in Italian, English, and German
- Official media in multiple languages
- Tour operator packages available from La Fuga (UK), CyclingHero, High Cadence Cycling Tours, Sports Tours International, and others
- EUR 1,399-4,399+ for guided tour packages (3-8 nights)

### Event Weekend Schedule (Typical)
- Friday: Race village opens, expo area, race kit collection
- Saturday: Mini Nove Colli (5km ride for children ages 7-14), Gravel event, pasta party setup
- Sunday: Gran Fondo (long + medium routes), finish line celebrations, pasta party, awards
- The event village is located in the heart of Cesenatico near the canal port

### Media Coverage
- Official YouTube channel, Facebook, Instagram, X (Twitter) presence
- Covered by GranFondoGuide, Cycling Weekly, Rouleur, DMT Cycling, Enervit
- Featured in Strava's "Gran Fondo Focus: Bucket List Events"
- Named in GranFondoGuide's "Top 10 European Gran Fondos" awards
- The Tour de France (2024) and Giro d'Italia (2020) both used Cesenatico/Nove Colli routes, bringing massive broadcast exposure

## Nearby Attractions
- Ravenna (30km): UNESCO Byzantine mosaics, Dante's tomb
- Rimini (22km): Roman arch, Tempio Malatestiano, Federico Fellini museum
- San Marino (50km): Independent republic, medieval fortress
- Santarcangelo di Romagna: charming hilltop town
- Mirabilandia: major Italian theme park
- The entire Romagna Riviera: extensive beach infrastructure

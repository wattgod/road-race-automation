## 2. Course and entry facts

| Fact | Best-supported finding |
|---|---|
| Venue | Start/finish at **Mike Weir Park, 2354 Lakeshore Road, Bright’s Grove/Sarnia**, on Lake Huron. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo), [official FAQ](https://www.ourbco.ca/the-bluewater-international-granfondo-faqs). |
| 2026 routes | **30 km, 50 km, 100 km and approximately 161 km/100 mi.** The organizer says 161 km; Race Roster confusingly labels it “160K” but describes it as 162 km. Use **161 km / 100 mi**, with an internal discrepancy note. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo), [Race Roster](https://raceroster.com/events/2026/108967/bluewater-international-gran-fondo-2026). |
| Elevation | No reliable current numeric ascent is published in the organizer’s text. It classifies the 30/50 km routes as **flat** and the 100/161 km routes as **rolling**. The official long-route map is [RideWithGPS route 39792548](https://ridewithgps.com/routes/39792548). **Do not retain 853 m/2,800 ft as confirmed:** it appears attached to the former 150 km route and has not been verified for the new anniversary course. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo). |
| Route character | Quiet rural roads, Lake Huron shoreline, fruit orchards and the rolling Arkona area. A detailed rider report describes the prior 150 km course running east on flat roads, north toward the Parkhill/Arkona farmlands—the hilliest portion—and west back to Sarnia; it also identifies wind as a meaningful course factor. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo), [Canadian Cycling route report](https://nusarnia.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/CCMv15i2_lambton_county.pdf). |
| Climbs | No named mountain or categorized climb is documented. The difficulty consists of short rolling Arkona-area hills, not a sustained alpine climb. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo), [Canadian Cycling route report](https://nusarnia.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/CCMv15i2_lambton_county.pdf). |
| Surface | **Road/paved, not mixed-surface or gravel.** Organizer rules prefer road bikes and describe riding on open public roads; the organization separately stages the explicitly gravel **BIG Gravel Grind**. “Fully paved” is reasonable, although the organizer does not publish a surface-percentage audit. [Official rules](https://www.ourbco.ca/the-bluewater-international-granfondo-rules), [Bluewater Cycling Organization events](https://www.ourbco.ca/). |
| Timing | Strictly speaking, the organizer says it **is not a race**. The 50, 100 and long routes use timing chips for selected sections only; cumulative segment time determines awards, and no start-to-finish result is issued. The 30 km family ride has no timed section and is explicitly “not to be raced.” [Official FAQ](https://www.ourbco.ca/the-bluewater-international-granfondo-faqs), [official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo). |
| Starts | Staggered by distance: long route 7:00, 100 km 7:45, 50 km 8:30 and 30 km 9:00. The long/100/50/30 routes respectively have 4/3/1/0 timed sections. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo). |
| Field | **1,000-rider safety cap.** It was 85% full by May 22 and all route categories are now marked sold out. [Sarnia News Today](https://sarnianewstoday.ca/sarnia/news/2026/05/26/granfondo-marks-10-years), [Race Roster](https://raceroster.com/events/2026/108967/bluewater-international-gran-fondo-2026). |
| Entry/support | Paid individual registration by chosen distance; supported with marked routes, fuel stops, bike/SAG support, event jersey, finisher medal, finish meal and entertainment. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo), [Race Roster](https://raceroster.com/events/2026/108967/bluewater-international-gran-fondo-2026). |
| Rider eligibility | Generally **15+**; younger riders may enter the accompanied family category, whose stated minimum child age is ten. E-bikes are permitted but do not receive competitive timing. [Official event page](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo), [official rules](https://www.ourbco.ca/the-bluewater-international-granfondo-rules). |

## 3. History and claims audit

- **Founded/inaugural edition: 2016, not 2017.** The inaugural event sold out at 500 riders; the organizer’s results archive also contains 2016 results. [Inaugural-event report](https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/1604/500-riders-complete-inaugural-bluewater-gran-fondo), [organizer results archive](https://www.ourbco.ca/bluewater-international-granfondo-results).
- The in-person event ran from 2016–2019, was cancelled in 2020, and became virtual in 2021 before returning in person. [Canadian Cycling history](https://cyclingmagazine.ca/sections/ridesevents/gran-fondo-documentary-the-b-i-g-cycle-of-life-reviewed/), [2020 cancellation report](https://cknewstoday.ca/chatham/news/2020/5/9/bluewater-granfondo-cancelled-due-covid), [2021 virtual-event report](https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6315/virtual-bluewater-international-granfondo-raises-%2440k-for-palliative-care).
- The palliative-care framing is correct but should name the beneficiaries: **Bluewater Palliative Care Retreat, Bluewater Health’s palliative-care program and St. Joseph’s Hospice**. Organizers reported more than $500,000 returned to the community by May 2026. [Race Roster beneficiaries](https://raceroster.com/events/2026/108967/bluewater-international-gran-fondo-2026), [Sarnia News Today](https://sarnianewstoday.ca/sarnia/news/2026/05/26/granfondo-marks-10-years).
- Do not publish an unqualified “Ontario’s/Canada’s #1.” Gran Fondo Guide placed it **#14 in North America, #2 in Canada and #1 in Ontario in 2019**; its current 2026 editorial list places it **#4 in North America**. Attribute the outlet and year because this is an editorial award, not an official championship ranking. [2019 ranking report](https://cknxnewstoday.ca/sarnia/news/2019/3/27/bluewater-granfondo-roll), [Gran Fondo Guide 2026 ranking](https://www.granfondoguide.com/Awards/Top10NorthAmericanGranFondos2026).

## Format verdict

This is an honest **mass-participation, fully supported road gran fondo/sportive**. It is not an ultra, travel tour, product category, MTB/gravel event, or ongoing virtual/Strava challenge. However, calling it a conventional “road race” would be misleading: there is no overall start-to-finish time, only selected competitive segments, and the family route is expressly non-racing. [Official FAQ](https://www.ourbco.ca/the-bluewater-international-granfondo-faqs).

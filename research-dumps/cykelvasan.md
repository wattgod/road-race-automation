# Cykelvasan — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Taxonomy flag (highest-priority finding)
This is emphatically an MTB/gravel-forest-road event, not a paved-road fondo — the
current `gran_fondo` road classification is materially misleading. 2026 course mix:
32 km option is 28.9 km forest road/gravel + 3.1 km asphalt; 45 km is 43.3 km
forest/gravel + 1.7 km asphalt; the 92 km main race is roughly 89 km off-road + 3 km
asphalt (~97% unpaved), including forest/gravel roads, classic ski trail, and
constructed boardwalks over marshland (more than 7 km moved from roads onto Vasaloppet
trail for 2026). Flagging for a human catalog call — never changed the discipline enum
directly.

## History
Vasaloppet tested the bike route in 2008, launched Cykelvasan in 2009 (inspired partly
by Norway's Birkebeinerrittet), immediately drawing ~2,000 riders. Organized by the
nonprofit Vasaloppsföreningen Sälen–Mora, owned by IFK Mora and Sälens IF.

## Criticism
Rider accounts describe congestion/limited passing around start and finish. Fast
gravel-bike riders report rough sections requiring more obstacle avoidance than
full-suspension MTB, with elevated puncture/crash risk. Heavy rain can turn the course
muddy or flood sections, forcing last-minute reroutes.

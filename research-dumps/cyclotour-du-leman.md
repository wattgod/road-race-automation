# Research Dump: Cyclotour du Léman

Research date: 2026-07-24 (codex gpt-5.6-sol web-search session, ed4 editorial batch)

## Quick Facts
- 23rd edition: Sunday, October 18, 2026 — confirmed on official site and 2026 regulations
- Moved from its traditional spring (May/June) slot to autumn because the G7 summit in Évian (June 15-17, 2026 final program; June 14-16 as first announced) imposed security/traffic restrictions around Lake Geneva
- Organizer/rights-holder: Human Sports Management AG (HSM)
- Official site: cyclotour.ch
- Slogan for 2026: "NO RACE – JUST PACE"
- 2026 regulations state: "It is not a race; no timing or ranking is carried out." (reglement_fr_26_v05.pdf)
- Nuance: in 2024 participant times were recorded and displayed alphabetically, expressly without ranking; by 2026 the published policy is stronger — no timing and no ranking at all
- Described by its organizer/rights-holder as "Switzerland's largest non-competitive cycling event" (German original: "gilt als" — "is considered") in the official 2026 HSM press release (080126_MM-Cyclotour_GER_final.pdf). This is an organizer marketing claim, not an independently audited ranking. Safest wording: "billed by its organizer as the largest non-competitive cycling event in Switzerland."

## Vitals reconciliation
- File previously listed 175.4 km / 890 m as a single-route figure. Official current full-route figure is 176 km / 890 m. The 175.4 is plausibly a GPX/platform trace of the nominally 176 km route (0.34% difference); 890 m matches exactly. Database should present 176 km / 890 m as the official full-circuit figure and note it is ONE of three route options.
- No official course record exists — the event is non-timed and non-ranked; "records" in official material are participation totals, not finishing times.

## Field size
- 2025: more than 4,000 entries; 3,600 starters (official post-event report, cyclotour.ch/news/thank-you-6839)
- 2024: 4,000 registered / sold out; 3,518 starters (official release); local press (Le Dauphiné Libéré) gave 4,068 pre-event registrations
- 2023: sold out at 3,800 registrations (official press release)
- "Around 4,000 participants" is registration-side shorthand; actual start-line fields recently 3,500-3,600

## Sol adversarial review additions (2026-07-24, second codex pass)
- OPEN-ROAD EVENT: the 2026 regulations and official FAQ (cyclotour.ch/en/qna) state the route remains open to normal traffic — riders must obey traffic lights, use cycle lanes where available, ride max two abreast, and accept neutralizations/exclusion for unsafe riding. Any "closed-road" description is WRONG; "marshalled open roads" is accurate. The event's value is organized pelotons, marshals, aid, route support, and boat transfers — not exclusive road access.
- Cutoff correction: the regulations say the Lausanne finish CLOSES AT 16:00 (4 pm), roughly a 10-hour window from the morning start with intermediate cutoffs — the previous "16 hours" vitals entry was a misreading.
- Edition count: 22 completed editions; the 23rd is scheduled for 2026.
- Date-change causation wording: organizer cites "anticipated security and traffic restrictions" from the G7 summit creating logistical conflicts — not a literal region shutdown.
- Timing history: times were recorded and published alphabetically in 2023-2024; timing ended in 2025; 2026 policy is no timing and no ranking.
- The origin of the old 175.4 km figure is unverified (no GPX source located) — describe it as unsourced, not as a GPS-trace variant.
- Rights-holder nuance: Human Sports Management is the rights holder; Association Cyclotour du Léman conducts the event. "Organizer" is acceptable shorthand; "official event materials describe it as..." is the exact attribution for the 'largest' claim.
- 2025 post-event report also available at https://cyclotour.ch/news/merci-1832 (FR variant of the thank-you page).

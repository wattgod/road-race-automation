# GranFondo Mont Sainte-Anne — Research Dump (Editorial Wave 8, 2026-07-24)

Research conducted via codex gpt-5.6-sol foreground web search, checked 2026-07-24.

## 1. Current eligibility — defunct/dormant, not active

No registration, results, organizer announcement, or confirmed edition found for 2020-2026. Last
planned event was July 19, 2020; Quebec's government ordered summer public sporting events cancelled
through August 31 that year, which covered it. The event never subsequently reappeared. Former domain
granfondomsa.com was newly re-registered in July 2025, another sign the original operation had lapsed.
A current Château Mont-Sainte-Anne page mentioning its "parcours" is only a legacy recreational-route
reference, not a live event.

## 2. Years and stopping point

Best-supported chronology: annual run 2013-2019. 2013 inaugural (June 22-23); 2014 second edition
(July 13); then July 19 2015, July 3 2016, July 16 2017, July 15 2018, July 14 2019. Eighth season
planned July 19, 2020 but effectively cancelled by Quebec's COVID event shutdown. No formal farewell or
relaunch explanation found — just six years of absence and lapsed organizer domains.

## 3. Course character

All-road Côte-de-Beaupré ride, largely following Avenue Royale/Route de la Nouvelle-France near the St.
Lawrence before repeatedly turning inland into the Laurentian foothills. Documented climbs: L'Ange-Gardien,
Château-Richer, Saint-Ferréol-les-Neiges, plus rang Saint-Achillée, Saint-Léon, Saint-Elzéar. The 160 km
Alto advertised ~2,550 m climbing with ramps to 17%; 2014 offered 46, 80, 121 and 161 km routes.
130 km/1,800 m is geographically plausible but was NOT a documented standard event distance — nearest
historical medium routes were 115-121 km, long route was 160-161 km. Treat 130.4/1,800 as unverified.
Source: https://laflammerouge.com/category/cyclosport/page/10/,
https://cyclingmagazine.ca/sections/ridesevents/gran-fondo-mont-sainte-anne-debuts-in-june/

## 4. Organizer and stature

Founder Sébastien Roy modeled the event on Gran Fondo New York; Luc Beaudet was event director by 2014.
Operated under the "Gran Fondo Éco" umbrella. Mélanie Turgeon (Olympic alpine skier) was 2013 ambassador.
Solid regional fondo, not internationally prestigious.

## 5. Field size

Strongest citable figure: ~400 riders in 2014. Inaugural preview advertised only 250 places per
course (~750 total capacity); press material merely hoped for a few thousand. The claimed "2,000
riders/600 finishers" could not be independently verified — do not convert the promotional target into
an actual field-size claim.
Source: metroquebec.com 2014 participation report; La Flamme Rouge 2013 capacity/course preview.

## Course — UNRESOLVED CONTRADICTION (flag for human review)

Two sources disagree and were not reconciled during research:

1. Battistrada's 2026 technical route listing places the start at Panamá Pacífico, heading west
   into Panamá Oeste (pineapple country), 134km/1,869m.
2. The retained firsthand video ("Is Panama the BEST Kept Secret for Cycling Enthusiasts?",
   luis scott, Aug 2025) is a detailed on-the-ground account of THIS event (names Rodolfo Jurado
   by name as organizer, matches the GFCC multi-city format) and describes a completely
   different course: start at Amador Causeway (El Paso de las Banderas), crossing the Panama
   Canal via the Puente de las Américas (formerly Thatcher Ferry Bridge, ~5-6% grade), heading
   west toward Arraiján/La Chorrera, finishing through Pedro Miguel and Paraíso near the canal —
   i.e., in and around Panama City/the Canal Zone, NOT "not Panama City proper" as previously
   asserted here.

Do not present either account as settled. Possible explanations: the course changed between the
2025 and 2026 editions; the Battistrada listing describes a different GFCC city or a stale/wrong
route; or the "Panamá Pacífico/pineapple country" framing is simply wrong. No named signature
climb was found in either source, but the video does name real landmarks: Puente de las
Américas and Pedro Miguel.

Video also sources: host hotel was Hotel El Panama (villa room ~$108/night, breakfast
included); airport-to-hotel private taxi ~$35, ~30 min via the "El Corridor" highway; first
rest stop at ~20 miles, well-stocked; GFCC operates the same fondo format in multiple cities
(Panama City, Boquete, Puerto Rico, Dominican Republic, Miami) with pace-leader groups and full
sag support. The creator has disclosed promotional ties (discount code, self-identified GFCC
Miami "event ambassador") — this is a firsthand account, not independent rider criticism.

# Research Dump: London Edinburgh London (LEL)

## Quick Facts
- Organizer: Audax UK's flagship randonnée. Official URL: https://londonedinburghlondon.com/
- Format: self-supported, non-competitive randonnée/brevet. Riders navigate a prescribed (fixed) route and visit timed controls; personal support vehicles are prohibited, but staffed controls provide food, beds, showers, and limited mechanical/medical help. No placings or prizes — official finishers are listed alphabetically, not ranked.
- Cadence: runs once every four years (normal sequence 2017, 2021, 2025, 2029). The 2021 edition was postponed by COVID-19 to 7-12 August 2022. The 2025 edition started but was abandoned mid-event due to Storm Floris, with no official finishers recorded.
- Therefore: most recently ATTEMPTED edition = 2025 (abandoned); most recently COMPLETED/validated edition = 2022; NEXT scheduled edition = 2029.
- 2029 edition: confirmed for Sunday, August 5, 2029, primarily starting from Writtle University College, Lordship Road, Writtle, Chelmsford CM1 3RR, with a secondary central-London start at Guildhall Yard. Route will not be finalized until March 2029.
- 2022 edition (last completed): distance 1,540 km, elevation 14,080 m (~957 mi / ~46,194 ft), per Audax UK's own indexed event record. Nominally classified as a "1,500 km" event. Start location was Loughton/Debden, Essex (NOT Writtle/Chelmsford — that's the 2029 start location only). Rider GPS totals for 2022 varied roughly 14,500-15,100 m due to differing elevation algorithms and a road-closure diversion some riders took; 10,973 m / 36,000 m is NOT an organizer-published 2022 figure and appears to have been a prior data error in this profile.
- 2022 time limit: standard start group had 128 hours 20 minutes; an early "fast" group start accepted a 100-hour limit. (The commonly quoted "116h40" figure belongs to a different, 1,400 km course at a 12 km/h minimum pace — not LEL's actual 2022 allowance.)
- 2022 participation: 1,500 starters, 890 completions per Audax UK's January 2023 annual report — a 59% completion rate (41% DNF/out-of-time). For comparison: 2013 had 998 starters/804 finishers; 2017 recorded 831 validations.
- 2022 route: extended to Dunfermline, crossed the Forth Road Bridge, passed through central Edinburgh; used 20 control visits across 13 locations including St Ives, Boston, Louth, Hessle, Malton, Barnard Castle, Brampton, Moffat, Dunfermline, Innerleithen, Eskdalemuir, and Great Easton.
- Country/location data-entry bug found in prior version of this profile: vitals.country was listed as "USA" despite the event being entirely within England/Scotland, UK. Corrected to "United Kingdom."
- Prior profile also listed distance/elevation (1,528.9 km / 10,973 m) that did not match any sourced LEL edition figure closely enough to identify its origin; replaced with 2022's sourced organizer figures (1,540 km / 14,080 m) as the best-evidenced representative vitals, with editorial notes explaining the event has no fixed distance across editions and did not run in 2026.

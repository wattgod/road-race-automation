## Vitals
375 miles / 604 km over three days, clockwise circumnavigation starting and
finishing in San Juan. Route: San Juan → east through Luquillo/Fajardo →
south to Ponce → southwest through Guánica, La Parguera, Cabo Rojo to
Mayagüez/Rincón → west/north coast through Aguadilla, Arecibo, Manatí,
Dorado → back to Old San Juan.
https://vueltapuertorico.com/?page_id=149 (Day 1), ?page_id=150 (Day 2),
Day 3 route PDF: https://vueltapuertorico.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/Day_3-_Vuelta_Puerto_Rico_Official_Route.pdf

## Format
Fully supported non-racing endurance tour, NOT a competitive race — three
pace-based pelotons (A ~21mph/25-30mph hammerheads, B ~18mph, C ~16mph).
Riders must sustain the group average for the full event. 2026 entry
$795 (early) rising to $1,195, excluding taxes/ACTIVE fees.
https://vueltapuertorico.com/ (official format), pricing page above.

## History
Founded 2006 by William Medina as "La Vuelta de los Nueve Faros de Puerto
Rico," 89 riders in the inaugural three-day edition. Organizer of record:
Puerto Rico 360, Inc.
https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a20007927/bicycling-travel/
Official travel guide: https://vueltapuertorico.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/2023-Travel-Guide.pdf

## Credible criticism
Older rider reports (not proof of current conditions) describe severe
potholes and crashes, late luggage, too few portable toilets, and
inadequate advance disclosure of a roughly 17% climb. Bicycling.com also
reported a delayed lunch truck and hazardous traffic exposure for riders
dropped behind the police escort. Current terms are unusually strict:
non-transferable, non-refundable, non-deferrable without optional ACTIVE
Refund coverage.
Rider report: https://tntvc.wordpress.com/la-vuelta-puerto-rico/
Bicycling report: https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a20007927/bicycling-travel/
Refund terms: https://vueltapuertorico.com/signup/

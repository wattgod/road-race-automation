# Perth Hills Gran Fondo — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Date conflict resolved (main task)
File showed "2026: March 29"; a Kalamunda council listing showed May 24. Resolved:
the 2026 edition moved to Sunday, May 24 due to Tropical Cyclone Narelle (WA,
March 23-28, confirmed by Bureau of Meteorology). The Race Roster registration page
header is stuck on the stale March 29 date even though the event copy on the same
page shows May 24 — an organizer data-hygiene issue, not a second event or fabrication.

## Status
Active. Third edition of the current Gooseberry Hill format (2024-2026), descended
from the 2014 City of Armadale Gran Fondo (organizer PIET's claimed "WA's first
gran fondo").

## Vitals
- Start/finish: Gooseberry Hill Hall, 42 Ledger Road, Gooseberry Hill
- Distances: Piccolo ~45-50km, Medio ~85-90km, Gran ~135-140km (all labeled
  approximate/TBC by organizer)
- Elevation: NO defensible current figure. The widely-repeated 2,369m stat belongs
  to the 2024 136km course, which started in Karragullen — a different venue from
  the current Gooseberry Hill start. Left null in profile pending real 2026 GPX data.
- Field: council planning docs estimate ~100-150 riders targeted, ~200 total attendees
  (a planning estimate, not a verified finisher count)

## Course
Hilly, fully paved, open roads under normal WA traffic law — Gooseberry Hill,
Kalamunda, Boya, Darlington, Mundaring, Carmel, Pickering Brook. Signature sections:
Zig Zag Scenic Drive/Kalamunda climb, Patterson Road near Perth Observatory, and
Heritage Drive (Gran course) — the event's own billed hallmark climbing sector.

## Organizer/history
Run by Perth Integrated Events Team (PIET). Descends from the City of Armadale
Gran Fondo (2014); returned as Perth Hills Gran Fondo in 2024 after a pandemic
hiatus.

## Criticisms
No substantive independent rider criticism found. Organizer-side rough edges:
open traffic, approximate routes with GPX released only close to race day, no
transfers/ordinary refunds, an "act of God" no-refund policy that applied to the
cyclone postponement, and the stale March 29 registration header.

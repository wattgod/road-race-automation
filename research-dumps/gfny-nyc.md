# Research Dump: GFNY NYC (Gran Fondo New York)

## Quick Facts
- 137km / 85mi, 1,942m / 6,371ft climbing
- Start/Finish: George Washington Bridge (NJ side) / Fort Lee, NJ
- Third Sunday in May (14th edition: May 17, 2026)
- ~4,000-6,000 riders from 90+ countries
- Founded 2011 by Uli and Lidia Fluhme
- Entry fee ~$350-400 (includes mandatory GFNY jersey made in Italy, chip timing, finisher medal)
- Mass start with corral system; GP NYC pro race (UCI 1.2) starts 10 min before
- Title sponsor: Michelob ULTRA
- "GFNY World Championship" — flagship of 30+ global GFNY events

## Course Profile (Post-2023, Bear Mountain REMOVED)
1. **Alpine Climb** — 1.6km, 106m gain, 6.4% avg, 10.2% max
2. **Gate Hill (Colle Andrea Pinarello)** — 3.5km, 186m gain, 5.2% avg, 8.9% max
3. **Cheesecote Mountain** — 1.5km, 79m gain, 5.2% avg, 9.6% max (18% initial ramp)
4. **Mott Farm Road** — undulating, no specific gradient data
- Bear Mountain (7.4km, 382m, Cat 2) was removed in 2023 — controversial, reduced course character significantly.
- Road surface: fully paved, well-maintained NJ/NY state roads (Route 9W, Palisades Interstate Park)
- Traffic: extensive police presence (~150 officers) but NOT full road closure. Partial closures on 9W northbound, GWB lower level closed all day.

## Logistics
- Airports: LGA, JFK, EWR — all within 30-60min of Fort Lee
- Hotels: Official partners $195-365/night in Fort Lee, Edgewater, Englewood, Manhattan
- Start area opens 5:00 AM. Flights must land by 1:00 PM Saturday for packet pickup.
- Course closes at 3:00 PM (~7h50m window from 7:10 AM start)

## History
- Founded 2011. Founders Uli (German lawyer in NYC) and Lidia Fluhme (15x Ironman finisher) quit jobs, invested $200K personal savings.
- Inspired by Italian gran fondo culture — NYC had no European-style mass-start road race.
- Key challenge: securing GWB closure from Port Authority (initially wanted $50-100K).
- Required permits from 15 municipalities, 3 parks, and bridge authorities.
- 1,900 riders year 1 → 5,000 year 2 → global brand with 30+ events across 15+ countries.
- 2012: First amateur competition worldwide to test athletes out-of-competition for doping.
- EPO positives found in 2012, 2015, 2017, 2019.
- 2024: Added GP NYC (UCI 1.2 pro race) — makes this one of only 3 UCI events in the US.
- GFNY brand extensions: apparel (2017), sunglasses (2018), coffee, casual wear.

## Climate (May in NYC/NJ)
- Average high 22°C/72°F, low 13°C/52°F
- 37% rain chance on any given day
- 7:10 AM start in cool conditions (~50-55°F), mid-race 70°F+
- Spring rain possible but generally pleasant

## Community & Culture
- "Most international peloton in the world" — 90+ countries
- GP NYC pro race: 126 riders across 22 teams (2025), including Alpecin-Deceuninck, Novo Nordisk
- Team GFNY Pro: 6 amateur champions race as pros, captained by Olympic medalist Nelson Vails
- Celebrity guests: Pedro Delgado, Raul Alcala, Nelson Vails
- Anti-doping program is a genuine differentiator — WADA Prohibited List, lifetime bans
- Post-race: BBQ, Michelob Ultra Beer Garden, NYC Bike Expo

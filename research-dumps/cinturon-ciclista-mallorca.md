## Status: defunct
Final (46th) edition ran April 6-9, 2011. Planned 2012 (47th) edition
suspended (not formally "cancelled") by organizer Germatur Gestión, citing
insufficient financing to meet minimum event requirements. Never returned.

## Format — NOT a gran fondo
Began as an amateur stage race, entered the UCI Europe Tour in 2005. 2011
regulations classified it UCI 2.2, restricted to Elite/U23 riders and UCI
Continental teams — an elite, lower-tier international men's stage race, not
an open-entry mass-participation event. Flagged via catalog_flags (discipline
enum left untouched per rule).

## Last-known vitals (2011, final edition)
Four days, 447.5km total: 7.1km individual time trial + road stages of
148.5km, 147.9km, 144km. ~200 riders started. Winner: Iker Camaño, ahead of
Tadej Valjavec and Ian Bibby.

## History
First held 1964, won by Francisco Juan Granell. Notable later winners: Sean
Kelly (1976), Vladimir Karpets (2000), Bradley Wiggins (2001), Sergio Henao
(2009). Frederik Bertelsen the only two-time winner (1994-95).

## Confirmed distinct from Mallorca 312
Mallorca 312 began separately in 2010 as a one-day amateur challenge (current
rules: open-entry gran fondo, 312/226/167km routes). The Cinturón was a
multi-day team race for Elite/U23/Continental riders. The two coexisted
2010-11 under different organizers — never the same event.

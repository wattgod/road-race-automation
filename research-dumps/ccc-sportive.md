# Research Dump: ORLEN Tour de Pologne Amatorow (formerly "CCC Sportive" / Tour de Pologne Amateur)

## Naming Clarification
The event commonly referred to as the "CCC Sportive" does NOT exist as a standalone race. CCC was a Polish shoe company that sponsored a UCI WorldTour cycling team (CCC Pro Team, 2019-2020, formerly CCC Sprandi Polkowice at Pro Continental level). CCC Team folded at the end of 2020 due to COVID-19 financial difficulties. The CCC brand was associated with Tour de Pologne through team sponsorship, not through an amateur sportive event.

The actual amateur sportive linked to the Tour de Pologne is the **ORLEN Tour de Pologne Amatorow — Ryszard Szurkowski Memorial**, organized by Lang Team (Czeslaw Lang's company). Since 2024, the full series of amateur races operates under the umbrella name **ORLEN Lang Team Race**, with the Bukowina Tatrzanska edition carrying the Szurkowski Memorial designation.

**Current title sponsor**: ORLEN (Polish petroleum company, replaced CCC branding entirely).

## Quick Facts
- **Discipline**: Road sportive (fully paved)
- **Series format**: 3 races per season across 3 distinct Polish regions
  - Stage 1: Arlamow (Bieszczady Mountains, SE Poland) — May
  - Stage 2: Bukowina Tatrzanska / BUKOVINA Resort (Tatra Mountains, S Poland) — August (flagship)
  - Stage 3: Bytow (Kashubia, N Poland) — September
- **Distances by stage**:
  - Arlamow: 75 km, ~1,600 m elevation gain
  - Bukowina (2025): 49 km, ~945 m elevation gain (2 x 25 km laps); 2024 edition was ~84 km
  - Bytow: Maxi ~93-108 km / Mini ~29-41 km (distances vary year to year)
- **Field size**: 2,000-2,500 per event (Bukowina flagship); ~3,000+ across the full series; started with 700 in 2010
- **Founded**: 2010 (first edition at the Tour de Pologne)
- **Entry fee (2026)**: PLN 465 (~EUR 110 / ~USD 120) for all three races (basic package); PLN 450 VIP package (250 spots, first-come-first-served); 50% discount under 18
- **Start format**: Mass start, electronic chip timing
- **Organizer**: Lang Team (Czeslaw Lang, director of Tour de Pologne since 1993)
- **Memorial name**: Ryszard Szurkowski Memorial (honoring the four-time Peace Race winner who died Feb 1, 2021)
- **Registration**: Via Decathlon GO platform; opens December for following season
- **Race connection**: Amateurs ride a portion of the Tour de Pologne UCI WorldTour route; the pro race follows on the same roads hours later
- **General classification**: Based on MAXI distance results from all three stages

## Course Profile

### Stage 1: Arlamow (Bieszczady Mountains)
- **Distance**: 75 km
- **Elevation gain**: ~1,600 m
- **Start/Finish**: Hotel Arlamow (a 4-star resort at ~440-580 m elevation)
- **Key climbs**: Kalwaria Paclawska and Gruszowa, each conquered twice
- **Terrain**: Demanding Bieszczady mountain loop through wild, forested southeastern Poland near the Ukrainian border
- **Road surface**: Paved mountain roads, generally good condition but narrower and more remote than typical Western European sportive roads
- **Character**: The most mountainous stage with the highest total climbing; remote, low-traffic roads through one of Poland's least populated regions

### Stage 2: Bukowina Tatrzanska — BUKOVINA Resort (Tatra Mountains) [FLAGSHIP]
- **Distance**: ~49 km (2025) — varies by year; 2024 was ~84 km
- **Elevation gain**: ~945 m (2025 49 km route)
- **Start**: BUKOVINA Resort, Bukowina Tatrzanska (~960-1,000 m above sea level)
- **Finish**: Rusin-Ski ski station
- **Key climbs**:
  - **Harnas Wall**: ~3 km, 6.4% average gradient, peaks of 14.5%
  - **BUKOVINA Wall** (Rzepiska/Gliczarow): Gradients up to 20% — the iconic signature climb
- **Terrain**: Two 25 km laps through the Tatra foothills; the same roads host the Tour de Pologne "queen stage" hours later
- **Road surface**: Well-maintained paved roads through tourist-heavy Podhale region
- **Character**: The marquee event — steep, punchy climbs at altitude with dramatic Tatra Mountain panoramas; Decathlon KOM positioned on the BUKOVINA Wall
- **Altitude**: Racing at 850-1,100 m; not high enough for altitude effects but adds scenic drama

### Stage 3: Bytow (Kashubia)
- **Distance**: Maxi ~93-108 km / Mini ~29-41 km (varies by edition)
- **Elevation gain**: Not published; rolling Kashubian terrain (hills, not mountains)
- **Start/Finish**: Bytow (hometown of Czeslaw Lang)
- **Key terrain**: Rolling Kashubian hills, farmland and lakes, relatively gentle compared to the mountain stages
- **Road surface**: Paved roads in good condition through the Kashubia Lakeland region
- **Character**: The most accessible stage; a celebration in Lang's birthplace with the rolling, scenic landscape of northern Poland

## Logistics

### Airports by Stage

**Arlamow (Stage 1)**:
- Rzeszow-Jasionka Airport (RZE): ~72-135 km depending on route, ~1h45-2h drive
- No closer commercial airport; this is remote southeastern Poland
- Hotel Arlamow offers airport transfer services

**Bukowina Tatrzanska (Stage 2 — Flagship)**:
- Poprad-Tatry Airport (TAT), Slovakia: 32 km / 48 min drive — closest but limited flights
- Krakow John Paul II International Airport (KRK): 85 km / ~1h25 drive — best-connected, numerous international flights
- FlixBus operates Krakow Airport to Bukowina Tatrzanska, ~2h40, EUR 3-6

**Bytow (Stage 3)**:
- Gdansk Lech Walesa Airport (GDN): 89 km / ~1h22 drive
- Well-connected airport with Ryanair, Wizz Air, LOT Polish Airlines, Lufthansa

### Lodging Strategy

**Bukowina Tatrzanska (flagship)**:
- BUKOVINA Resort (Hotel BUKOVINA, Hotel HARNAS) — the venue partner; 4-star, from ~$208/night on booking sites
- Includes access to Termy BUKOVINA thermal baths (20 pools, 28-36C water, 8 sauna types) — the largest thermal complex in Poland
- Bukowina Tatrzanska is a major Polish tourist resort with hundreds of pensions, guesthouses, and apartments; budget options from ~PLN 150-300/night (~$35-70)
- Nearby Zakopane (15 km) adds even more lodging inventory

**Arlamow**:
- Hotel Arlamow (4-star, the start/finish venue): from PLN 608/night (~$145); offers cycling-specific packages during Lang Team Race weekend
- Very limited alternative lodging nearby — this is remote Bieszczady; book the hotel or plan to drive in from Ustrzyki Dolne or Przemysl (~35 km)

**Bytow**:
- Small town with limited hotel inventory; guesthouses and agritourism available
- Gdansk (89 km) offers full hotel infrastructure if driving in for the day

### Race-Day Transport
- All stages are loops (start = finish) so no shuttle logistics needed
- Closed roads on race course; roads reopen for pro race later the same day
- Parking at start venues (Hotel Arlamow, BUKOVINA Resort, Bytow town center)

### Feed Zones / On-Course Support
- Basic package includes: race number with electronic chip, professional timekeeping, commemorative medal, sponsor gadgets, recovery meal, marked route, full medical security, and organizer support
- Feed zones on course (specific locations not published but standard for the distances)

## History

### Origin Story
- The Tour de Pologne dates to 1928 (founded by the Warsaw Cycling Society and Przegald Sportowy newspaper, inspired by the Tour de France). First edition: 7-11 September 1928, 71 riders. It remained amateur-only until 1993.
- In 1993, Czeslaw Lang became race director and transformed it into a professional event, eventually reaching UCI WorldTour status.
- In 2010, Lang created the Tour de Pologne Amatorow as a companion event, allowing amateur cyclists to ride sections of the professional course. First edition: 700 participants.
- The concept: amateurs race in the morning, professionals race the same roads in the afternoon — creating a unique "ride where the pros ride" experience.

### Czeslaw Lang
- Born May 17, 1955 in Kolczyglowy (near Bytow, Kashubia)
- Silver medalist, 1980 Moscow Olympics (team time trial)
- Double medalist at World Road Cycling Championships
- 9x Polish Champion; Tour de Pologne winner (1980)
- Professional career 1982-1989 (GIS-Olmo, Carrera, Del Tongo-Colnago, Malvor, Sidi Colnago)
- After retirement, built Lang Team into Poland's premier cycling event organizer
- Named among top 5 most influential people in Polish sports by Forbes Poland
- Appointed attache of Polish Olympic team for 2026 Cortina d'Ampezzo Winter Games

### Ryszard Szurkowski (Memorial Honoree)
- Born January 12, 1946; died February 1, 2021 (age 75)
- Four-time Peace Race winner (1970, 1971, 1973, 1975)
- 1973 Amateur Road World Champion + 2 additional World Championship golds
- Silver medalist at 1972 Munich and 1976 Montreal Olympics (team time trial)
- Could not turn professional due to Soviet-bloc restrictions despite offer from Molteni (Eddy Merckx's team)
- Managed Polish national team 1984-88; mentored Lech Piasecki to World Championship (1985)
- Active ambassador for the amateur race from its 2010 founding until a 2018 crash in Germany left him paralyzed
- His son Norbert died in the September 11, 2001 attacks
- Wroclaw named a tram after him in 2024

### CCC Connection (Why It Was Called "CCC Sportive")
- CCC (shoe company, owner Dariusz Milek) sponsored Polish cycling teams from 2000
- CCC Sprandi Polkowice was a Pro Continental team
- In 2018, CCC took over BMC Racing Team's WorldTour license, creating CCC Pro Team (2019-2020)
- CCC Team was the first Polish-registered WorldTour team to start the Tour de Pologne (2019, 76th edition)
- CCC's association with Tour de Pologne led to informal use of "CCC Sportive" in some English-language references
- CCC Team disbanded end of 2020 (sold WorldTour license to Circus-Wanty Gobert)
- ORLEN became the title sponsor of both the Tour de Pologne and the amateur series

### Growth Trajectory
- 2010: 700 participants (first edition)
- 2015-2018: Growth to ~1,500-2,000
- 2023: Rebranded as ORLEN Lang Team Race series (3-stage format)
- 2024: ~2,200 finishers at Bukowina alone; ~84 km route
- 2025: 2,000-2,500 at Bukowina (49 km route); record celebrity participation (Robert Kubica, Bartosz Zmarzlik)

### Notable Moments
- **2019**: CCC Team makes history as first Polish WorldTour squad at Tour de Pologne; amateur race runs same weekend
- **2021**: Race dedicated as Ryszard Szurkowski Memorial following his death
- **2025**: F1/Le Mans driver Robert Kubica and speedway world champion Bartosz Zmarzlik ride the amateur race, drawing massive media attention; TVP Sport broadcasts live
- The amateur race serves as prologue to the professional "queen stage" at Bukowina — riders experience the same dramatic climbs that decide the Tour de Pologne GC

## Climate

### Bukowina Tatrzanska (August — Flagship)
- **Avg high**: 22C / 72F
- **Avg low**: 12C / 54F
- **Temperature range during race**: 14-26C / 57-79F
- **Precipitation**: August is drier than June-July (peak rainfall); roughly 12-14 rainy days per month in summer
- **Altitude effect**: At ~1,000 m elevation, temperatures are 4-6C cooler than Polish lowlands
- **Afternoon thunderstorms**: Possible in Tatra Mountains — brief, intense, with risk of sudden temperature drops
- **Sunshine**: ~8-9 hours of sunshine per day in August
- **Risk factor**: Mountain weather is unpredictable; rain gear advisable even on clear mornings

### Arlamow (May)
- **Avg high**: 18-20C / 64-68F
- **Avg low**: 7-9C / 45-48F
- **Spring weather**: Variable; can be cool and wet. Late May generally pleasant.
- **Risk**: Higher precipitation probability than August

### Bytow (September)
- **Avg high**: 17-19C / 63-66F
- **Avg low**: 9-11C / 48-52F
- **Early autumn**: Generally stable weather; cooling temperatures. Baltic influence makes conditions milder but potentially damp.

## Community & Culture

### Atmosphere
- Celebration of Polish cycling heritage — not just a race but a cultural event
- The Bukowina flagship has a festival atmosphere at BUKOVINA Resort, with thermal baths as post-race recovery
- Bytow stage is a homecoming celebration in Czeslaw Lang's birthplace
- Strong emphasis on memorializing Ryszard Szurkowski and Poland's cycling legacy
- The unique "ride before the pros" format creates electric anticipation as the peloton knows WorldTour riders will follow

### Celebrity Participation
- **Robert Kubica** (F1 driver, 24h Le Mans winner): Regular participant, covers 12,000-15,000 km/year on a bicycle
- **Bartosz Zmarzlik** (multiple Speedway World Champion): Uses cycling for cross-training
- Polish sports celebrities, retired professional cyclists, and athletes from other disciplines regularly participate
- Czeslaw Lang himself rides with participants and engages with the peloton

### International Participation
- Predominantly Polish field — this is a domestic-focused event
- English-language website and regulations available; registration open to all nationalities
- Growing international interest but remains ~90%+ Polish participants
- Proximity to Slovakia (Poprad) and Czech Republic may draw some cross-border riders
- Not widely known on the international sportive circuit — discovery opportunity for foreign riders

### Media Coverage
- **TVP Sport** (Poland's national sports broadcaster): Live coverage and highlight packages
- Tour de Pologne Amatorow receives a dedicated 15-minute broadcast on TVP Sport/TVP Kobieta
- Full integration with Tour de Pologne UCI WorldTour media coverage (180+ countries for the pro race)
- Social media presence via Lang Team and Tour de Pologne channels
- Moderate domestic media coverage; minimal international coverage for the amateur event specifically
- The 2025 Kubica/Zmarzlik participation generated significant national sports media buzz

### Post-Race
- Recovery meal included in registration
- Commemorative medal (engraving available for PLN 25)
- At Bukowina: access to Termy BUKOVINA thermal baths — arguably the best post-race recovery facility of any sportive in Europe (20 pools, 8 saunas)
- Prize ceremony and overall classification awards
- Sponsor gadgets/swag in race pack

## Scoring Dimension Notes

### Distance (scoring 1-5 scale)
- Ranges from 29 km (Bytow Mini) to 108 km (Bytow Maxi), with flagship at 49-84 km depending on year
- For the flagship Bukowina event: moderate distance (49-84 km)
- Not a gran fondo distance — more of a sportive/medium format

### Climbing
- Bukowina: 945 m in 49 km is significant per-km climbing; BUKOVINA Wall at 20% is brutal
- Arlamow: 1,600 m in 75 km is genuinely mountainous
- Bytow: Rolling hills, modest climbing
- Flagship course punches well above its distance on climbing intensity

### Descent Technicality
- Mountain descents in Tatra foothills and Bieszczady are technical but on paved roads
- BUKOVINA Wall descent: steep, tight switchbacks
- Not Alpine-grade hairpins but demanding for the distances involved

### Road Surface
- Fully paved throughout all three stages
- Bukowina/Podhale: well-maintained tourist roads in good condition
- Arlamow/Bieszczady: roads may be narrower and rougher in remote sections
- Bytow/Kashubia: good-quality lowland roads

### Climate Risk
- August Tatras: moderate risk (afternoon thunderstorms possible)
- Mountain weather at 1,000 m adds unpredictability
- May (Arlamow): higher precipitation risk
- September (Bytow): generally favorable

### Altitude
- Bukowina: racing at 850-1,100 m — no physiological altitude effect but scenic mountain character
- Arlamow: 440-700 m
- Bytow: near sea level (~150 m)

### Logistics
- Bukowina: accessible from Krakow (85 km, major international airport) — good logistics
- Arlamow: remote, limited transport options, 2h from nearest airport — challenging logistics
- Bytow: 89 km from Gdansk — moderate logistics
- All events are loop courses (no shuttle needed)

### Prestige
- Official amateur extension of a UCI WorldTour race — significant prestige
- Ryszard Szurkowski Memorial adds historical gravitas
- Organized by Czeslaw Lang (Olympic medalist, race director since 1993)
- Not internationally recognized as a standalone sportive brand (unlike GFNY, L'Etape, Haute Route)
- More domestic prestige than international prestige

### Organization
- Professional organization by Lang Team — Poland's premier cycling event company
- Electronic timing (STS-Timing), full medical coverage, marked routes
- Integrates with UCI WorldTour logistics infrastructure
- TVP Sport broadcast coverage
- Registration via Decathlon GO (modern platform)

### Scenic Experience
- Bukowina: Tatra Mountain panoramas, traditional Podhale architecture — stunning
- Arlamow: Wild Bieszczady Mountains, forests, near-wilderness — dramatic and remote
- Bytow: Kashubian Lakeland, rolling farmland — pleasant but less dramatic
- Overall: varied scenic profiles across the three stages; Bukowina flagship is world-class scenery

### Community / Culture
- Deep roots in Polish cycling heritage (Szurkowski Memorial, Lang's legacy)
- "Ride before the pros" concept creates unique connection to professional peloton
- Festival atmosphere with thermal baths at Bukowina
- Celebrity participation adds star power
- Primarily domestic community; limited international networking

### Field Depth
- 2,000-2,500 at flagship (strong for Eastern Europe)
- Growing field with waiting-list potential
- Mix of serious amateur racers and recreational cyclists
- Former professional cyclists and celebrity athletes in the field
- Not as deep as major Western European sportives (15,000+ at L'Etape, etc.)

### Value
- PLN 465 (~EUR 110) for THREE races — exceptional value
- Includes timing, medal, recovery meal, medical coverage, route marking
- Compare: single-race gran fondo entry in Western Europe typically EUR 80-150+
- BUKOVINA Resort access adds premium post-race value

### Expenses
- Total trip cost moderate by European standards
- Poland is significantly cheaper than Western European destinations
- Bukowina accommodation: PLN 150-600/night ($35-145)
- Meals: PLN 30-80 ($7-19) for restaurant dining
- Flights to Krakow: typically EUR 50-200 from major European cities (budget carriers)
- Overall: low-to-moderate expense compared to Alpine or Mediterranean sportives

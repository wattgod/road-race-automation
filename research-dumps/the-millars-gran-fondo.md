# Research Dump: The Millars Gran Fondo

## Quick Facts
- 157 km (156.6-157.7 km per 2026 provisional route), ~2,200m / 7,218ft climbing
- Start/finish: Castellón de la Plana, Castellón Province, Spain
- Follows the Mijares/Millars river corridor through more than 20 municipalities and Serra d'Espadà Natural Park
- Founded 2024 (inaugural edition, Vila-Real, ~1,500 riders from 27 countries)
- 2025 edition relocated to Castellón de la Plana
- 2026 (third edition, 22 March 2026) sold out at 2,000 riders from 36 nationalities
- UCI affiliation: The Millars is Spain's only UCI Gran Fondo World Series qualifier. Fastest 25% in eligible age groups qualify for the UCI Gran Fondo World Championships.
- Organizer: Infinitri Sports Events S.L.
- Ambassadors: Ivan Basso, Joaquim "Purito" Rodríguez, Fabio Aru, Ibon Zugasti
- Signature climbs: Coll de la Ibola (17 km, ~3% avg to 580m), Coll de la Lloma (10 km, opening 5 km at 6-8% to 593m), Puerto de Aín, Salto del Caballo
- No shorter medio fondo route on the current official site — single 157 km distance only
- 2027 pricing: €65 promotional rising to €95, plus €12 non-federation-licence surcharge, €20 optional cancellation insurance
- Route explicitly described by organizer as provisional, subject to change for roadworks

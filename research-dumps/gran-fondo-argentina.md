# Research Dump — Gran Fondo Argentina (editorial wave 8, 2026-07-24)

Researched via codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, live web search) for GOLD-register editorial pass.

## Key verified facts

- **2026 date**: Confirmed Sunday, October 11, 2026 — the official schedule identifies it as the 4th edition, with the 122km start at 06:00 and 46km start at 06:20. Source: granfondoargentina.com/info/cronograma/ and granfondoargentina.com/reglamento/.
- **Status**: Active, registration open through Oct 1, 2026 or until the 4,000-rider cap is reached. Source: granfondoargentina.com/info/, go.granfondoargentina.com/.
- **Distance**: Current official 2026 offering is 122km (long) and 46km (short), start/finish at Parque Sarmiento, Av. Dr. Ricardo Balbín 4750. The prior file value of 123.9km reflected a 2025-edition figure (124km), not the current course.
- **Elevation**: The official long-course page simultaneously displays "323m Vertical" and "233m/1,060ft elevation" for the same course — these figures are internally inconsistent (1,060ft converts to ~323m, not 233m), and the same contradictory numbers are repeated on the 46km short-course page, suggesting a template-copy error. No single trustworthy elevation figure could be verified; treated as unverified/null in the database rather than guessed.
- **Field size**: 2026 capacity is 4,000 total (2,000 per distance) — a cap, not a finisher count. City of Buenos Aires government reported 4,000 cyclists participated in the 2025 (3rd) edition. 2024 (2nd edition): ~2,500 participants. 2023 (1st edition): 2,300 registered.
- **Organizer**: Gran Fondo Asociación Civil, a nonprofit civil association (personería No. 1959279). Team: Gaby Castillo (co-creator, general producer, race director), Matías Gutiérrez Moyano (co-creator, executive director), Darío Colla (sporting director). Event founded 2023.
- **Course character**: Fast, predominantly flat, fully closed-road urban course entirely within Buenos Aires city limits — NOT a pampas/rural fondo. 2026 start/finish moves to Parque Sarmiento. Uses paved city arterials and motorways: General Paz, Perito Moreno, 25 de Mayo, 9 de Julio Sur, Illia, Cantilo/Lugones, Parque de los Niños area. Closed to motor traffic, monitored by ~600 traffic cameras per organizer material. Finishers ride a ceremonial lap on the Parque Sarmiento cycling track. 2025's route (different start/finish, Puerto Madero) passed Paseo del Bajo, Costanera, near the Monumental and Vélez stadiums — not assumed to carry over to 2026's new start/finish.
- **UCI/prestige — IMPORTANT NEGATIVE FINDING**: This event is **NOT** listed in the UCI Gran Fondo World Series. The UCI's own 2026 series-expansion announcement (ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/2026-uci-gran-fondo-world-series-expands-with-nine-new-events/) identifies **Gran Fondo Siete Lagos** (Patagonia) as the UCI series' first event in Argentina — strongly indicating Gran Fondo Argentina (Buenos Aires) is not UCI-sanctioned or a UCI qualifier. No evidence found that it belongs to any other international series. It does have Buenos Aires city-government support and a legislative sporting-interest declaration, which is real but distinct from UCI/international sanctioning.
- **No major scandal/cancellation found.** Main concerns are the elevation-figure inconsistency and a weather-cancellation-without-refund policy. The rain-affected 2024 edition had minor slides but no major complications reported.

# SBS Bank Tour of Southland — Research Dump (Editorial Wave 4)

Researched: 2026-07-24 via codex gpt-5.6-sol foreground web research.

## Postponement and reschedule chronology

- Original 69th-edition dates: 2-8 November 2025.
- Postponed 27 October 2025 after an unprecedented 23 October regional storm triggered a Southland State of Emergency; traffic-management resources (scarce) were prioritized for cleanup/restoration, and organizers judged proceeding inappropriate for rider safety and community needs.
- New dates announced 29 October 2025: 18-24 January 2026.
- Additional route change: storm damage to Queens Park plus planned nearby roadworks forced the prologue, street race, and final circuit away from the traditional Queens Park course to Bainfield Road/Queens Drive in Waikiwi.
- Rescheduled race completed successfully 24 January 2026; Josh Burnett won.
- 70th edition scheduled 1-7 November 2026, returning to the traditional slot.

Sources: official Cycling Southland news archive (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/news/, 27 & 29 Oct 2025 entries); Scoop reschedule release (https://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/BU2510/S00491/sbs-bank-tour-of-southland-rescheduled-for-2026.htm, 29 Oct 2025); Scoop postponement release (https://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/CU2510/S00219/tour-of-southland-postponed-due-to-state-of-emergency.htm, 27 Oct 2025); Scoop revised-course release (https://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/CU2511/S00198/sbs-bank-tour-of-southland-announces-revised-course-for-69th-edition.htm, 26 Nov 2025); Cycling New Zealand 70th-edition announcement (https://www.cyclingnewzealand.nz/blog/post/166691/tour-of-southland-turns-70/, 24 Jun 2026).

Editorial framing used: call January's race the "rescheduled 69th edition" — two editions fall within calendar year 2026 because of the postponement.

## Field size and format

- January 2026: 96 riders in 16 six-rider teams. Source: official 18 January start report (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/news/) and official teams page (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/teams/).
- Typical/promotional norm: "more than 100 riders from about 18 teams" per Southland NZ destination site (https://southlandnz.com/events/southlands-unmissable-events/tour-of-southland/) — treated as rounded norm, not exact 2026 figure.
- NOT organized into separate elite/amateur divisions — one men's team-race peloton; elite and U23 race together; U23 and O35 are secondary in-race classifications; club/regional/mixed/national/eligible Continental-team riders can share the race. No mass-entry recreational wave. Source: 2021 race regulations (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/files/22485_2021101894847-1634503727.pdf); current classification material (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/classifications/).

## Sanction status

- Gained UCI 2.2 status in 2002 (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/history/).
- Later relinquished UCI international classification, became a stand-alone national tour (contemporary Otago Daily Times report: https://www.odt.co.nz/sport/cycling/cycling-possible-now-tour-attract-top-nz-riders).
- Latest located official regulations describe it as a National Tour on the Cycling New Zealand national calendar, organized under UCI racing regulations — not the same as current UCI-calendar sanction. No indexed 2026 technical manual found to confirm exact current wording. Database-safe label used: "Cycling New Zealand national-calendar/National Tour; run under UCI-style regulations; not currently advertised as a UCI-classified race."
- Fully organized/supported (not self-supported): commissaires, race radio, neutral technical vehicles, motorbike support, medical services, team vehicles, team-manager meetings per official regulations; trained marshals and traffic management per organizer volunteer/traffic-support material and Invercargill City Council road-closure documentation.

## Current key stages/climbs

- Blackmount Hill: still current — appeared on Stage 3 (Riverton-Te Anau), significant tactical point in January 2026. Source: official January coverage (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/news/).
- The Remarkables: principal current mountain finish — Stage 4, ~6km ski-field access road ascent. Source: 21 Jan 2026 report (https://southlandapp.nz/NewsStory/defending-champion-climbs-into-sbs-bank-tour-of-southland-orange-jersey/697046e7d0f2d60047166fce).
- Bluff Hill/Motupohue: the other decisive hilltop finish, reached after the long Stage 6. Source: 23 Jan 2026 report (https://southlandapp.nz/NewsStory/tour-of-southland-stage-ends-in-photo-finish/697312b74661df0047b169f1).
- Coronet Peak is HISTORICAL, not current — replaced by The Remarkables in 2020, absent from both 2024 and January 2026 routes. Source: 2020 route-change report (https://southlandapp.nz/NewsStory/new-route-to-challenge-sbs-bank-tour-of-southland-riders/5f4d82c19ffdb10028127940). This profile's youtube_data (2015/2016 archival videos) references Coronet Peak as the then-current queen stage — retained as historical color in youtube_data, but course_description now correctly identifies The Remarkables as the current queen stage and explains the 2020 swap.
- November 2026 (70th edition) climb lineup remains provisional/unpublished as of the 24 June 2026 federation statement.

## History

- Founded 1956 (not an inferred ~1957). Inaugural three-day Tour began 27 September 1956 as part of Invercargill's centenary celebrations; Kelvin Hastie won. No Tour was held in 1958 (explains edition numbering vs. elapsed years). Became team-based in 1995; gained UCI status 2002. Source: official history (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/history/).
- 69th edition = January 2026 (rescheduled); 70th edition = 1-7 November 2026. Sources: official site; Cycling New Zealand 24 Jun 2026 announcement.

## Defensible weaknesses (evidence-based, not invented complaint quotes)

- High entry barrier — selected, licensed team race, not individually enterable.
- Team-intensive logistics — week-long campaign requiring riders, manager/driver support, vehicles, equipment across Invercargill/Te Anau/wider region. Organizer maintains dedicated accommodation/van-hire info (https://www.tourofsouthland.com/pages/local-information/).
- Travel burden — Invercargill Airport has scheduled direct domestic flights from Auckland/Christchurch/Wellington, but international arrivals need a domestic connection (https://invercargillairport.co.nz/invercargill-airport/).
- Weather exposure — January finale raced in wind/rain; November date historically confronts severe wind/changing weather (Cycling New Zealand, 24 Jan 2026: https://www.cyclingnewzealand.nz/blog/post/159302/burnett-wins-third-sbs-bank-tour-of-southland-title/).
- Recent schedule uncertainty — 2025 postponement was real and materially affected teams/accommodation/volunteers/roads/course; framed as an unprecedented emergency response, not chronic unreliability.
- No current UCI points/status — nationally prestigious but less valuable for riders chasing international UCI ranking points.
- Incomplete upcoming-course info — November 2026 route/distance still pending as of the 24 June federation announcement despite entries opening in July.

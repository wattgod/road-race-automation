# Research Dump: GFNY Miami (Michelob ULTRA Gran Fondo New York — Miami)

## Quick Facts
- **Long course**: ~52 mi / 84 km (2 loops of Krome Avenue); **Medium course**: ~26 mi / 42 km (1 loop). Note: 2024-2025 editions listed the long course as ~70.7 mi / 113.8 km (likely 3 loops); the 2026 official course page describes a 2-loop, 52-mile long course with full road closure. Third-party sites (finishers.com) still list 70.71 mi for 2026 — treat official miami.gfny.com as authoritative.
- **Elevation**: Virtually zero. Average elevation ~6 ft (2m) above sea level. Long course has ~53 ft (16m) total elevation gain — the flattest course in the entire GFNY World Series.
- **Location**: Krome Avenue (State Highway 997), western Miami-Dade County, Florida, USA. Western edge of Miami, adjacent to the Everglades.
- **Start/Finish**: Raccoon Lot at Miccosukee Casino & Resort, 500 SW 177th Ave, Miami, FL 33194.
- **Date**: Third Sunday in April. 3rd edition: April 19, 2026. Inaugural: April 21, 2024. 2nd edition: April 13, 2025.
- **Field size**: ~700 riders from 30+ countries at the inaugural 2024 edition. Expected to grow for 2026.
- **Founded**: 2024 (inaugural edition). Part of the GFNY World Series (founded 2011 in NYC).
- **Entry fee**: Tiered pricing with monthly increases. GFNY entry typically includes official race jersey (Ale brand, made in Italy), chip timing, finisher medal, goodie bag, aid station support. Exact pricing for Miami not publicly listed in search results, but GFNY regional events generally range $150-250 depending on registration tier. VIP upgrade available (email support@gfny.com).
- **Start format**: Mass start with corral system. 5K runners start at 6:30 AM, cyclists follow.
- **Route options**: Long course (competitive, ranked, 2 loops) and Medium course (recreational, 1 loop, timed but unranked — results listed alphabetically).
- **Cutoff**: 8:50 AM cutoff at Mile 26.3 (1h50m from start, ~15 mph average) to continue to the 2nd loop.
- **Also includes**: 5K run (2.5 km north, U-turn, 2.5 km south; must finish under 30 minutes).
- **Title sponsor**: Michelob ULTRA (title sponsor of all GFNY USA events since 2024).
- **Designation**: GFNY North America Championship. Serves as the North American season opener.
- **Official supporter**: Miami-Dade County Tourist Development Council + Miami-Dade County Department of Cultural Affairs.

## Course Profile
- **Terrain**: Absolutely flat. South Florida is a limestone plateau with virtually no topographic relief. Krome Avenue runs north-south through the agricultural flatlands west of suburban Miami, paralleling the eastern boundary of the Everglades. There are no hills, no bridges of consequence, no overpasses on the race course. The 53 ft of total "elevation gain" across the long course is essentially measurement noise from road crown and gentle undulations.
- **Road surface**: Krome Avenue (State Highway 997) is a four-lane divided state highway. Paved asphalt, maintained by FDOT, generally in good condition. Wide lanes with clear sightlines. No cobbles, no gravel, no technical descents. The road is arrow-straight for long stretches.
- **2026 road closure upgrade**: For the 3rd edition, all four lanes of Krome Avenue — both northbound and southbound — will be fully closed to motor traffic. This is a major upgrade from 2024-2025, when only one lane in each direction was closed to traffic (with regular traffic coned off to the other lanes and managed by police). The full closure makes this exceptionally safe and allows the peloton to spread across the full road width.
- **Course layout**: Out-and-back format. Riders exit Miccosukee Resort, turn left (north) onto Krome Avenue, ride ~13 miles north, make a U-turn, ride ~13 miles south past the start area, completing one loop. Medium course finishers stop here (~26 miles). Long course riders continue for a second identical loop, finishing at the timing line on Krome Avenue after ~52 miles.
- **Scenery**: Sawgrass prairies, cypress trees draped with Spanish moss, tropical wetland fauna (alligators, wading birds, raptors). The Everglades are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The landscape is vast, open, and unlike any other GFNY course — a subtropical marshland ecosystem.
- **Technical features**: None. No climbs, no descents, no sharp corners, no cobbles. The only variable is wind — on a flat, exposed road with no tree cover or buildings for wind protection, crosswinds and headwinds on the return leg can be significant. Strong tropical winds are the defining course challenge. April is Miami's windiest month (avg 13.6 mph / 21.9 kph, gusts 19-24 mph possible).
- **Racing character**: This is a power/aero course. Draft management and pack positioning are critical. No climbing ability required. Expect fast speeds — the inaugural winner finished 70.7 miles in under 2:40. Sprint finishes are likely on the flat run-in.

## Logistics
- **Nearest airports**:
  - Miami International Airport (MIA): ~17 miles / 20-30 minutes by car from start venue. Primary airport.
  - Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL): ~45 minutes north. Good alternative, especially for budget airlines.
- **Flights**: United Airlines offers GFNY discount (code ZQT3627310). SkyTeam Alliance offers up to 15% off Business and Economy fares.
- **Ground transport**: Renting a car is strongly recommended. Need a car for expo pickup (in downtown Miami area), race-day transport to Miccosukee Resort (western edge of Miami), and general sightseeing. Uber/Lyft available from airports.
- **Arrival timing**: Recommended arrival is Friday (day before expo). If arriving Saturday, flight must land by 3:00 PM to make expo/packet pickup. You cannot race without your race materials.
- **Packet pickup / Expo**: Pre-race activities (packet pickup, expo) take place in the heart of Miami, NOT at the race start venue. Race-day activities (start, finish, awards, post-race party) are at Miccosukee Casino & Resort.
- **Start area**: Miccosukee Casino & Resort, Raccoon Lot. Ample free parking. RV parking available at $25/day (no hookups; parking-only lot; sells out in April). Pool, sauna, and gym access included with RV parking.
- **Lodging**: April is peak season in Miami — book early. GFNY partner hotels offer special rates and guarantee: bikes allowed in room, 4:00 AM race-day breakfast. Room blocks expire in February/March or when sold out. Wide range of Miami accommodations: South Beach luxury, downtown business hotels, airport-area budget options, or stay at Miccosukee Resort itself.
- **Feed zones / Aid stations**: GFNY entry includes full aid station support along the course (standard GFNY protocol: water, sports drink, gels, fruit, mechanical support). Specific aid station locations for 2026 not published at time of research.
- **Post-race**: Awards ceremony and post-race party at Miccosukee Resort. Michelob ULTRA Finish Festival with "Cheer Beers." Finisher medals for all distances. Finisher towels.

## History
- **Origin**: GFNY Miami is a regional expansion of the GFNY (Gran Fondo New York) global brand. GFNY was founded in 2011 by Uli and Lidia Fluhme in New York City, inspired by Italian gran fondo culture. The NYC event grew from 1,900 riders in year one to a global series of 30+ events across 15+ countries by the 2020s.
- **Inaugural edition (2024)**: Announced as part of the 2024 GFNY calendar. The first edition was held April 21, 2024, with 700 riders from 30+ countries. Designated as the 2024 GFNY North America Championship. GFNY CEO Uli Fluhme: "Miami is a hotbed for a lot of fun things and that definitely also includes road cycling, so we're proud to be able to host a GFNY there." Course was 70.7 miles on Krome Avenue with partial road closure (one lane each direction).
- **2024 race action**: The race started fast with speeds hitting 50 km/h (31 mph). After ten aggressive minutes, the peloton split into three groups. At mile 40, Chris Frederick (BMC Team) broke away and extended a 45-second lead. In the chase group of 15 riders: Andrea De Silvestris (Officine Mattio) and GFNY Republica Dominicana runner-up Ramon Adames, who closed the gap, leading to a sprint finish. Women's winner: Emma Drabe (USA) in 2:42:28, followed by Melissa Gomez (USA) and Maria Balatti (Argentina).
- **Second edition (2025)**: Held April 13, 2025. Oscar Stangoni (Aros Cycling/USA), the GFNY Florida Sebring 2024 champion, won in 2:39:50, beating Officine Mattio teammates Michele Bertaina (Italy) and Sebastian Sanchez (USA) in another sprint finish.
- **2026 milestone**: Full closure of all four lanes of State Highway 997 (Krome Avenue) — a project two years in the making. Represents a significant upgrade to rider safety and race experience. Miami-Dade County Tourist Development Council renewed partnership. Expected to welcome riders from 30+ countries.
- **GFNY global context**: GFNY Miami is one of ~7 GFNY USA events (alongside NYC World Championship, Florida Sebring, Pittsburgh, Maryland, etc.) and part of 30+ global events including races in France (7), Latin America (14+), and destinations on every continent. GFNY NYC is the World Championship (held third Sunday in May, ~4,000-6,000 riders).
- **Notable**: Selected as a Top 10 North American Gran Fondo for 2026 by Gran Fondo Guide.

## Climate (April in Miami)
- **Temperature**: Average high 82-85F (28-29C), average low 65-71F (18-22C). Race-day morning start temps likely 68-74F (20-23C). Afternoon highs can reach 90F (32C) on warmer days.
- **Humidity**: April is Miami's least humid month (relative — still humid by most standards). Average relative humidity 72%. Morning humidity peaks at ~80%, dropping to ~53% by early afternoon. Dew point moderate but noticeable.
- **Precipitation**: ~85 mm (3.3 in) total for April, distributed over ~7 days. Roughly 23% chance of rain on any given day. April is in the dry season (November-April). Thunderstorms more common May-October. Low rain risk on race day, but tropical showers are always possible.
- **Wind**: April is Miami's windiest month. Average wind speed 13.6 mph (21.9 kph). Sustained winds 8-18 mph common, gusts to 19-24 mph possible. On the flat, exposed Krome Avenue corridor, wind is the primary weather-related challenge. Crosswinds from the Everglades can be relentless. Aero equipment and drafting tactics are rewarded.
- **Sun**: April is Miami's sunniest month. UV index high. Sunscreen, arm cooling, and hydration are essential.
- **Heat risk**: Moderate. Not yet full summer heat, but 82-85F with humidity and no shade on Krome Avenue creates real heat stress for riders from cooler climates. Acclimatization recommended for Northern riders.
- **Overall climate risk**: Low-to-moderate. Heat/humidity manageable in April (vs. summer), rain unlikely but possible, wind is the real variable.

## Community & Culture
- **International peloton**: 30+ countries represented at the inaugural edition. Miami's position as the "Gateway to Latin America" naturally draws strong participation from Central and South American riders, Caribbean nations, and European cyclists. The GFNY brand has deep roots in Latin American cycling (14+ races across the region in 2025).
- **Latin American connection**: GFNY describes Miami as "the Latin American capital" and explicitly markets to riders from the Northeast US, Latin America, and Europe. The post-race awards ceremony at the inaugural event was described as "chanting and dancing riders... a cycling party under the Floridian sun. Podium finishers grabbed their national flag from the start corrals and showed their colors with pride."
- **Local Miami cycling community**: Miami has an active road cycling scene, with many local riders already participating in GFNY Sebring, GFNY NYC, and other GFNY events. The event taps into existing South Florida cycling culture.
- **Cultural experience**: Miami offers a rich cultural experience beyond the race: South Beach, Coral Gables, Coconut Grove, Little Havana, Little Haiti, Wynwood Arts District, Art Deco architecture. The county partnership specifically highlights "celebrating the region's vibrant cultural identity, global appeal, and active lifestyle."
- **Race weekend format**: Multi-day experience. Friday arrival recommended. Saturday expo and packet pickup in downtown Miami. Sunday race at Miccosukee, followed by awards ceremony and Michelob ULTRA Finish Festival. Family-friendly destination.
- **GFNY membership ecosystem**: Riders can join the GFNY Club, access group rides, coaching plans, training camps. Results contribute to GFNY Rankings (top 3 results per year count; regional championships carry 2x point multiplier; NYC World Championship carries 3x). Miami as a regional championship offers double points.
- **Pro-am bridge**: GFNY is exploring adding a professional cycling race alongside the amateur gran fondo in Miami, similar to the GP NYC UCI 1.2 pro race added in 2024 alongside GFNY NYC.
- **Media presence**: GFNY has a YouTube channel, podcast, Gruppo newsletter. Moderate media coverage for a regional event, elevated by the GFNY brand association. Not yet at the coverage level of GFNY NYC, but growing.
- **Atmosphere**: Festive, inclusive, international. Not a super-serious racing-only event — the medium course is explicitly recreational and unranked. But the long course competitive field is legitimate, with continental-level racers (Officine Mattio team members, national federation riders) competing for the North American Championship title.

## Scoring Dimension Notes
- **Distance (2)**: 52 miles / 84 km is short for a gran fondo. Well below the traditional Italian gran fondo standard of 120+ km. The 2024-2025 long course at ~70.7 mi was more competitive; if 2026 stays at 52 mi, this is on the shorter end.
- **Climbing (1)**: 53 ft / 16m elevation gain. Functionally flat. The flattest GFNY course worldwide.
- **Descent technicality (1)**: No descents whatsoever.
- **Road surface (3-4)**: Well-maintained FDOT state highway. Smooth asphalt, wide lanes, no technical features. Clean and fast.
- **Climate risk (3)**: Heat/humidity moderate in April, wind is the primary variable. Not extreme but needs attention.
- **Altitude (1)**: Sea level. Average elevation 6 ft / 2m.
- **Logistics (4)**: Miami has world-class airport connectivity (MIA direct from nearly everywhere). Abundant lodging. Car rental needed. Course venue is 17 miles from MIA. Well-organized GFNY logistics (partner hotels, flight discounts, detailed race guide).
- **Prestige (3)**: GFNY North America Championship designation carries weight within the GFNY ecosystem. Top 10 North American Gran Fondo for 2026 (Gran Fondo Guide). Only 3 years old — still building prestige. Part of the most recognized gran fondo brand globally. But not yet in the league of established Italian gran fondos or GFNY NYC.
- **Organization (4)**: GFNY is a professional, well-run organization with 14+ years of experience. Full road closure (2026), chip timing, aid stations, anti-doping program, detailed race guide, VIP options. Michelob ULTRA title sponsorship, county government partnership. Minor concern: event is only in year 3.
- **Scenic experience (3)**: The Everglades are a unique, dramatic landscape — sawgrass prairies, cypress trees, subtropical fauna. But the course is an out-and-back on a straight highway, repeated twice. Views are expansive but repetitive. Not mountainous, not coastal, not through charming towns. Visually striking for its otherworldliness, but limited variety.
- **Community/Culture (4)**: Strong international participation from 30+ countries. Latin American cultural energy. Festive post-race party atmosphere. Miami's cultural richness adds off-course value. Double GFNY ranking points. Multi-sport weekend (5K + cycling).
- **Field depth (2-3)**: 700 riders at inaugural edition is modest. Growing event. Competitive front group includes continental-level racers, but overall field is small compared to established gran fondos. North American Championship draws legitimate competitors.
- **Value (3)**: Entry includes jersey, medal, chip timing, aid stations. Typical GFNY value proposition. But total trip cost to Miami in April (peak season lodging, car rental, flights) adds up. Racing experience is good but course is very simple (flat out-and-back).
- **Expenses (3)**: Miami in April = peak season pricing. Hotels can be expensive. Car rental required. Entry fee in the $150-250 range (estimate). Flights to MIA are generally well-served with competitive pricing. Total weekend budget likely $800-1,500+ depending on lodging choices.

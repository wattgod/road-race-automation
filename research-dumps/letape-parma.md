# Research Dump: L'Étape Parma

## Quick Facts
- Ran two editions only: 2024 (inaugural) and 2025 (final, 4 May 2025)
- Official 2026 L'Étape Italy announcement (27 December 2025) lists two Piedmont stops — Entracque (31 May) and Mondovì (20 September) — with no Parma date; L'Étape Italy has relocated out of Emilia-Romagna
- 2025 (final) edition: start/finish Parco Ducale, Parma
- Organizer course guide: 134 km / 2,660 m long route, 81.1 km / 1,095 m short route
- Municipal road-closure roadbook (Comune di Parma): 133.4 km / 3,167 m long route — a 507 m discrepancy with the organizer's own figure for the same 2025 edition
- Course: plains/foothills south of Parma through Lesignano de' Bagni, Neviano degli Arduini, Tizzano Val Parma, climbing to Lagrimone-Schia/Monte Caio (km 56.8-63.9, 7.1 km, ~7.3% net average, cresting 1,228 m) then Passo dei Caprioli (1,273 m)
- Rivalta KOM: km 18.9-22.9, 4.0 km, 165-353 m
- Passo del Crocione KOM: climbed twice — km 33.2-38.6 (5.4 km to 629 m) and again km 89.4-91.5 (2.1 km, ~7.4% net average)
- 2025 field: 2,000+ riders from 20 countries (reported, not an official verified cap)
- Co-organized by Extragiro, Comune di Parma, A.S.O., Regione Emilia-Romagna

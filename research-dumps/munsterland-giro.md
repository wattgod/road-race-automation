## Flagged claim: [UCI affiliation] tagline — "UCI pros battle on the same roads" / "Germany's bicycle capital"

**TRUE.** The event is structured as amateur Jedermann races in the morning
(Cup 65/95/125) followed by the UCI professional race in the afternoon, on
October 3 each year — same date, same finish location.

- Wikipedia confirms current UCI classification: "became part of the new UCI
  ProSeries in 2020," i.e. currently a UCI ProSeries (1.Pro) category race.
  Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%BCnsterland_Giro — HTTP 200 verified.
- History: first held 2006 as UCI Europe Tour 1.2, upgraded to 1.1 in 2007,
  1.HC in 2015, UCI ProSeries (1.Pro) in 2020.
- procyclingstats.com confirms annual pro results 2023-2025 on Oct 3 each year:
  https://www.procyclingstats.com/race/munsterland-giro/2025/result

## Flagged claim: [UCI affiliation] final_verdict.one_liner — "Schlossplatz alongside a UCI 1.Pro professional race"

**TRUE — finish location and same-day pairing confirmed.** All Jedermann/Cup
races and the professional race finish at Schlossplatz in Münster; "the
international professional field provides the finale with an exciting sprint"
in the afternoon, after the amateur Cup races finish there in the morning.
Münster is widely branded Germany's "Fahrradhauptstadt" (bicycle capital) in
tourism and event marketing.

- Source: https://www.muensterland.com/en/tourism/topics/cycling/sparkassen-munsterland-giro/ (already in profile citations) — official tourism board copy backs the "bicycle capital" framing.
- Source: https://www.westfalen-regional.de/de/giro/ — event/region background, HTTP 200 verified.

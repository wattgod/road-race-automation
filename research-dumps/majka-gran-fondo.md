# Research Dump: Majka Gran Fondo

Wave 6 / 2026-07-24. Researched via codex gpt-5.6-sol web search.

## Quick Facts
- Status: **Revival announced, but unscheduled. No 2026 edition confirmed.** Not defunct (a March 2026 Małopolska regional-government notice reports organizers in discussion with Rafał Majka about a "next edition"), but not currently eligible as a scheduled event either — no year, date, course, or registration published for any future edition.
- Latest completed edition: **8-9 June 2024**. Official results archive stops there; the official site still displays 2024 dates/routes/rules/registration links (stale, not evidence of a 2025/2026 running).
- Does not appear on the current 2025-26 or 2026-27 UCI Gran Fondo World Series calendar. A February 2024 organizer statement promising a 2025 UCI-series return is an unfulfilled forecast, not confirmed fact.
- On-file "August TBD 2026" date is unsupported — no August edition has ever been held (2022/2023 editions ran in May, 2024 in June).

## Identity & Name Resolution
- "Majka" = Polish road cyclist **Rafał Majka**, identified by the organizer as creator/host/ambassador/co-organizer. Legal organizer: G&G Promotion Grzegorz Golonko. Athlete-eponymous name, not an apparel/equipment sponsor.
- Genuinely a mass-start road cycling gran fondo (paired with an individual TT) for amateur/Masters riders — official regulations mandate road bicycles for the two longer distances, prohibit e-assist, and disqualify motor use. Not a motorcycle event, not virtual/Zwift.
- Name variants for the SAME event: "Majka Gran Fondo," "Majka Granfondo," "Rafał Majka Gran Fondo," "Majka UCI Gran Fondo," "Małopolska Majka Gran Fondo." A March 2026 government article spells it "Majka Grand Fondo" — no corresponding site rebrand found, treated as an editorial spelling variant not a verified rebrand.
- **Distinct related event — do not conflate**: "Małopolska Majka Days" predates Majka Gran Fondo (already running in 2018), and was presented in 2023 as the second race in a two-event Małopolska series alongside Majka Gran Fondo. The on-file founding year of 2018 belongs to Majka Days, not to Majka Gran Fondo (founded 2022).
- **PZU Gran Fondo Series** is separate despite Majka's 2026 PZU ambassadorship — its calendar covers Ełk/Poznań/Warsaw/Toruń, not Dobczyce.

## Location & Course Correction
- **Canonical location: Dobczyce, Lesser Poland (Małopolskie), Poland — near Kraków.** "Krakow, Poland" (on file) is acceptable only as a broad regional locator, not the actual host city — the 2024 TT and all road-race starts/finishes were in Dobczyce.
- 2024 route region: Dobczyce and the surrounding Pogórze Wielickie area, including Wiśniowa, Raciechowice, and Zegartowice (Majka's home village).
- Myślenice is historical context only: 2022/2023 road races were based in/around Myślenice (TT in Dobczyce); in 2024 the event consolidated around Dobczyce. The on-file course_description's "Myslenice" double-loop detail is obsolete — it describes the 105km 2022-23 UCI course (which added a repeated 34km loop), not the shorter 2024 Dobczyce course.
- Actual month: latest edition was June 2024; 2022/2023 were May. No August edition, ever, is documented.

## Verified Course Facts (latest completed edition, 2024)
- Main Gran Fondo: **86.1 km / 1,510 m ascent** (official page's detail section gives 85.9km — "approximately 86km" is safest). On-file 117.5km/1,585m is unsupported.
- Other distances: Medio Fondo 68.1km/1,180m; Adventure 48.5km/825m.
- Route: start Dobczyce town hall, finish Dobczyce sports complex.
- Surface: paved public roads, rolling terrain; road bicycles required for Gran Fondo/Medio Fondo.
- Gran Fondo/Medio Fondo split at km 52.3; feed stations ~km38 and ~km56.
- No reliable named/categorized climbs published for the 2024 Gran Fondo course itself. A separate organizer-linked 16km TT route names "Brzezowa Up" (2.68km @ 4.9%) and "Dębowa Climb" (1.48km @ 7.3%) — these belong to the TT, not the Sunday Gran Fondo, and should not be attached to it.
- Field: 500+ riders started the Sunday 2024 road races across all three distances combined (not 500 on the long course alone).
- Entry fee: not currently verifiable — surviving 2024 rules reference a tiered payment schedule without stated amounts; registration service link is expired. Do not infer a fee from the separately-listed 50 PLN/€20 rider-change fee.
- Founded: **2022** for Majka Gran Fondo itself (UCI contemporaneously called it new/first-edition). On-file 2018 belongs to the separate, older Majka Days event.
- No primary-source support found for guaranteed "Tatra views" on the 2024 route — the Tatras appear only as a nearby regional attraction on an old UCI page, not a documented course sightline. Softened/removed.

# Göteborgsgirot — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Identity resolution
Confirmed a genuine, distinct cycling festival — not Göteborgsvarvet (the 21.1km running
half marathon). Göteborgsgirot premiered 10-12 May 2013; the official site lists cycling
results from 2013 through its tenth edition in 2023.

## Eligibility
**Status: unknown.** The organizer explicitly cancelled the 2025 edition (following a 2024
pause) but said it was still exploring a future return. No 2026 announcement, date,
course, or active registration has been published. "Cancelled" applies only to the 2025
edition specifically; "defunct" is not yet proven given the organizer's stated intent to
return.

Official site (curl-verified 200): https://goteborgsgirot.se/ — its registration page is
legacy content linking to MIKA Timing, not verified 2026 registration.

## Vitals
2026 date/distances/elevation: not announced. Historical event base: Heden, central
Gothenburg. A third-party directory's "May 22-24" claim is explicitly marked
"Unconfirmed" and reproduces an obsolete schedule — do not use.

Last verified program (2023, not current): 150km Gran Fondo road race, 60km Stadsgirot
road ride, 50km XC MTB. Road routes crossed Gothenburg and parts of Härryda, Mölndal, Mark
and Kungsbacka; MTB course used Delsjön's forest terrain. No trustworthy elevation figure
available from the organizer for any year.

## Course character
Urban-to-rural, rolling road cycling through numerous Gothenburg neighborhoods and
surrounding municipalities. The shorter road option (Stadsgirot) was recreational; the
Gran Fondo mixed experienced amateurs with elite riders. The MTB option combined trail,
gravel and pavement.

## History
Conceived by Christer Wallin in autumn 2012, launched 2013. Operated by
Göteborgsgirot/Giro Cycle Club with local clubs and companies; early corporate ownership
involved GAIS, Frölunda HC and GT/Expressen.

## Criticism
Participation failed to recover to pre-pandemic levels, forcing the 2024 pause and 2025
cancellation — a clear viability risk. The website leaves obsolete registration and 2023
pricing/forms online, creating avoidable ambiguity for anyone finding it today.
Historically, the organizer acknowledged muddy, difficult MTB sections and congestion
before redesigning that course.

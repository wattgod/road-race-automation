# Yangyang Gran Fondo — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Identity check
Confirmed distinct from Korea Epic Ride (a separate, self-supported 500 km event, 55%
unpaved forest roads, koreaepicride.kr) and from any older/differently-named Korean gran
fondo. This is a Yangyang County-hosted, supported road gran fondo through Yangyang and
Inje, first held **17 June 2023** (130 km/2,371 m, ~1,200 finishers) — the profile's
existing "founded 2017" is incorrect and should be corrected to 2023.

## Eligibility
No official 2026 edition announced as of this research date. Official registration site
(https://ygranfondo.raceplan.co.kr/, operated by Wizrun/Raceplan) is live (curl-verified
200) but frozen on the 2025 edition. Ahotu's Korean listing shows no 2026 edition (curl
403 on fetch, so treated as a secondary/unverifiable signal, not evidence of cancellation).
**Status: unknown** — insufficient to call defunct or cancelled; last edition evidenced 2025.

## Vitals (last verified edition — 26 April 2025)
- Distance/elevation: 151 km / 2,380 m long course; 68 km mediofondo option
- Start/finish: Yangyang Namdaecheon Sports Park, Yangyang-eup
- Course runs through Yangyang and Inje counties, East Sea coastal roads plus mountain
  sectors around Garisan and Osaek Pass/Hangyeryeong (Osaek climb is real, ~897 m)
- Coastal "headwind section" is not an officially documented course feature — real,
  weather-dependent wind exposure reported by at least one rider, not a fixed segment

## History
Inaugural edition 17 June 2023 (130 km/2,371 m, ~1,200 finishers); 2024 organizer material
explicitly calls that year's edition the second. Host: Yangyang County Sports Council with
Wizrun Solution handling registration/logistics.

## Criticism
No major controversy found. A credible 2024 participant report cited corrupted GPX
elevation data, rough pavement around km 105, and incomplete traffic exclusion on
Hangyeryeong with tight descents shared with vehicles — the county's own traffic notice
confirms controls varied between slowed traffic and full closures rather than a fully
closed course throughout.

# Research Dump: Vélomédiane Claudy Criquiélion

## Quick Facts
- Founded 1989 (not 1990) by Hubert Goffinet in La Roche-en-Ardenne, Belgium — originally ~170 km with 3,300 m and 20+ climbs. The organizer's own site calls it "the first Belgian cyclosportive."
- Claude "Claudy" Criquiélion was the event's patron from its FIRST (1989) edition — the name predates his death and is not a posthumous memorial rebrand
- **Correction to a working assumption**: Criquiélion did NOT die in a road-cycling accident. He died 18 February 2015 following a severe stroke and brain hemorrhage (Cyclingnews, quoting the hospital; NOS). He was also not merely a "world championship contender" — he won the professional road World Championship outright in 1984, and is separately famous for the disputed 1988 collision with Steve Bauer that cost him another title shot.
- 2026 edition (37th): confirmed active, Saturday, 22 August 2026, La Roche-en-Ardenne, registration open (advance entries close 17 August, onsite 21-22 August)
- Official site: velomediane.com; official registration: SQM Time (sqmtime.com/fr-velomediane)

## Verified Course Facts (2026)
- **Distance**: canonical advertised Grand Challenge distance is 165 km/102.5 mi (registration/practical pages); the organizer's own detailed turn-by-turn itinerary and RouteYou both put the actual mapped route at 167 km/103.8 mi. The previously-filed 165.8 km has no exact organizer source — use 165 km as the marketed figure with a 167 km itinerary note.
- **Elevation**: the organizer's own 2026 claim is 3,570 m/11,713 ft for the Grand Challenge — NOT a unit error, this is deliberate. However there's a genuine measurement discrepancy: RouteYou's calculation of the same 167 km organizer-linked trace gives 3,121 m, and a 2024 participant ride report recorded ~3,200 m over 171 km. The 18 named climbs (Mur de la Vélomédiane, Saint-Quoilin, Parc à Gibier, Haussire, Roche-à-Frêne, Rideux Nord, etc.) make 3,570 m plausible but not independently confirmed — present as "organizer-stated," not surveyed.
- **Route options for 2026**: Grand Challenge 165 km (€34 + €5 onsite), Challenge 134 km (€32 + €5 onsite), Gravel 86 km (€26 + €5 onsite), Petite Crique — distance itself is inconsistent across the organizer's own pages: registration lists 93 km, the detailed Petite Crique page says 96 km/1,800 m, the homepage says 93 km/1,910 m (€20 + €3 onsite). This is a real organizer-side inconsistency, not resolved by this research — note it rather than pick one silently.
- **Route (2026 Grand Challenge)**: La Roche-en-Ardenne loop through Vecmont, Ortho, Warempage, Wibrin, Chabrehez, Maboge, back through La Roche, then Samrée, Manhay, Roche-à-Frêne, Rideux, Fisenne, Beffe, Dochamps, finishing in La Roche
- **Surface**: Grand Challenge/Challenge/Petite Crique are paved public roads (RouteYou classifies as road cycling); the 86 km Gravel option is separate and partly GPX-navigated unpaved route
- **Field size**: no organizer-published 2026 cap or count found; "Not specified" is the safe value. A third-party calendar claims a 4,000-rider max (unconfirmed by organizer). Most recent actual reported turnout: ~1,600 riders across the whole 2024 event (TV Lux).
- **Format**: technically a timed randonnée under the post-2019 regulatory format — riders get a time and diploma, but results are published alphabetically, not as an official race classification. Open to all cyclists 18+, mass/rolling starts.

## Route-Option History (not a rebrand — the same event, evolving distances)
- 1989 original: ~170 km / 3,300 m / 20+ climbs
- 2017-2018: still ~170 km / 3,300 m, plus a ~95 km Petite Crique
- 2022: long/medium road courses ~173 km / ~133 km (medium built from long-route shortcuts)
- 2023: organizer's gravel-route archive documents an 86 km gravel course
- 2025 (per a Belgian club calendar): 172 km/3,570 m, 138 km/2,830 m, 96 km/1,800 m, gravel 86 km/1,950 m
- 2026: 165 km, 134 km, 93 km, 86 km per registration (see distance-inconsistency note above)
- "Pinarello Challenge/Gravel" sponsor label has changed to "Wallifornia.cc Challenge/Gravel" on current organizer pages — an option-level sponsor-name change, not a rebrand of the event itself

## Identity Check
Confirmed genuine mass-participation cyclosportive/fondo, NOT a professional race and NOT motorsport. It is explicitly distinct from the Grand Prix Criquiélion, a separate professional UCI 1.1 Men Elite race held in Ath/Lessines (2026 edition ran 24 May) — same namesake, different event, different format, different date/location. No motorsport or virtual-event conflation found.

## Dedupe determination
No separate duplicate profile exists in the corpus for this event under a different name or the professional Grand Prix Criquiélion (which is out of scope for this road-cycling-fondo database regardless, being a UCI pro race). No dedupe action needed.

# Research Dump: L'Eroica

## Quick Facts
- 210.9km / 131mi (long route), 3,873m / 12,707ft climbing, ~104km on strade bianche (white roads)
- Route options:
  - Leisure "Valle del Chianti" (45.1km/28mi, 755m/2,477ft, 17.5km white road, 1 refreshment stop, departs 08:00-09:30)
  - Short "Gallo Nero" (80.7km/50.1mi, 1,612m/5,289ft, 29km white road, 2 refreshment stops, departs 08:00-09:30)
  - Hundred "Val d'Arbia" (106.3km/66mi, 1,795m/5,889ft, 40.5-44.1km white road, 3 refreshment stops, departs 07:00-07:30)
  - Medium "Crete Senesi" (132.5km/82.3mi, 2,327m/7,635ft, 51.7km white road, 4 refreshment stops, departs 06:00-07:00)
  - Long "L'Eroico" (210.9km/131mi, 3,873m/12,707ft, 104.3km white road, 6 refreshment stops, departs 04:30-06:00)
- Start/Finish: Gaiole in Chianti, Province of Siena, Tuscany, Italy
- First weekend of October annually. Saturday for long/medium routes, Sunday for shorter routes
- 2025 edition: October 4-5. 2026 edition: Saturday October 3 / Sunday October 4 (confirmed by official regulations)
- ~9,000 riders in 2025 edition (grown from 92 riders in 1997 first edition)
- International participation from dozens of countries worldwide
- Founded 1997 by Giancarlo Brocci
- Entry fee: EUR 80 (Ciclo Club Eroica members), EUR 40 (children 13-17), EUR 90 (first 1,000 "Privilege and Friend Sport Team" spots), standard late registration available after early bird sells out. Registration opens December 2025 for 2026 edition
- Non-competitive start format, staggered departures by route (long route departs pre-dawn 04:30-06:00, shorter routes later morning)
- Mandatory vintage/vintage-style bicycle: steel frame, toe clips/straps on pedals, downtube-mounted gear shifters, wheels with at least 32 spokes and low-profile rims, external cable routing. Bikes must be built before 1987 or be newly-built vintage-style replicas conforming to same standards. Vintage or historically-inspired cycling clothing required (wool jerseys encouraged). Helmets required. Medical certificate required for routes 106km+ (competitive cycling certificate) or non-competitive certificate for shorter routes. Bike rental available through official partners for those without a vintage bike.

## Course Profile

### Defining Feature: Strade Bianche (White Roads)
The strade bianche are THE signature element of L'Eroica. These are unpaved roads made of a clay-limestone mix called "galestro" that gives them their distinctive white/pale grey color. The full 209km route includes approximately 104km (nearly 50%) on white roads, with the remainder on asphalt. The white road surface is hard-packed but loose in dry conditions, creating clouds of white dust. When wet, the galestro clay becomes slippery and muddy. These roads wind through the Chianti, Crete Senesi, and Val d'Orcia regions, passing through vineyards, olive groves, and cypress-lined hills.

L'Eroica was founded partly to protect these historic roads from being paved over. The strade bianche later inspired the professional UCI WorldTour race "Strade Bianche" (first held in 2007 as "Monte Paschi Eroica"), which uses many of the same white road sectors.

### Key Sectors and Climbs
- **Castello di Brolio** — The long route begins with a torch-lit pre-dawn departure past this iconic castle, seat of the Ricasoli wine dynasty. Dramatic climb on white roads with views across the Chianti valley.
- **Monte Sante Marie** — The most famous sector, shared with the professional Strade Bianche race. An 11.5km white road sector climbing from 172m to 329m with relentless rollers. Average gradient modest overall but with steep pitches. On the medium and long routes, this is where the challenge truly begins. The Strava segment shows 553m total elevation gain over the full sector due to undulations.
- **Buonconvento** — Medieval village along the Via Cassia, certified as one of "the most beautiful villages in Italy." Key stop on medium and long routes.
- **Crete Senesi** — The lunar-like landscape south of Siena, characterized by clay hills (biancane) and deep gullies (calanchi). The rolling white roads through this zone are among the most scenic and demanding.
- **Val d'Orcia** — UNESCO World Heritage landscape. The long route passes through this zone with views of isolated farmhouses, cypress rows, and the hilltop town of Montalcino.
- **Radda in Chianti** — Historic village on the short route, with an official food stop and town shops welcoming riders.
- **Lamole** — Famous food stop on the short route where "traditionally dressed Tuscan ladies offer homemade ribollita, local cold cuts and rivers of wine."

### Terrain Character
The route is characterized by continuous rolling terrain through the Sienese hills, with no single massive mountain pass but rather relentless undulations on mixed surfaces. Elevations range from roughly 150m in the valleys to approximately 500-600m on the hilltops. The difficulty comes not from extreme gradients but from the combination of distance, elevation accumulation, rough white road surfaces, and the limitations of vintage equipment (no modern gearing, no clipless pedals). The long route averages 15 hours of riding, with many finishers arriving after 10pm.

### Road Status
Roads are not fully closed to traffic. The event is classified as a "ciclostorica" (historical cycling event) and is non-competitive. Riders share some roads with local traffic, though major white road sectors are effectively car-free due to their nature.

## Feed Zones (Ristori)
The refreshment stops are a legendary aspect of L'Eroica and a major draw in themselves. They serve authentic Tuscan food and local wine, not energy gels. Reported offerings include:
- Homemade ribollita (traditional Tuscan bread soup)
- Local salumi and cold cuts
- Pecorino cheese
- Crostini
- Tuscan bread with olive oil
- Wholemeal organic spelt tart (Buonconvento stop)
- Organic food from local producers
- Local Chianti Classico wine poured freely
- Brunello di Montalcino (on routes passing through Val d'Orcia)
- Coffee
- Water and soft drinks

The number of stops varies by route: 1 stop on the leisure route, up to 6 on the long route. These are not quick fuel-and-go operations but convivial gatherings where riders stop, eat, drink, and socialize, often for extended periods.

## Logistics
- **Nearest airports**: Florence (FLR, Amerigo Vespucci, ~75km/1-1.5hr drive), Pisa (PSA, Galileo Galilei, ~150km/2hr drive, more international connections), Rome Fiumicino (FCO, ~270km/3hr drive), Bologna (BLQ, ~200km/2.5hr drive)
- **Nearest train station**: Siena (30km from Gaiole), then local bus or taxi
- **Lodging**: Gaiole in Chianti is a small village (~2,800 population) with very limited accommodation. Book many months in advance. Options include agriturismos (farm stays), B&Bs, and small hotels in Gaiole and surrounding villages (Radda in Chianti, Castelnuovo Berardenga, Castellina in Chianti). Official camping area available. Official tour operator packages from EUR 490 (4 days/3 nights including registration, through Terra Eroica/C-Way). Premium guided tour packages from EUR 1,899-3,180+ (5-8 days including prep rides, wine tastings, olive oil tastings). Independent agriturismo/B&B rates roughly EUR 80-200/night in season. Siena hotels are 30km away but offer more availability (EUR 100-300+/night).
- **Race-day transport**: Limited. Gaiole is a small hill town with narrow streets. Most riders stay in nearby accommodation and ride to the start. No large-scale shuttle system like major sportives. Parking available in designated areas around Gaiole.
- **Start area logistics**: Bib pickup at the event secretariat in Gaiole. Bike checks to verify vintage compliance. Pre-dawn departure (04:30) for the long route means riders must be ready in darkness. Torch-lit departure past Castello di Brolio is a signature moment.
- **No cutoff times listed** in official materials — the event is non-competitive and riders are expected to be self-sufficient. Long-route riders commonly finish after 10pm (15+ hour ride).

## History
- **1997**: Founded by Giancarlo Brocci in Gaiole in Chianti with 92 riders — "hunters of feelings and emotions" as Brocci called them. Created to celebrate the heritage of classic Italian cycling (the era of Coppi and Bartali) and to protect Tuscany's strade bianche from being paved over.
- **Origin story**: Brocci, passionate about the values of past cycling, wanted to reconnect people to the heritage that inspired Italian history, literature, culture, and music. The motto: "We want people to rediscover the beauty of fatigue and the thrill of conquest."
- **Rapid growth**: From 92 riders in 1997 to thousands within a decade, to 9,000+ participants by 2025.
- **2007**: The success of L'Eroica inspired the creation of a professional race on the same white roads. The first edition of "Monte Paschi Eroica" (now known as Strade Bianche) was held in October 2007, won by Alexander Kolobnev. This professional race eventually moved to early March and became a UCI WorldTour event, now considered cycling's unofficial "sixth Monument." Fabian Cancellara holds the men's record with 3 wins; Tadej Pogacar has also won 3 times and had a sector named after him in 2026.
- **Global expansion**: The Eroica brand has expanded to multiple countries:
  - Classic events: Eroica Japan, Eroica Montalcino, Eroica Valkenburg (Netherlands/Limburg), Eroica Germania, Eroica Dolomiti, Eroica California (Cambria/Paso Robles), Eroica Cuba, Eroica Hispania, Eroica South Africa (Montagu/Cape Winelands)
  - Nova events (modern bikes allowed): Nova Eroica Prosecco Hills, Nova Eroica Gran Sasso, Nova Eroica Buonconvento, Nova Eroica Ivrea, Nova Eroica Istria, Nova Eroica Switzerland, Nova Eroica California, Nova Eroica South Africa
- **Eroica Bike Register**: Official register of vintage bicycles built before 1987, cataloguing Racing, Travel, City, Sport, Cyclocross, and Folding bicycles that have reached the present in an appreciable state of conservation or restoration.
- **Permanent route**: A 207-209km permanent route through the Siena area (Chianti, Crete Senesi, Val d'Orcia) divided into 12 stages/sectors can be ridden year-round with any type of bike, with variants for further exploration.
- **Ciclo Club Eroica (CCE)**: Official membership club with personal bib numbers and priority registration.
- **Notable editions**: The event has run every year since 1997 without interruption, making the 2026 edition the 30th anniversary edition.

## Climate (October)
- **Temperature**: Daytime highs typically 18-22C (64-72F), overnight/dawn lows 8-12C (46-54F). Long-route riders depart at 04:30 in near-darkness; temperatures at that hour can be in single digits Celsius.
- **Precipitation**: October is one of the wetter months in Tuscany. Siena area averages approximately 110mm of rainfall over ~12 rain days in October. Roughly 24% chance of rain on any given day, though some sources cite higher probability.
- **Sunshine**: Moderate — approximately 180 hours of sunshine in October in the Siena area. Daylight approximately 11 hours (sunrise ~07:25, sunset ~18:30).
- **Humidity**: Typically 70-80%.
- **Dust conditions**: In dry weather, the white roads generate significant clouds of white dust (galestro clay). This is part of the atmosphere but coats riders, bikes, and lungs. In wet conditions, the same clay becomes slippery mud that can clog drivetrains and brakes — especially problematic on vintage equipment with rim brakes and friction shifters.
- **Fog**: Possible in early morning in the valleys, adding to the atmosphere of the pre-dawn long-route departure.
- **Recommendation**: Layering essential. Arm warmers, vest, and rain jacket advisable. Long-route riders experience a full range from cold pre-dawn temperatures to midday warmth and potentially cold again in evening.

## Community & Culture

### The Atmosphere
L'Eroica is widely described not as a race but as a pilgrimage, a celebration, and a living museum of cycling culture. The atmosphere is fundamentally different from modern sportives — the emphasis is on conviviality, historical appreciation, and the experience of effort rather than competition or time.

### Vintage Culture
- Participants dress in wool jerseys, leather hairnet helmets (though modern helmets are now required), vintage sunglasses, and period-appropriate clothing
- Bikes must meet strict vintage specifications (pre-1987 or compliant replicas)
- The bike jumble/marketplace in Gaiole is legendary, with vendors selling vintage components, frames, jerseys, and accessories
- "Concorso d'Eleganza" — elegance contest for the best-dressed riders and most beautiful vintage bikes

### Food and Wine
Food and wine are absolutely central to the experience, not peripheral. The refreshment stops are the heart of the event. Local restaurants participate in the "Piatto Eroico" (Heroic Dish) initiative, creating special menus. The event culminates in a large communal dinner/pasta party. The Confcommercio Siena organizes "Eroica Shop Windows" where local businesses in Gaiole, Radda, and Siena decorate to welcome the event.

### The Festival
L'Eroica weekend includes far more than just riding:
- Vintage bicycle marketplace and swap meet
- Live music
- Concorso d'Eleganza (best vintage bike/outfit)
- Communal dinners in the village
- Wine tastings from local producers (Chianti Classico, Brunello di Montalcino)
- Eroica Shop Windows throughout the village

### International Participation
The event draws riders from across Europe, North America, Asia, and beyond. The 2025 edition had 9,000 participants, making it one of the largest vintage cycling events in the world. The website is available in Italian, English, French, German, Spanish, Dutch, Japanese, and Chinese, reflecting the global reach.

### Media Coverage
- Il Sole 24 Ore: "The most classic of cycle races... a brand that has now spread from Tuscany all over the world"
- CyclingUK: "the world's premier event for lovers of old-school cycling"
- The Guardian: "A classic cycle ride in Italy's Chianti country"
- RAI (Italian national broadcaster) coverage
- Extensive international cycling media coverage (Rouleur, Cycling Weekly, PezCycling News)
- Official social media presence with large following

### The "L'Eroica" Brand Expansion
From a single event in Tuscany, L'Eroica has become a global brand and movement:
- 10+ "Classic" Eroica events worldwide (vintage bikes only)
- 10+ "Nova" Eroica events (modern bikes permitted, gravel-focused)
- Events on 4 continents (Europe, North America, Asia, Africa)
- Eroica Caffe (branded cafe concept)
- Official merchandise shop
- Terra Eroica (tourism/permanent routes organization)
- Ciclo Club Eroica (membership club)
- The brand has become synonymous with a philosophy of cycling that values heritage, effort, community, and simplicity over technology and competition

### Strade Bianche Pro Race Connection
L'Eroica directly inspired the creation of the professional Strade Bianche race in 2007, initially called "Monte Paschi Eroica." The pro race uses many of the same white road sectors around Siena and the Crete Senesi. Strade Bianche has since become one of the most prestigious one-day races in professional cycling, reaching UCI WorldTour status in 2017. It is frequently called "Europe's southernmost northern classic" and is informally considered cycling's "sixth Monument."

## Claim: "World Championship wall" (tagline; terrain.features: Via Salviati)
- **Verified true.** The Via Salviati climb — a short (~600m), steep (avg ~10-12%, pitches to 16%) ramp in Florence — was the decisive climb of the 2013 UCI Road World Championships Elite Men's road race, taken on each of 10 laps of the Florence–Fiesole circuit. Rui Costa (Portugal) won; Alejandro Valverde and Joaquim "Purito" Rodríguez were among the contenders who fought on the climb.
- Sources: https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-road-world-championships-2013/elite-men-road-race/live-report/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_UCI_Road_World_Championships ; https://inrng.com/2013/09/world-championships-firenze-2013-preview/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_UCI_Road_World_Championships_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_road_race
- The profile's own claim that "Rui Costa won the 2013 World Championship road race" on Via Salviati (biased_opinion.summary) is accurate.
- Minor gradient variance note (not corrected — not a fabrication flag): independent sources give Via Salviati's average as ~10-12% with pitches to 16%; the profile states "12% average, maximum 20%." Both describe the same real, famously steep short ramp; the discrepancy is within normal source-to-source variance for short urban climbs and doesn't change the underlying "World Championship wall" claim, which is the audited claim.

# Peaks and Plains — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Identity check
Confirmed as the Magdeburg, Germany ultracycling event at peaksandplains.cc, organized
by freshpepper GmbH & Co. KG. NOT the UK Peaks & Plains Housing Trust (peaksplains.org),
the UK "Peaks and Plains" swimming league, or Colorado's former Peaks-to-Plains Trail.

## Status
Active. 2024, 2025, 2026 editions held; 2027 (May 28-30) already announced with
registration opening in October, switching to a lottery after 2026 sold out in ~2 minutes.

## Vitals
- 2026 date: May 29 18:00 start through May 31 18:00 (48-hour limit)
- Location: Magdeburg start/finish, loop through the Harz Mountains and Magdeburger Börde
- Advertised: 555km / 7,300m (organizer spec, retained in profile)
- Actual ridden-course reporting: ~550km/7,200m per post-race accounts (ROADBIKE, rider reports)
- Field: ~150 starters 2026 (126 solo reported + pair-category riders; no official split found)

## Format
Timed, competitive, unsupported ultra-distance road event/race — solo or 2-person pair
categories, GPX route, mandatory GPS trackers, disqualification for shortcuts/outside
assistance, no drafting outside registered pairs. Recorded placings + named winner
(Sebastian Trimborn, 2026) confirm competitive format, not brevet-style homologation.

## Organizer/history
freshpepper GmbH & Co. KG (Magdeburg), managing director Martin Hummelt. Ran the
regional CYCLE TOUR road program for a decade, ended it 2025, named Peaks & Plains a
continuing focus project. Debuted 2024 at ~500km/6,000m, 48-hour limit.

## Rider accounts (2026)
Published 2026 rider accounts (a finisher's LinkedIn account and the winner's
race report) separately describe storms, thunderstorms, heat, punctures,
navigation-equipment failure, and repeated mechanicals during the race.

## Criticisms
No scandal/results dispute found. Substantiated complaint: 2026 registration sold out
in ~2 minutes (r/ultracycling thread); field size too small for demand. Organizer
response: 2027 moves to a lottery.

1. **MAJOR — The document conflates two distinct plan-generation engines.**

   The white paper cites the custom pipeline’s discipline overlay as the mechanism behind Roadie plans. But the 40 road master plans are generated by `gravel-god-training-plans`, where variation is implemented through `demand_emphasis`, not `disciplines.road`.

   The master engine maps:

   - `all_rounder` toward Race Simulation and Torque
   - `climber` toward Threshold, Torque, and Durability
   - `ultra` toward Tempo, Threshold, Durability, and Endurance
   - `punchy` toward Anaerobic, Explosive, VO2max, and Race Simulation

   See [workout_generator.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/engine/workout_generator.py:535). The road base intakes map the four commercial variations onto those generic emphases, for example [compete-road-rolling-fast-12wk.json](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/athlete-intake-road-base/compete-road-rolling-fast-12wk.json:17).

   The reference needs an explicit “which engine does what” section. Otherwise an implementation claim verified against one engine can remain false for the shipped output of the other.

2. **MAJOR — Three of the four road overlay rows in §3.1 do not create the differentiation claimed.**

   In the custom selector:

   - Base `intensity_2`: the Road Tempo alternative is active, but it only widens a pool whose default is already `Tempo with Accelerations`. It does not replace Gravel SFR, and Road still receives generic SFR in `intensity_3`.
   - Build `intensity_1`: Road adds `Threshold Progressive`, but that workout is already a generic alternative.
   - Build `intensity_3`: Road adds `Blended VO2max and G Spot`, but that is already the generic default.
   - Race-prep `intensity_3`: the G-Spot overlay is genuinely inert because the generic slot has `default: null`.

   Config: [workout_selection.yaml](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/config/workout_selection.yaml:164). Generic build pools: [workout_selection.yaml](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/config/workout_selection.yaml:56). The selector skips a null slot before applying the discipline overlay: [workout_selector.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/scripts/workout_selector.py:154).

   The accurate description is: **Road currently has one effective pool expansion in base; two duplicate build additions; and one unreachable race-prep addition.**

3. **MAJOR — “Gravel → SFR / Road → Tempo” falsely reads as replacement logic.**

   Discipline additions are appended as rotating alternatives. They are not authoritative substitutions. Selection rotates through default plus alternatives according to block number, while duplicates are discarded: [workout_selector.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/scripts/workout_selector.py:159).

   The table in [ROADIE_COACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/road-race-automation/docs/ROADIE_COACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md:43) therefore overstates the strength and regularity of the Road bias.

4. **MAJOR — The methodology-selection description does not match the road masters.**

   The white paper says the engine chooses among four methodologies using hours, limiter, variation profile, and runway.

   The road master engine actually supports twelve methodologies: [methodology_selector.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/engine/methodology_selector.py:13). It considers realistic hours, experience, event demands, inferred limiter, and preferences, but not plan runway or road variation.

   More importantly, every current road base intake supplies a `methodology_override`. Overrides return immediately with a score of 999: [methodology_selector.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/engine/methodology_selector.py:182). The road catalog effectively uses:

   - Polarized for Finisher, Compete, Masters, and Stage Race
   - G-Spot for Time-Crunched

   Thus the road masters are not being methodologically selected from the athlete/variation profile. They are being pinned by product tier.

5. **MAJOR — The custom four-method selector also does not support the full claim.**

   The custom pipeline does choose among four methods, but its actual inputs are hours, history, stress, schedule, race demands, goals, and preferences: [select_methodology.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/scripts/select_methodology.py:448). The passed `derived` data is not used to score runway or the Roadie variation.

   Furthermore, the chosen methodology changes one protected secondary slot in the workout selector rather than defining the complete periodization model: [workout_selector.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/scripts/workout_selector.py:187).

   “Commits to one model” is too strong. “Applies one named methodology bias within a shared phase structure” is more accurate.

6. **MAJOR — The Testing Week claim describes a feedback loop that does not exist.**

   The document states that “every later zone prescription derives from numbers the athlete produced.”

   In the master engine, all weeks are generated in one pass. Testing Week is inserted, but there is no ingestion of its FTP, five-minute, sprint, fade, decoupling, or strength-screen results before subsequent weeks are built: [workout_generator.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/engine/workout_generator.py:717).

   The guide instead tells the athlete to update FTP manually: [training_guide_builder.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/scripts/training_guide_builder.py:960).

   Honest wording would be: **Week 1 gives the athlete data to update FTP and calibrate execution manually. The already-generated plan does not adapt itself to the complete test battery.**

9. **MAJOR — Heat is not currently applied “where race climate demands.”**

   The master generator adds heat cues based on plan week, without checking climate or race location: [workout_generator.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/engine/workout_generator.py:999). The generated guide recommends heat acclimation even for cold events: [training-guide.html](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/master_plans/finisher-road-all-rounder-f5e7/training-guide.html:693).

10. **MINOR — The four variations and 427 SKU mappings are real, but the mapping is much coarser than the prose implies.**

   All 427 SKUs are mapped:

   - Alpine Fondo: 176
   - All-Rounder: 153
   - Distance: 73
   - Rolling-Fast: 25

   However, the classifier is a first-match threshold cascade:

   1. Durability ≥ 7 → Distance
   2. Altitude or climbing ≥ 7 → Alpine
   3. VO2max ≥ 7 → Rolling-Fast
   4. Otherwise → All-Rounder

   See [assign_tp_skus.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/road-race-automation/scripts/assign_tp_skus.py:39).

   This means a long alpine event is classified Distance before Alpine, and an alpine race with strong stochastic demands becomes Alpine before Rolling-Fast. The document should disclose the priority rule rather than implying a nuanced multidimensional match.

11. **MINOR — “Shared 525-workout Durability library” is imprecise.**

   The current master library contains 524 `.zwo` files across ten categories, not a 525-workout Durability library. Durability is one category within that total. Category definitions are in [workout_library.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/engine/workout_library.py:23).

   The custom athlete pipeline describes a separate 100-archetype × six-duration system: [archetype_registry.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/scripts/archetype_registry.py:62).

   The document needs to name which library and counting method it means.

12. **MAJOR — The philosophy’s Road demand model is too generic and underspecified.**

   “Sustained climbs, repeated surges, pack dynamics, and pacing at higher average speeds” is not a sufficient road-racing demand analysis.

   A serious road coach will ask which event is being modeled:

   - Gran fondo or sportive completion
   - Competitive mass-start road race
   - Rolling breakaway race
   - Hill climb
   - Century/endurance ride
   - Stage race
   - Time trial
   - Criterium or sprint-finish race

   These differ in drafting exposure, stochasticity, coasting, aerodynamic position, cadence, W′ depletion, sprint reserve, tactical cost, and acceptable pacing error. The current product scope excludes crit and TT as dedicated plan types, yet the paper frequently speaks about “road racing” as a single physiology.

13. **MAJOR — Tempo is not a persuasive universal base-phase Road differentiator.**

   Tempo is useful for time-efficient aerobic work, sustained climbing, fatigue resistance, and some fondo demands. But it is not inherently more road-specific than endurance, cadence development, neuromuscular speed, or low-cost sprint exposure.

   Worse, the code does not actually perform a clean SFR-to-Tempo replacement. Road athletes still receive SFR through the generic third intensity slot. The philosophy is therefore both scientifically thin and inconsistent with its implementation.

   A stronger base rationale would prescribe mostly aerobic endurance while layering:

   - cadence range and leg speed
   - short, non-fatiguing sprint mechanics
   - position-specific tempo where warranted
   - progressive group-riding exposure
   - terrain-specific torque only for the appropriate variation

14. **MAJOR — Repeated 30–90 second road surges are named but not deliberately prescribed.**

   The active road overlay favors Tempo, progressive Threshold, Blended VO2/G-Spot, and an unreachable G-Spot race-prep workout. That is weak coverage of attacking, bridging, closing gaps, accelerating after corners, cresting climbs, and recovering while still riding hard.

   The existing library already contains better material, including:

   - Anaerobic: `Above-CP 90s Repeats`, `W-Prime Depletion`, `2min Killers`
   - VO2max: `Microbursts`, `Ronnestad 30-15`, `40-20`
   - Race Simulation: `Criss-Cross`, `Make the Break`, `Peak and Fade`
   - Sprint: `Attacks and Repeatability`
   - Durability: loaded-recovery and tired 30/30 sessions

   The problem is selection and tagging, not lack of workouts.

15. **MAJOR — Pack skills are treated as reading material rather than a trained progression.**

   The guide can explain pacelines and cornering, but the schedule does not ensure that an athlete practices:

   - holding a wheel safely
   - accelerating out of corners
   - moving within a group
   - crosswind positioning
   - taking a bottle under load
   - feeding without losing position
   - responding to a split
   - sprinting safely after fatigue

   These should appear as progressive outdoor notes or group-ride substitutions attached to actual weeks. A prose chapter alone is not a training intervention.

16. **MAJOR — TT/solo pacing, cadence, and sprint mechanics are important omissions despite available library support.**

   The library already includes useful families such as sustained Threshold, Kolie Moore TTE, TT Send, NP/IF Target, High Cadence, form sprints, sprint buildups, fatigued sprints, and Z2-plus-sprints. None is organized into a coherent road-specific progression.

   The document should either add these capabilities or clearly state that Roadie currently targets fondo/sportive completion and general road fitness—not time trials, crits, or sprint-finish preparation.

17. **MAJOR — Some current tactical advice is actively wrong for competitive road racing.**

   The generated guide advises that if a group goes, the athlete should let it go and ride their power: [training-guide.html](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/gravel-god-training-plans/master_plans/finisher-road-all-rounder-f5e7/training-guide.html:783).

   That is often sensible for fondo completion. It can be race-losing advice in a mass-start road race when the decisive split forms.

   The guide needs separate tactical registers:

   - Fondo/sportive: pacing discipline, drafting economy, fueling, controlled surges
   - Competitive road race: positioning, split recognition, bridging decisions, conserving in the bunch, final selection
   - Solo/TT: aerodynamic pacing and power control
   - Hill climb: maximal sustainable pacing with terrain-dependent allocation

18. **MINOR — The zone and intensity doctrine needs definition and reconciliation.**

   The custom guide defines a modified FTP model that includes G-Spot: [training_guide_builder.py](/Users/mattirowe/Documents/GravelGod/athlete-custom-training-plan-pipeline/athletes/scripts/training_guide_builder.py:930). Elsewhere it characterizes Zone 3 as a harmful gray zone while the Road philosophy deliberately increases Tempo exposure.

   The reference needs to specify:

   - zone boundaries and governing metric
   - whether prescriptions are FTP, LTHR, HRmax, or RPE based
   - how G-Spot relates to conventional Coggan zones
   - whether “80/20” means time in zone, sessions, or intended workout classification
   - how warmups, recovery intervals, and mixed workouts are counted
   - when Tempo is intentional versus accidental gray-zone accumulation

19. **MINOR — Some claims do survive the audit.**

   The document is correct that:

   - four road variations exist across the 40-plan tier/duration matrix
   - all 427 mapped SKUs receive a variation
   - the race-prep third-slot Road overlay is inert
   - the standard Road TP configuration omits the named mental-program merchandising tile
   - the road catalog uses the shared workout assets rather than a separate road workout library

   These should remain, but with precise engine and output-path attribution.

## Missing reference-document material

The white paper needs:

1. An architecture and source-of-truth section separating the custom pipeline, master-plan engine, TP SKU mapping, guide generators, and shipped artifacts.

2. A zone-model appendix defining boundaries, metrics, G-Spot, distribution accounting, and manual FTP updates.

3. A concrete tier matrix showing scheduled hours, realistic hours, key days, strength frequency, recovery cadence, long-ride caps, methodology overrides, and available durations.

4. A variation matrix showing classifier thresholds, precedence, SKU counts, `demand_emphasis` values, target workout families, and unsupported event formats.

5. A testing feedback-loop description specifying which results alter the plan, which require athlete action, and which are currently informational only.

6. A supported-scope statement: fondo, sportive, century, hill climb, stage race, competitive road race, TT, crit, and sprint finish should each be explicitly supported or excluded.

7. Coach-level FAQ answers for missed workouts, group-ride substitution, outdoor execution, no-power athletes, retesting, illness, travel, taper strength, and solo versus bunch tactics.

8. Evidence and safety notes for heat, women-specific guidance, masters recovery, strength frequency, and absolute “gray-zone” claims.

## Five highest-leverage improvements

1. **Separate the two engines and rewrite every implementation claim against the shipped artifact.** Immediately correct methodology selection, Testing Week adaptation, tier hours, guide-section availability, heat gating, and library count.

2. **Replace the duplicate/inert Road overlays with variation-specific active pools.** The existing workout library already contains the surge, sprint, TT, cadence, climbing, and durability sessions needed.

3. **Calendarize road specificity.** Add phase- and variation-aware notes for group riding, corner accelerations, feeding, crosswinds, cadence, positioning, and safe sprint mechanics instead of leaving those subjects in guide prose.

4. **Define the actual product scope and split tactics by event format.** Fondo completion advice should not masquerade as universal competitive road-racing strategy.

5. **Add a technical reference appendix for zones, tier load, variation classification, methodology overrides, and the testing feedback loop.** This would make the white paper auditable by a coach instead of merely persuasive to a buyer.

## Overall verdict

The white paper has a defensible core idea—shared physiology plus road-specific demand overlays—but it is not yet an intellectually reliable reference document.

Its largest problem is architectural: it describes the custom athlete pipeline and the prebuilt road-master engine as if they were one system. That makes several statements technically true somewhere in the repositories but false for the products currently generated from the road intake matrix.

13. **MAJOR — Tempo is not a persuasive universal base-phase Road differentiator.**

   - terrain-specific torque only for the appropriate variation

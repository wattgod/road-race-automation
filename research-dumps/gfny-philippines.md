## Most recent confirmed edition: Iloilo City, September 22, 2019 (GFNY Asian Championship)
- 133km long course, 650m climbing; 64km medium course, 220m climbing (timed, non-competitive). Started Iloilo Business Park/Festive Walk, crossed 14 municipalities + 2 cities. 300+ riders from 15 countries, ~50 local. Winner Romeo Camingao, 3:36:34.
- Source: https://www.iloilotoday.com/iloilo-gfny-asian-championship/, https://www.sunstar.com.ph/iloilo/barber-from-marikina-wins-gfny-asia-championship, https://philippines.gfny.com/results/

## Earlier 2019 edition: Bohol, January 27, 2019
- ~140km long course, ~1,554m climbing; 66km/554m short course. Well-paved backroads; police/medical/mechanical support, 6 aid stations. Philippine Tourism Promotions Board records it completed (dates 26-28 Jan 2019); Bohol had originally been selected as a multi-year venue through 2021 but subsequent editions did not happen.
- Source: https://boholnewsdaily.com/2019/01/page/2, https://tpb.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/1Q-Accomplishment-Report-2019.pdf

## Format verdict (historical)
When it existed, GFNY Philippines was a genuine mass-participation road gran fondo (mass start, chip timing, age-group competition on the long course, timed non-competitive medium course) — not an ultra, travel product, virtual challenge, or MTB/gravel event. It behaved as a GFNY country-chapter/franchise that moved location between its two known editions (Bohol -> Iloilo) rather than a single fixed annual race — that instability, combined with six years of silence since 2019, supports a defunct/discontinued determination per the birkebeinerrittet-road.json precedent.

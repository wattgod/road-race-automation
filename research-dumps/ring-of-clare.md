# Research Dump: Škoda Ring of Clare Cycle

## Quick Facts
- Location: Ennis / Clarecastle, County Clare, Ireland. Start/finish hosted by Clarecastle GAA Club.
- Status: amateur mass-participation leisure sportive, part of the Škoda Celtic Cycle Series (Škoda is title/commercial sponsor). Not UCI-sanctioned. Entry requires Cycling Ireland membership or a one-day leisure licence for insurance.
- 2026 date: NOT confirmed as of research date. Škoda's own cycling content (welovecycling.com, 28 Aug 2025) says the event "returns in July 2026" but gives no exact date. The official site (ringofclare.ie) still displays 2025 event info with closed registration. "July 18, 2026" appears only in third-party calendars; Gran Fondo Guide explicitly marks its 2026 listing "Unconfirmed."
- Routes: two options, officially 160 km and 120 km (not one distance). Organizer does not publish dependable ascent totals. Independently mapped GPX tracks: 160 km route measured at 165 km/1,611 m by one tool and 169.6 km/1,855 m by another; 120 km route measured at 119.7 km/1,272 m. A third-party overview estimates roughly 1,600 m / 1,200 m for the two routes respectively.
- Field size: over 2,000 riders in 2025 (Clare Live, 24 Jul 2025; corroborated by Škoda's own recap). No defensible 2026 number exists yet.
- Organizer: Škoda Celtic Cycle Series; Mark O'Connell identified as series organizer (Eventmaster testimonial page). Clarecastle GAA Club hosts start/finish and runs its own event-day BBQ/fundraising. Riders can fundraise for a range of charities via Eventmaster — not a single-charity ride. 2025 Eventmaster fundraising campaign raised €12,715.
- Course highlights: The Hand climb near Kilmaley (~1.9 km at 4.2% average, ramps to ~9.6%) as the first real test, then Doonbeg/Kilkee and the Wild Atlantic Way, Loop Head on the long route, Kilrush, and rolling/headwind-exposed roads along the Shannon Estuary on the return.
- No substantiated widespread 2024-25 organizational complaints found. Main issue is information quality: stale 2025 pages on the official site, at least one 2024/2025 date typo, and one Škoda-published article that mislabeled the short route's distance as "100 km" (it's 120 km). Older (2018) rider feedback complained about registration/BBQ queues but is too dated to characterize current organization.

## Identity resolution (this profile was contaminated with a different ride)

The prior profile's `course_description` and every `youtube_data` video/quote/rider_intel entry
described a ~130km remote gravel ride over **Prince Alfred's Pass** and the **Seven Passes road**
(Cape Epic-adjacent backcountry terrain north of Knysna) — captured in a first-person CADE Media
vlog and a separate motorbike tour of the same pass. That is a real place, but a completely
different, unrelated ride roughly 90 minutes from Knysna proper.

The **actual Knysna Cycle Tour** is a paved 102km road race run out of the Knysna Oyster
Festival grounds, organized by the Rotary Club of Knysna, going back to 1987. It is NOT a
gravel event and does not touch Prince Alfred's Pass.

Other name-adjacent events checked and confirmed separate:
- **Cape Town Cycle Tour** — separate event, 109km, Cape Town (cycletour.org.za)
- **Lions Karoo to Coast** — separate off-road event, Uniondale-to-Knysna via Prince Alfred's
  Pass, ~95.95km, held in September (karootocoast.com) — this is actually the real-world event
  the contaminated gravel video material was describing
- **Simola Hill/Knysna Enduro** — the MTB/enduro portion of the same festival weekend, distinct
  from the 102km road race

## Course

Paved road race. ~6km flat through town, then Kyters Nek (~2.5km climb), out toward Buffalo Bay
(two steeper coastal climbs), past Groen Vlei toward Sedgefield, then the long Barrington Hill
climb, back to Knysna on the undulating N2.

## History

Founded 1987 as a winter-tourism draw for Knysna; Rotary Club of Knysna took full control
1993-94, moved it into a trust structure 2006-07 for liability/tax reasons. Anchor cycling
event of the Knysna Oyster Festival.

## Credible criticisms

- 2016 taxi hit-and-run: a taxi entered the temporary cycling lane on the N2 and struck a rider
  (minor injuries) — historical incident, not evidence of current unsafety
- Strict entry terms: no refunds even for organizer-side postponement, route changes, or
  weather/force-majeure cancellation
- Some listing pages have printed conflicting info (wrong day, "no timing" claims) that the
  organizer's own site and the completed results contradict

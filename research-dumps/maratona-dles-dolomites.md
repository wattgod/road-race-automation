# Research Dump: Maratona dles Dolomites

## Quick Facts
- 138km / 85.7mi, 4,230m / 13,878ft climbing, 7 Dolomite passes
- Route options: Sellaronda (55km/1,780m), Medio (106km/3,130m), Maratona (138km/4,230m)
- Start: La Villa, Alta Badia → Finish: Corvara, South Tyrol, Italy
- First Sunday in July (39th edition: July 5, 2026)
- ~8,000-9,000 riders from ~30,000 applicants, 81 countries, lottery entry
- Founded 1987 (166 riders first edition)
- EUR 180 entry fee
- Wave start in 4 groups (A/B/C/D by qualifying time)
- Mandatory rain jacket (no vests), helmet, medical certificate
- Course record: 4:23:31 (Negrini, 2006)

## The 7 Passes (Full 138km Course)
1. **Campolongo** (1,875m) — 5.8km, 353m gain, 6.1% avg, 11% max. Cat 2. Warmup climb, repeated at km 55.
2. **Pordoi** (2,239m) — 9.2km, 638m gain, 6.9% avg, 10% max. Cat 1. Fausto Coppi monument. Highest paved Dolomite pass.
3. **Sella** (2,244m) — 5.5km, 436m gain, 7.9% avg, 12% max. Cat 1. Highest point on course. Steepest Sellaronda pass.
4. **Gardena** (2,121m) — 5.8km, 250m gain, 4.3% avg, 10% max. Cat 3. Easiest pass, active recovery before Giau.
5. **Giau** (2,236m) — 9.9km, 922m gain, 9.3% avg, 15% max. HC. THE queen stage. 29 hairpins. Decisive.
6. **Falzarego** (2,105m) — Part of combined climb. 5.8% avg from Pocol. WWI battlefield.
7. **Valparola** (2,200m) — Final summit. 83m from Falzarego to Valparola. WWII museum.
- **Mur dl Giat** — 370m at 12.4% avg, 19% max. Final sting before Corvara finish.

## Logistics
- Airports: Bolzano (BZO, 1.5hr, limited flights), Innsbruck (INN, 2hr), Venice (VCE, 3hr, best for international)
- Lodging: Book 1 year ahead. Hotels prefer full-week bookings. Tour operator packages EUR 1,399-4,399+.
- Race day transport: Free shuttle buses across Alta Badia villages during Rider's Week.
- Roads fully closed to all motorized vehicles.
- 1,500+ volunteers, Green Angels (mechanics on e-bikes), Audi support cars.

## Feed Zones
- Multiple stations on/near each major pass. Serving: Enervit gels/bars, Dole fruit, Marlene apples, Segafredo coffee, Birra Dolomiti beer, Loacker biscuits, Giovanni Rana tortellini, Speck Alto Adige, local strudel, Yoga juices, Cantine Maschio prosecco at finish.

## Climate (July)
- Valley 20-28°C, summits can be 8-15°C lower. Dawn start potentially single digits.
- 63% rain chance. Thunderstorm risk HIGH (afternoon storms common).
- Mandatory rain jacket enforced. 1989 edition shortened by ice.

## History & Culture
- Founded 1987 celebrating Cycling Club Alta Badia 10th anniversary.
- Named in Ladin language (minority Alpine culture). Each edition has a theme (2026: PAX).
- From 166 riders (1987) to 8,000+ from 32,700 applicants (2025).
- Lottery introduced 2005 due to overwhelming demand.
- Bradley Wiggins rode 2017 edition.
- National Geographic: "one of the biggest, most passionate, and most chaotic bike races on Earth."
- UNESCO Dolomites World Heritage Site.
- Post-race: pasta party with prosecco, Rider's Week village, awards in ice stadium.

# Gran Fondo Montevideo — Editorial Wave 9 Identity Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Identity resolution (highest-risk item this wave)

1. **Different events confirmed.** The UCI-series URL (ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/montevideo/)
   belongs to **Gran Fondo Asociación Española**, not Gran Fondo Movistar. The UCI page
   explicitly names "Granfondo Asociacion Espanola" and describes its 130 km
   Montevideo-Canelones course.

   **Gran Fondo Movistar Montevideo** is a separate April race with its own website,
   branding, and results (granfondomovistar.uy). Its 2025 edition was 99.4 km/56.4 km.
   Asociación Española's race is held in September with longer routes.

2. **"Gran Fondo Española" and "Gran Fondo Asociación Española" are the same event** — the
   official site (espanolagranfondo.uy) uses the shortened title "Gran Fondo Española" while
   identifying the event itself as "Gran Fondo Asociación Española." Not a Movistar
   sponsor-name phase; the Asociación Española-branded race.

   Identity rule:
   - ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/montevideo/ -> Gran Fondo Asociación Española
   - granfondomovistar.uy/...montevideo/ and Movistar footage -> Gran Fondo Movistar Montevideo (different event, NOT this profile)
   - "Gran Fondo Española" -> shortened alias of Gran Fondo Asociación Española

3. **2026 status.** A 2026 Gran Fondo Asociación Española is confirmed by the organizer for
   12-13 September 2026, main race Sunday 13 September 08:00. Published distances: 138 km
   Gran Fondo and 60.7 km Medio Fondo. Registration live via TicketFácil
   (https://ticketfacil.uy/event/Gran-Fondo-Asociacion-Espaola/registerToEvent) from the
   official site (https://espanolagranfondo.uy/).

   Caveat: Montevideo is NOT presently listed in the UCI's published 2025-26 or 2026-27
   qualifier calendar (ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/calendar/). The September race is
   confirmed by the organizer, but its 2026 UCI-qualifier status is not currently confirmed
   by the UCI calendar itself. The /montevideo/ page appears to describe the 2025 UCI
   edition.

   For Gran Fondo Movistar Montevideo, its official page still exposes the 2025
   edition/results; no official 2026 registration or results page found.

4. **Distance/elevation.** The 130 km / 512 m record is credible for the 2025 UCI Asociación
   Española course (highest point only 74 m — flat and fast with modest rolling terrain, not
   a climbing gran fondo, but not a purely flat coastal out-and-back either): only the first
   2 km along the coast, then inland through Barros Blancos, Santa Rosa, San Jacinto, a short
   categorized climb around km 71, then ~30 km near the sea toward the finish.

   For 2026, the official distance changed to 138 km with a new Garmin course link; no
   checkable official ascent figure published yet for the new route.

# Research Dump: Cadel Evans Great Ocean Road Ride (TAC People's Ride)

## Quick Facts
- **Flagship distance**: 124km / 77mi (The Cadel Classic), ~1,200-1,500m / 3,900-4,900ft climbing
- **Route options**: 124km (The Cadel Classic), 59-63km (Cadel's Hometown), 35km (short course added 2026), plus free GeelongPort Family Ride (~10km, Friday evening)
- **Start/Finish**: Steampacket Gardens, Eastern Beach Road, Geelong Waterfront, Victoria, Australia
- **Date**: Late January / early February (Saturday of race weekend; pro races Sun). 2025: Sat 1 Feb. 2026: Sat 31 Jan.
- **Field size**: ~3,000 riders (both 2025 and 2026 reported "nearly 3,000")
- **Founded**: 2015 — inaugural People's Ride held alongside Cadel Evans' farewell pro race
- **Entry fee**: ~$150-185 AUD (estimated; includes mandatory official Santini event jersey valued at $129.95 AUD RRP, chip timing, feed stations, mechanical support, sag wagon). Jersey must be worn on race day.
- **Start format**: Mass start from Geelong Waterfront. Long course departs 7:00 AM, short course 7:30 AM.
- **Discipline**: Gran fondo / sportive (recreational mass-participation ride on closed/traffic-managed roads)
- **Title sponsor**: Mapei (from 2026); previously TAC (Transport Accident Commission, Victoria)
- **UCI connection**: Held on the Saturday before the UCI WorldTour men's and women's professional races (Sunday)
- **Named for**: Cadel Evans — 2011 Tour de France champion, 2009 UCI Road World Champion, first Australian TdF winner

## Course Profile

### The Cadel Classic (124km Long Course)
The flagship route mirrors much of the professional race course through Geelong, the Bellarine Peninsula, and the Surf Coast. The 2025 edition extended the course by 11km from previous years.

**Route sequence**:
1. **Geelong Waterfront Start** (0m elevation) — mass start at Steampacket Gardens along Eastern Beach Road. Brief loop through Geelong Botanic Gardens.
2. **Portarlington Road to Wallington** — rolling road southeast through suburban Geelong, past vineyards and orchards toward the Bellarine Peninsula.
3. **Barwon Heads** — Cadel Evans' hometown. The course crosses the iconic Barwon Heads Bridge and passes the buzzing Barwon Heads Hotel and cafe. This section is flat to gently rolling along Barwon Heads Road.
4. **Thirteenth Beach** — one of the most scenic stretches. Smooth, fast road along the coastline with ocean views and natural saltmarsh environment. This was the start of nearly all of Cadel's home training rides. Described as "one of the most scenic spots to ride a bike in all of Australia."
5. **Surf Coast section** — through Torquay and past the world-famous Bells Beach surf break. The climb up from Bells Beach is described as "one of Australia's most iconic stretches of road." Crosswinds can pick up significantly along this exposed coastal section.
6. **Challambra Crescent** (optional on long course) — the signature climb. Long course riders can choose to tackle or bypass it via a shortcut at Scenic Drive.
7. **Return to Geelong** — rolling farmland roads back into the city, finishing at the waterfront.

### Key Climbs

**Challambra Crescent** (the signature climb):
- Distance: 1.1-1.4 km (sources vary; ~1.3km commonly cited)
- Average gradient: 7.9-9.6% (varies by measurement source)
- Maximum gradient: 11-15% (final section steepest)
- Elevation gain: ~87-104m
- Character: Short, punchy power climb. Not devastatingly hard but enough to split a peloton. In the pro race, riders tackle it 4 times (men) or 2 times (women). In the People's Ride, riders tackle it once with the option to bypass.

**Mount Moriac**:
- A sharp incline to the summit via rolling country roads. The turn at the Moriac General Store marks a strategic pressure point on the course.
- Located roughly at the halfway point of the pro race.

**Ceres township**:
- Rolling approach through farmland to the hilltop town of Ceres at 166m elevation.
- The course peaks at 188m above sea level near Gnarwarre Road before turning east toward Ceres and back into Geelong.

### Road Surface
- Fully sealed/paved roads throughout. High-quality Australian state and local roads.
- Barwon Heads Road noted for especially "smooth surfaces."
- Thirteenth Beach Road: smooth and scenic.
- No gravel, dirt, or cobblestone sections.
- Roads are traffic-managed (rolling and fixed closures) but not fully closed for the People's Ride. The pro race has full road closure.

### Cadel's Hometown (59-63km Short Course)
- Flatter cycling experience designed for beginner and intermediate riders.
- Loop around Geelong Botanic Gardens, then out-and-back along Barwon Heads Road to Barwon Heads.
- Bypasses the Challambra Climb and the Surf Coast section entirely.
- Takes advantage of the smooth, flat surfaces on Barwon Heads Road.

### 35km Course (added 2026)
- Shortest road option, details limited. Likely covers the Geelong-Barwon Heads corridor only.

**Getting to start**:
- The start/finish at Steampacket Gardens is walkable from central Geelong hotels.
- Free event parking details in annual Rider Guide (PDF published ~2 weeks before race).
- Public transport: V/Line train to Geelong Station, then short walk/ride to waterfront.
- Taxi/rideshare from Avalon: ~$30-40 AUD.
- SkyBus operates airport-to-Geelong shuttle from Avalon.

### Lodging Strategy
- **Geelong Waterfront** (prime): Novotel Geelong (esplanade views, walking distance to start), Oaks R Suites Geelong, R Hotel Geelong (heritage building, self-contained apartments), Vue Apartments (Eastern Beach outlook). Book early -- race weekend is peak demand.
- **Bellarine Peninsula**: Barwon Heads, Ocean Grove — charming coastal towns on the course. 20-30 min drive to start.
- **Budget**: Geelong & Bellarine hotels from ~$69 AUD/night (off-peak), expect $150-300+ race weekend.
- **Unique**: Mt Duneed Estate — vineyard accommodation pods, 20 min from CBD.
- **Melbourne**: 75 km away, viable if driving early. Not recommended given 7 AM start time.

### Race-Day Support
- **Feed stations**: 3 well-placed aid stations with volunteers. Key locations include Elephant Walk Car Park (opposite Zeally Bay Road on The Esplanade, Torquay) and Thirteenth Beach Road.
- **Mechanical support**: On-course bicycle mechanics available.
- **Medical**: Event medical services stationed along the course.
- **Sag wagon**: Transport available for riders who cannot finish.
- **Course closure time**: Not widely published for People's Ride but expected ~6-7 hours based on similar events.

### Registration
- Via Race Roster (raceroster.com)
- Typically opens September/October for following January/February event
- Closes ~2 days before race day or when field limit reached (2026 closed Fri 30 Jan at 4 PM)
- Packet pickup required before race day (details in Rider Guide)

## History

### Origin Story
The Cadel Evans Great Ocean Road Race was created in 2015 as a farewell event for Cadel Evans, who retired on 1 February 2015 after completing this race -- the very first edition. Evans was already Australia's greatest road cyclist: the only Australian to win the Tour de France (2011, riding for BMC Racing) and the 2009 UCI Road World Champion (won in his home region of Geelong). He also won the UCI ProTour in 2007.

Evans was born in Katherine, Northern Territory, but grew up and lived in Barwon Heads, a coastal town southwest of Geelong. His deep connection to the region -- training daily on these exact roads -- made the course authentic rather than manufactured. Evans began his cycling career as a mountain biker, winning the UCI Mountain Bike World Cup in 1998 and 1999, and competed in four Olympics (1996, 2000 in MTB; 2008, 2012 on road).

### Growth and UCI Status
- **2015**: Inaugural edition. Evans' farewell race. Gianni Meersman won the men's pro race.
- **2016**: Peter Kennaugh won.
- **2017**: Men's race elevated to UCI WorldTour status. Nikias Arndt won, edging Simon Gerrans in a sprint finish. The People's Ride established as a major mass-participation fixture.
- **2018**: Jay McCarthy won.
- **2019**: Elia Viviani won.
- **2020**: Women's race added to UCI Women's WorldTour calendar. Dries Devenyns won the men's race.
- **2021**: Cancelled (COVID-19).
- **2022**: Cancelled (COVID-19, announced December 2021).
- **2023**: Returned after two-year hiatus. Marius Mayrhofer won. Loes Adegeest won the women's race.
- **2024**: Laurence Pithie won. Rosita Reijnhout won the women's race.
- **2025**: Mauro Schmid won the men's race. Ally Wollaston (NZL, FDJ-Suez) won the women's race. Paris Olympic gold medallist Grace Brown joined Cadel Evans to ride the People's Ride alongside ~3,000 participants.
- **2026**: Tobias Lund Andresen won the men's race. Ally Wollaston won the women's race (back-to-back, becoming the rider with the most editions won). Mapei became title sponsor. ~3,000 riders in the People's Ride.

### Notable Milestones
- The event went from a farewell gesture to a UCI WorldTour fixture in just 3 years (2015-2017).
- Equal prize money for men's and women's races.
- The People's Ride has maintained consistent ~3,000-rider fields, a strong number for an Australian gran fondo.
- The event has become a key season-opener on the WorldTour calendar, attracting top WorldTour teams to Australia.

## Climate

### Typical Race-Day Conditions (Late January / Early February)
Geelong sits in a subtropical oceanic climate zone. Late January is peak summer in Victoria.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average high | 26.6C / 80F |
| Average low | 14.2C / 57F |
| Average temp | 20.4C / 69F |
| Extreme high (rare) | 41.5C / 107F+ (record: 46.3C / 115F in Jan 2014) |
| Average rainfall | 30mm over ~3 rain days in January |
| Humidity | ~64% average |
| Wind | 19.7 kph average (moderate; exposed coastal sections can be higher) |
| Daylight | ~14.5 hours (sunrise ~6:20 AM, sunset ~8:45 PM) |
| Sunshine | 8-11 hours/day |

### Key Weather Risks
- **Heat waves**: Victoria is prone to sudden, extreme heat events from inland desert winds. Single-day spikes to 40C+ are possible and have occurred during race weekend in past years. Heat exhaustion is a real risk.
- **UV exposure**: January UV index in Geelong regularly exceeds 11 (extreme). Sunscreen, arm/leg coverage, and hydration are critical.
- **Coastal wind**: The Surf Coast section is exposed. Crosswinds and headwinds can be significant, especially Thirteenth Beach to Torquay.
- **Rain**: Unlikely (only ~3 rain days in January) but when it comes, roads can be slick.
- **Early start mitigation**: The 7:00 AM start means the first 2-3 hours are in cooler conditions (15-20C), with heat building through mid-morning.

## Community & Culture

### Atmosphere
- The event has a festival atmosphere across the entire race weekend. Friday evening features the free GeelongPort Family Ride. Saturday is the People's Ride. Sunday features the UCI WorldTour pro races.
- Participants wear the mandatory official Santini jersey (a different color/design each year), creating a striking visual "sea of color" through the streets.
- Cadel Evans himself rides the People's Ride with participants, an extraordinary draw for cycling fans. In 2025, Olympic gold medallist Grace Brown also rode.
- The Geelong Waterfront precinct transforms into a live event village with screens, food, and spectator areas at Steampacket Gardens.

### International Participation
- While primarily an Australian event (Victoria/Melbourne cycling community forms the bulk), the WorldTour pro race connection draws international attention and some international amateur participants.
- The event does not have the same 90-country participation breadth as GFNY NYC, but its WorldTour status gives it credibility that few gran fondos can match.

### Media Coverage
- Extensive domestic TV coverage via SBS Sport (Australia's cycling broadcaster).
- The pro race is broadcast globally as part of UCI WorldTour coverage.
- CyclingNews, Velonews, ProCyclingStats, and all major cycling media cover the event.
- Strong social media presence (@cadelroadrace on Instagram).
- The People's Ride gets significant local and regional media coverage (Geelong Times, Surf Coast Times, Visit Melbourne).

### Connection to Pro Race
This is the People's Ride's unique selling proposition and its most distinctive feature among global gran fondos. Very few mass-participation cycling events are directly linked to an active UCI WorldTour race:
- Participants ride the same roads as WorldTour professionals.
- The People's Ride on Saturday creates buzz for the Sunday pro race.
- The course's key feature (Challambra Crescent) is the same decisive climb in the pro race.
- Cadel Evans bridges both worlds as a Tour de France champion who personally rides with amateurs.
- The event structure (People's Ride Saturday, pro race Sunday) creates a weekend cycling festival format.

### Geelong as a Cycling City
- Geelong hosted the 2010 UCI Road World Championships (where Evans finished 7th; the following year's Worlds in Copenhagen saw Evans finish 8th, but he had won the title in Mendrisio 2009).
- Strong local cycling culture with Cycling Geelong and multiple cycling clubs.
- Growing cycling infrastructure with dedicated bike lanes and paths.
- The Bellarine Peninsula and Surf Coast offer year-round riding with minimal traffic on scenic roads.

## Pro Race Details (for context / prestige scoring)

### Men's Race (UCI WorldTour)
- Distance: ~182-184 km
- Elevation: 2,000+ meters
- 4 ascents of Challambra Crescent in the finishing circuit (21km loop)
- Mountain classification points on first 3 Challambra ascents
- Full road closure with police escort
- Peak elevation: 188m (near Gnarwarre Road)
- WorldTour since 2017

### Women's Race (UCI Women's WorldTour)
- Shorter course (~110-115 km)
- 2 ascents of Challambra Crescent
- Women's WorldTour since 2020

## Scoring Dimension Notes

### Distance (124km long course)
Mid-range gran fondo distance. The 124km Cadel Classic is shorter than many flagship European gran fondos (140-180km) but longer than typical US charity centuries. Multiple route options (35/59/124km) provide accessibility.

### Climbing (~1,200-1,500m for People's Ride)
Moderate climbing. Challambra Crescent is the only significant pitch, and it's optional. The rest is rolling coastal/farmland terrain. The pro race accumulates 2,000m+ over 184km with 4x Challambra, but the People's Ride does it once.

### Descent Technicality
Low. Descents are gentle, on wide, well-maintained sealed roads. No hairpins, no technical switchbacks, no gravel descents.

### Road Surface
Excellent. Fully sealed, well-maintained Australian state roads. Barwon Heads Road and Thirteenth Beach Road noted as especially smooth.

### Climate Risk
Moderate-to-high. January heat in Victoria can be extreme (40C+ heat waves possible). UV exposure is severe. However, the early start time (7 AM) mitigates the worst heat. Rain is unlikely (~3 days/month).

### Altitude
Negligible. Sea level start, peak elevation 188m. No altitude-related performance concerns.

### Logistics
Good. Avalon Airport is 25km away (18 min drive). Melbourne Tullamarine is 82km (1 hour drive). Geelong has ample accommodation from $69-300+ AUD/night. The start is in central Geelong, walkable from most hotels. Well-organized event village.

### Prestige
High. This is the only mass-participation ride directly associated with a UCI WorldTour race. Named for a Tour de France champion. Evans rides with participants. WorldTour status since 2017.

### Organization
High. Run by a professional events team with government/state backing (TAC, Visit Victoria). Feed stations, mechanical support, medical, sag wagon all provided. Detailed Rider Guide published. Smooth registration via Race Roster.

### Scenic Experience
High. Coastal views along the Great Ocean Road/Surf Coast, Bells Beach, Thirteenth Beach saltmarsh, Barwon Heads Bridge, rolling pastoral farmland, Geelong Waterfront. One of Australia's most scenic cycling routes.

### Community & Culture
High. Festival weekend atmosphere, mandatory event jersey creates visual spectacle, Cadel Evans rides alongside participants, connection to WorldTour pro race, strong local cycling culture in Geelong/Bellarine.

### Field Depth
Moderate. ~3,000 participants is solid for an Australian gran fondo but modest by global standards (GFNY NYC: 4,000-6,000; Cape Town Cycle Tour: 35,000). The pro race field is WorldTour-caliber.

### Value
Good. Entry fee (~$150-185 AUD / ~$95-120 USD) includes a high-quality Santini jersey ($130 AUD RRP), feed stations, mechanical support, medical, and sag wagon. Strong value proposition.

### Expenses
Moderate. Geelong is more affordable than Melbourne or Sydney. Hotel rates $100-300 AUD race weekend. Flights to Melbourne or Avalon are the main cost for interstate/international visitors. The AUD is generally favorable for USD/EUR visitors.

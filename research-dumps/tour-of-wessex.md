# Tour of Wessex — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Status: defunct (medium-high confidence)
No 2026 edition or registration exists in either format (see below). The former
official domain (tourofwessex.com/enter) now redirects to an unrelated,
non-cycling site — do NOT cite or link that URL as a live source. No formal
closure announcement found, so classified as "defunct" rather than "cancelled."

## What changed
Classic three-day sportive was still advertised for 27-29 May 2023, based in
Langport, Somerset, with 355km/535km options. By 2024-2025 the name had been
repurposed for a Sherborne-based, primarily one-day repeated-lap challenge — a
different event/format under the same name, not a rename of the same product.

## Last advertised vitals
- 2025 (successor format): 24 May 2025, Sherborne, Dorset; 125/250/375km (one to
  three laps of a 125km circuit) plus a separate 110km cafe ride on 25 May. No
  elevation figure survives on the 2025 page (saintpiranservicecourse.com).
- Classic format last full profile: Langport HQ, 535km over three days (2023);
  2021 route archive totals ~538km/5,300m climbing. Yeovil was on-route, not
  the event base; earlier editions (pre-2012) used Somerton.

## Course character (classic format)
Open-road paved West Country lanes, three consecutive century-scale days.
Signature climbs: Cheddar Gorge, King Alfred's Tower, Lulworth/Purbeck roads,
Quantock Common, Porlock Toll Road. Hardest climbing traditionally late stage 3.

## Organizer/history
Running by 2006, created/promoted by Nick Bourne (Pendragon Sports, later
Earth Sports branding). Bourne was still promoting the 2025 edition per LinkedIn.
Field: 2,300 riders (2012), 1,458 (2013); no credible 2024-25 total published.

## Criticism
2016 review (Broleur) praised the ride but criticized the organizer's website
for thin maps/feed-station details/climb profiles. Separate proposed Premier
Calendar road races under the Tour of Wessex name were cancelled 2008-09 over
policing/road-closure objections — the sportive itself continued afterward.

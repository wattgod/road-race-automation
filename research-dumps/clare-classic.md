# Clare Classic — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Identity resolution (two separate contaminations found)

1. **youtube_data was entirely Irish.** All videos/quotes/rider_intel on file (ŠKODA Ring of
   Clare Cycle, ŠKODA Boyne Valley Classic) describe Ireland's Ring of Clare Cycle sportive
   (Ennis, County Clare — Wild Atlantic Way/Loop Head/Shannon Estuary) and a separate Drogheda
   event. Confirmed via ringofclare.ie official event info: starts/finishes at Clarecastle GAA
   near Ennis, 120/160km coastal routes.

2. **On-file date was wrong-race.** "2026: February 15" belongs to Spain's UCI-listed Clásica
   de Almería (uci.org/competition-details/2026/ROA/77995), not this event.

The real, separate **Australian Clare Classic** is in Clare Valley, South Australia, ~140km
north of Adelaide — Bicycling Australia's "The Classics" series (cyclingclassics.com.au/clare/),
the series' second gran fondo after Bowral.

## 2026 status: UNKNOWN (dormant, not confirmed defunct)

Only one edition ever ran: **Sunday 8 April 2018**, ~900 riders (~40% interstate), 160/110/50km
courses. A second edition was scheduled for 5 April 2020 (with a planned Saturday "Dirty Clare"
80km gravel add-on) but cancelled 16 March 2020 for COVID-19. No edition has run since — as of
2026 the organizer's Clare page shows only stale/misapplied date text ("Sunday, 19 October
2025") while sibling events Bowral and Mudgee carry live, dated 2026 editions. No explicit
discontinuation notice found — hence `unknown`, not `defunct`.

## Course

Rolling, punchy Clare Valley wine-country roads — vineyards, valley views, historic towns,
Mintaro as a recognizable rest-stop feature. No named KOM/signature climb documented. Heat
(33-36°C in 2018) and gusty northerly crosswinds were notable factors (organizer materials
warned deep-section wheels could be affected by crosswinds). One contemporary rider report
described the 110km course as harder than the organizer's Bowral Classic event.

## Organizer detail

The Classics series was founded in 2016 by Yaffa Media and its Bicycling Australia brand;
Bowral Classic (New South Wales) is the series' flagship event, Clare Classic its second.
The organizer's own 2018 post-event coverage described the debut as "a major success" —
this is the organizer/publisher's own characterization, not an independently verified
rider consensus.

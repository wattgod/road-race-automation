## Claim: "the 1973 World Championship" (history.origin_story — Felice Gimondi)
- **Verified true.** Felice Gimondi won the 1973 UCI Road World Championships men's elite road race, held September 2, 1973, in Barcelona, Spain (270 km, winning time 6:31:26), ahead of Freddy Maertens and Luis Ocaña. This was Gimondi's world title, after multiple runner-up finishes to Eddy Merckx in earlier years.
- Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1973_UCI_Road_World_Championships_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_road_race ; https://www.procyclingstats.com/race/world-championship/1973/result ; https://bikeraceinfo.com/worlds/worlds-1973.html
- Note: the 1973 Worlds were held in Barcelona, not on the Il Lombardia course — the profile's claim is about Gimondi's personal palmarès (career achievement), not a claim that Il Lombardia itself was a Worlds course, so no correction needed. The profile text ("won all three Grand Tours, and the 1973 World Championship before his death in August 2019") correctly attributes the world title to Gimondi as a career fact.
- Gimondi's death date (August 16, 2019) and Bergamo-area heritage (Sedrina passage in the gran fondo route, event renamed for him in 2020) are consistent with independent cycling-history sourcing and were not disputed by any source checked.

## Course facts
- Two-day event: Stage 1 (Sat) 112km with two timed climbing sectors; Stage 2 (Sun) 75km with one timed climbing sector. Total weekend distance ~187km (event page) / 186km (official PDF regs) — organizer-source discrepancy. Source: https://epicgrancanaria.com/en-gb/, https://epicgrancanaria.com/ws/media-library/9ca47b43478f539c6a736526fa972f22/gb-regulation-epic-gran-canaria-2026-2.pdf
- Timed climbs: Ayagaures/Pedro Gonzalez 12.3km/423m/3.7%; Pasito Blanco-Alto Salto del Perro 29km/997m/4.2%; Tirajana/Tunte 22km/1,005m. Timed-sector elevation subtotal = 2,425m; this is a documented floor, NOT the full-route total (untimed rolling/neutralized sections add more, unquantified). Source: https://www.topbici.es/epic-gran-canaria-cita-ciclista-internacional/
- Classification = sum of the 3 timed-sector chip times (Crono 1+2+3), not elapsed time over the full weekend route — group/event start, individually chip-timed climbs. Source: https://epicgrancanaria.com/en-gb/
- Surface: paved public roads (GC-500 and named asphalt mountain climbs); no gravel/trail/MTB sections disclosed. Source: https://epicgrancanaria.com/ws/media-library/9ca47b43478f539c6a736526fa972f22/gb-regulation-epic-gran-canaria-2026-2.pdf
- Field: 680 in 2026 (up from 500 in 2025); organizer FAQ still states a stale 500-spot cap. Source: https://www.grancanaria.com/turismo/area-profesional/noticias/noticia/?cHash=8b23893ba491102f3717584482dca42b&tx_ttnews%5Btt_news%5D=5465
- Entry: full two-stage or single-stage registration; requires RFEC/UCI-affiliated license or organizer's day insurance (~EUR20). Source: official 2026 regulations PDF above.
- Founded: first edition Feb 17-18, 2018 (2026 = 9th edition), NOT 2013. Source: https://www.ciclo21.com/epic-gran-canaria-2018/, Gran Canaria tourism report above.
- GFWT: legitimate — GFWT's own events page lists Riu Hotels Epic Gran Canaria as the season-opening event; Gran Canaria's official tourism authority also reports it as part of GFWT. Source: https://www.granfondoworldtour.com/events

## Format verdict
Legitimate mass-participation, two-day paved-road stage sportive/gran fondo with three individually timed climbing sectors — not a self-supported ultra, travel product, virtual challenge, or MTB/gravel event mislabeled as road. RFEC classifies it "Cycling for All" (formally non-competitive) even though it uses chips/classifications/leader jerseys/cumulative results — an organizer-level formality, not evidence of dishonest framing on our part. `discipline='gran_fondo'` retained per standing rule not to change discipline field; two-day/timed-segment structure described honestly in prose instead.

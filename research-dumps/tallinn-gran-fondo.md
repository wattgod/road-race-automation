## Identity resolution (highest-risk item this wave)
No evidence a "Tallinn Gran Fondo" matching the prior stub's vitals (130.4 km,
fixed August 15 date) ever existed. The content and video footage attached to
this slug actually describe **Gran Fondo Estonia**, a real Tallinn-Tartu
point-to-point controlled group ride.

The stub's attached "Tour of Estonia 2017: Tallinn-Tartu GP" video is
definitively the WRONG event — Tour of Estonia lists Tallinn-Tartu as a
licensed professional stage race, a separate event from the recreational
Gran Fondo Estonia.

## Real vitals
Approximately 230-234 km, traditionally August 20, Tallinn to Tartu.
Non-competitive, controlled group ride (not a timed race) with escorted
pace groups, food/medical/mechanical support. A roughly 100-115 km Medio
Fondo option ended at Tamsalu rather than continuing to Tartu.
https://sport.err.ee/94058/suur-rattasoit-labi-eesti-toimub-ka-jargmisel-aastal
https://www.kuulutaja.ee/PDF/30_2017.pdf (2017 local report)

## Course character
Mostly flat Tallinn-to-Tamsalu, then hillier Pandivere Upland terrain. The
documented 2015 route included a short (~1km/~10%) climb at Emumägi, one
short gravel sector despite the "road" framing, two timed segments, and a
narrow cobbled finishing climb beneath Tartu's Angel's Bridge (Inglisild).
https://www.piiriveere.ee/ckfinder/userfiles/files/%C3%95ppereis%20Piiriveere%20Liider%20tegevusgrupi%20piirkonda%2C%20lk%202.pdf

## History
First held 2015; 2017 edition described as the third running. Organizer:
MTÜ TriSmile, led by former triathlete Ain-Alar Juhanson, sponsored by
Hawaii Express. 2018 reversed direction (Tartu-Tallinn); 2019 returned to
Tallinn-Tartu (last confirmed edition). Planned 2020 edition reassigned to
MTÜ Sportlik Eesti — no evidence it ran.
https://www.sportos.eu/ee/ru/gran-fondo-estonia-tartu-tallinn/registreerunud/yvJO/
https://www.tallinn.ee/est/g17208s142444 (Tallinn grant)

## Criticism/caveats
No credible published organizer scandal or sustained rider criticism found.
The meaningful caveats: it was not actually a race, included an unadvertised
gravel section, and finished on a constricted cobbled climb — material
mismatches for riders expecting a standard paved competitive fondo.

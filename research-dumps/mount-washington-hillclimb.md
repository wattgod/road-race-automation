# Research Dump: Mt. Washington Auto Road Bicycle Hillclimb

## Quick Facts
- 7.6mi / 12.2km, 4,678ft / 1,426m elevation gain, single climb (no descent)
- Start: Mt. Washington Auto Road toll booth, Pinkham Notch, Gorham, NH (elev. ~1,600ft / 488m)
- Finish: Summit of Mt. Washington (6,288ft / 1,917m) -- highest peak in the northeastern United States
- Third Saturday in August (53rd edition: August 15, 2026; rain date August 16)
- 2025 was August 16 (52nd edition); 2024 was August 17 (51st edition)
- Field size: 480 riders (2026), was 635 in 2024. Historically up to 600. Sells out. Waitlist available.
- Founded 1973 (first winner: John Allis, 1:15:05)
- Entry fee: $150 registration + mandatory minimum $300 fundraising = $450 total (2026). Summit Fund-Racer category: $150 + $850 fundraising. Under 19: $100 + $300 fundraising.
- Start format: Mass start (cannon start), 9:00 AM (2026), historically 8:30 AM. All riders go together.
- Fundraising event benefiting Tin Mountain Conservation Center (TMCC)
- Course record (post-2022 paving): Male: Ian Boswell 0:50:15 (2025). Female: Illi Gardner 0:58:01 (2025). Nonbinary: Gustavo Caicedo 1:46:13 (2022).
- Historical pre-2022 records (before full paving, since voided/reset): Male: Tom Danielson 0:49:24 (2002). Female: Jeannie Longo 0:58:14 (2000).
- Practice ride: July 19, 2026 (separate registration required)
- The hillclimb and practice ride are the only two days per year cyclists are permitted on the Auto Road

## Course Profile
- Distance: 7.6mi / 12.2km (some sources cite 7.4mi / 11.9km -- slight variance based on measurement)
- Elevation gain: 4,678ft / 1,426m
- Average gradient: 12%
- Maximum gradient: 22% (final 50 yards to summit)
- Steepest mile: ~13.8%
- Steepest quarter-mile: ~16%
- Gradient distribution: 61% of the route (4.5 miles) at 10-15% grade; 11% (0.8 miles) at 15-20% grade
- Extended sections of 18% gradient
- Road surface: 100% paved as of 2022. Prior to 2022, a section known as the "5-mile stretch" was unpaved gravel -- the last section of the original 1861 road to be paved. Records were reset after full paving.
- No feed zones / water stops mentioned in any source. Riders must be self-sufficient.
- Recommended gearing: 34/36 or lower (compact with ultra-low cassette)
- PJAMM Cycling difficulty index (PDI): 45.6 (ranked 3rd hardest climb in the US, behind Mauna Kea and Haleakala)
- PJAMM FIETS index: 17.9

### Segment-by-Segment Approximate Profile
- **Mile 0-0.5**: Brief flat section from toll booth, then hits ~12% ramp immediately
- **Mile 0.5-2**: Grade eases slightly after initial ramp, then rolling ramps of 12-15% with occasional short shallow sections. Through dense forest.
- **Mile 2-3**: Passes 2,000ft mark. Sustained steep gradients. Average ~11-12%.
- **Mile 3-4**: Continued steep climbing. Approaching treeline (~4,400ft / 1,340m).
- **Mile 4-5**: The "5-mile" section (historically gravel, now paved). Treeline reached around mile 4. Above treeline, fully exposed to wind and weather.
- **Mile 5-6**: Above treeline. Crosswinds and headwinds become a major factor. Extended 12-15% grades.
- **Mile 6-7**: Approaching summit. Grades steepen to 15-18%. Views of the Northern Presidentials.
- **Mile 7-7.6**: Final push to summit. Brutally steep finish -- 18% sustained, final 50 yards at 22%. The Cog Railway visible nearby.

### Above-Treeline Exposure
- Treeline on Mt. Washington is approximately 4,400ft (1,340m)
- Roughly half the Auto Road (the upper ~3.8 miles) is above treeline
- Above treeline: zero shelter from wind, rain, fog, cold, or lightning
- Riders are fully exposed to whatever weather the summit is producing
- Wind gusts regularly exceed 40 mph even in August
- Aimee Vasse was literally blown off her bike by crosswinds during the 2005 edition

## Logistics
- **Location**: 1 Mt. Washington Auto Road, Gorham, NH 03581 (Route 16, Pinkham Notch)
- **Nearest airports**:
  - Portland (PWM), Maine: ~93mi / 2hr drive
  - Manchester (MHT), New Hampshire: ~128mi / 2hr 10min drive
  - Boston Logan (BOS), Massachusetts: ~180mi / 3hr drive (best for international arrivals)
  - Burlington (BTV), Vermont: ~3hr drive
- **Lodging**: Gorham, NH is the primary lodging hub (~8-9 miles from Auto Road base)
  - The Glen House: 0.2mi from Auto Road base (closest)
  - AMC Joe Dodge Lodge at Pinkham Notch: 2.6mi from base
  - Town and Country Inn, Gorham: 8.5mi
  - Quality Inn, Gorham: 8.1mi
  - Multiple motels/inns in Gorham corridor
  - Jackson, NH and North Conway, NH also viable (~20-30min drive)
  - Book early -- race weekend fills up local accommodations
- **Race-day transport / descent**: Riders MUST pre-arrange their own ride down from the summit. Cycling down the Auto Road is prohibited. Each rider receives one "Auto Pass" attached to their bib to give to their support driver. Support drivers must have a vehicle carrying minimum 2 racers to access the Auto Road. Auto Road closes at 7:45 AM for support vehicle ascent; drivers must be at the summit before riders finish. There is a rideshare Facebook group for those without a driver.
- **Bib pickup**: Thursday before race 3-6 PM at Tin Mountain Conservation Center; Friday at Auto Road base 2-7 PM; Saturday morning 6:15-7:30 AM at base.
- **Cutoff time**: No formal cutoff time specified in sources. The race will be stopped if conditions become unsafe. Race has historically been cancelled/shortened for weather.
- **Rain date**: August 16, 2026 (the following day). If cancelled both days, no refunds -- entry fee becomes a tax-deductible donation to TMCC.
- **Registration**: Opens January 1 for special attendees (10+ race veterans), January 15 for everyone. Sells out. Waitlist available after main field fills. No day-of registration. Online registration deadline August 3, 2026.
- **Cancellation policy**: Refund minus $75 admin fee if cancelled by July 1. After July 1, no refunds.
- **Post-race**: Complimentary meal at summit (ham sandwich noted). Warm, dry clothes essential at summit. Support driver brings warm clothes for the rider.

## History
- **1861**: Mt. Washington Auto Road opens (August 8, 1861) -- oldest man-made and continuously operating tourist attraction in North America. Built by hand with shovels and black powder.
- **1973**: First Mt. Washington Auto Road Bicycle Hillclimb. John Allis wins inaugural edition in 1:15:05.
- **Early 1990s**: Tin Mountain Conservation Center (TMCC) approached by Auto Road to organize the hillclimb. Partnership formed -- TMCC has hosted it since.
- **1980**: Dale Stetina (father of Pete Stetina) wins in 0:57:41, smashing John Allis's record by nearly 4 minutes.
- **1997**: Mike Engleman sets men's record at 0:53:53.
- **1998**: Marilyn Ruseckas sets first women's bicycle record at 1:11:38.
- **1999**: Tyler Hamilton wins (his first of four victories), setting a new record.
- **2000**: Jeannie Longo sets women's record at 0:58:14 (stood as pre-paving record until reset in 2022).
- **2002**: Tom Danielson sets men's record at 0:49:24 -- the legendary pre-paving all-time record. First sub-50-minute ascent.
- **2005**: Tyler Hamilton wins first race after 11-month ban (non-UCI sanctioned event). Ned Overend celebrates 50th birthday with 4th-place overall finish, setting multiple masters records.
- **2006**: Tyler Hamilton's 4th and final victory in 0:52:21.
- **2008**: Phil Gaimon wins first of his four victories (0:54:57). 600-rider field. Zero wind at summit -- rare benign conditions.
- **2011**: Race organizers void records by Tyler Hamilton (USA) and Genevieve Jeanson (Canada) due to admitted PED use.
- **2022**: The "5-mile stretch" is paved, making the Auto Road fully paved top-to-bottom for the first time in 161 years. All records reset. 49th edition. Phil Gaimon sets new men's record at 0:50:38. Courtney Nelson sets women's record at 1:09:35.
- **2024**: 51st edition. Kristen Kulchinsky sets new women's record at 1:06:08. Cameron Cogburn wins men's race.
- **2025**: 52nd edition. Ian Boswell (ex-WorldTour pro, 34) sets new men's record at 0:50:15 (some sources say 0:50:18 -- slight discrepancy between timing sources). Peter Stetina 2nd in 0:51:36. Illi Gardner (British National Hill Climb Champion, 26) sets new women's record at 0:58:01 (some sources say 0:58:07), finishing 6th overall and obliterating the previous women's record by over 8 minutes.

### "The Worst Weather in the World"
- Mt. Washington held the world record for highest directly measured surface wind speed: 231 mph (April 12, 1934). This stood as the world record until 1996 (Barrow Island, Australia, during a cyclone -- a disputed measurement). It remains the record for the Northern and Western Hemispheres.
- Record wind chill: -108.4F / -78C (February 2023)
- Annual average summit temperature: 33.9F / 1.1C
- No temperature above 72F / 22C has ever been recorded at the summit
- Three major weather tracks converge over the summit
- The mountain sits at the intersection of major storm paths and is the most prominent peak for hundreds of miles in every direction
- Winds exceed hurricane force (75+ mph) more than 100 days per year

## Climate (August Race Day)
- **Summit August averages**: Mean temp 48.7F / 9.3C. Daily max avg 54.2F / 12.3C. Daily min avg 43.2F / 6.2C.
- **Summit August extremes**: Record high 72F / 22C (1975). Record low 20F / -6.7C (1986).
- **Base temperatures**: Gorham/Pinkham Notch area in August typically 60-80F / 16-27C
- **Temperature differential**: Base to summit can be 30-40F+ difference. Starting at 65F at the base, riders may finish in sub-40F conditions.
- **Wind**: Even in August, summit winds regularly 20-40 mph. Gusts can exceed 60-80+ mph. Highest August gust ever: data not specified but July record gust is 154 mph (1996).
- **Fog**: Fog is extremely common. In a typical summer month, fog is present for 70-80% of hours. Visibility can drop to near zero.
- **Rain**: Summer precipitation is frequent. Thunderstorm risk is real above treeline. Lightning is a genuine hazard with no shelter available.
- **Race-day conditions vary wildly**: Some years are calm and sunny; other years feature driving rain, fog, and 40+ mph winds. Riders have been blown off bikes. Race can be stopped mid-event.
- **Gear requirement**: Warm, dry clothes MUST be available at the summit (via support driver). Post-race at the summit in wet cycling kit can be hypothermia-inducing even in August.

## Community & Culture
- **The New England hillclimb tradition**: MWARBH is the crown jewel of northeastern US hillclimb racing. It anchors the BUMPS (Bike Up Mountain Points Series), the only hillclimb series of its kind in America.
- **"The toughest hillclimb in the US, if not the world"**: This is the event's official tagline and widely endorsed by the cycling media.
- **"Rockpilers"**: Riders who have completed 10+ editions are recognized as "Rockpilers" -- an honor roll maintained by the race.
- **Pure hillclimb suffering**: There is no descent. No flat respite. No drafting benefit (too steep). Just 7.6 miles of relentless uphill. The suffering is the point. As Illi Gardner said: "It's harder than almost every climb I've done."
- **Fundraising culture**: Since the mid-2010s, the event has shifted to a mandatory fundraising model. The $150 registration is just the start -- riders must raise additional funds for TMCC. The 2026 edition requires a minimum $300 fundraise (total $450). This aligns the event with its charity mission but changes the economic model from a pure race entry.
- **Field composition**: Mix of serious racers, ex-pros, strong amateurs, and bucket-list riders. Age groups span under-19 to 75+. The field includes "Alternative Pedal Power Bikes" (recumbents, etc.).
- **Competitive depth**: The open field format means ex-WorldTour pros (Boswell, Stetina, Gaimon) race alongside recreational riders. Top times are world-class; the tail of the field may take 2+ hours.
- **The cannon start**: Traditionally started with the firing of a cannon -- a beloved tradition.
- **Post-race at the summit**: Complimentary meal. Cold, windy, exhilarating. Support drivers deliver warm clothes. Photos with the summit sign. Then everyone drives back down.
- **No cycling down**: This is a one-way event. You climb. You stop. You get driven down. There is no descent.
- **Practice ride**: A separate event (July) where registered riders can ride the course before race day. The only other day cyclists are allowed on the Auto Road.
- **Media coverage**: Covered by Cycling Weekly, CyclingNews, Escape Collective, VeloNews, Gran Fondo Guide, Gran Fondo Daily News. Strambecco rates it one of the best cycling events in New England and one of the best hillclimbs in America.
- **Unsanctioned**: Not a UCI or USA Cycling sanctioned event. This has historically allowed banned riders (e.g., Tyler Hamilton during his 2-year UCI ban) to compete.
- **The "This Car Climbed Mt. Washington" tradition**: The famous bumper sticker for the Auto Road. Cyclists earn their own bragging rights.

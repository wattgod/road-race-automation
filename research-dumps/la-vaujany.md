## The contradiction, resolved
Two profiles in our catalog (la-vaujany.json, canonical; gfny-la-vaujany-alpe-dhuez.json, dupe stub) carried directly contradictory 2026 eligibility audits both dated 2026-07-23: one said a 21 June 2026 edition was active, the other said the 2026 edition was cancelled. Fresh research (2026-07-24) shows BOTH audits were partially right because they were looking at TWO DISTINCT GFNY EVENTS based in the same town:

| Event | 2026 status | Date |
|---|---|---|
| GFNY La Vaujany – L'Alpe d'Huez | HELD/completed — not cancelled | Sunday, 21 June 2026 (6th edition) |
| GFNY Alpes Vaujany – Croix de Fer | 2026 status unresolved-leaning-cancelled (no GFNY date; conflicting third-party listings — see sol corrections below) | provisional 23 August 2026 |

- Definitive evidence: GFNY's official post-race report (published 24 June 2026) states the sixth GFNY La Vaujany–L'Alpe d'Huez took place the previous Sunday. Because of an Orange Heat Alert, organizers replaced the planned long course with the fully timed 60 km medium course. https://gfny.com/gfny-super-sunday-results-2/
- GFNY's official Race Finder lists "GFNY La Vaujany Alpe d'Huez" with date 21 June 2026, and "GFNY Alpes Vaujany Croix de Fer" separately WITHOUT a date. https://gfny.com/race-finder/?fwp_race_finder_event_type=cycling
- The Alpes Vaujany legacy site (vaujany.gfny.com) is stale: still refers to the 2025 medal, no working 2026 registration, results stop at 2025.
- Vaujany's official 2026 summer agenda lists only the 21 June GFNY event, no August edition. https://www.vaujany.com/wp-content/uploads/agenda-ete-vaujany-web.pdf
- CycloWorld ID 39459 (gfny-la-vaujany) = the June Alpe d'Huez race → correctly listed as going ahead. CycloWorld ID 40542 (gfny-alpes-vaujany) = the separate late-summer Croix de Fer race → correctly listed as cancelled for 2026.
- Both events existed independently in 2025: La Vaujany on 15 June 2025, Alpes Vaujany on 24 August 2025 — different courses, different registration records. Near-identical branding + shared venue + cross-contaminated copy caused the apparent contradiction.

## 2026 edition facts (GFNY La Vaujany – L'Alpe d'Huez)
- 6th edition held Sunday 21 June 2026 (consistent with founding first edition 2021)
- Orange Heat Alert forced the planned long course to be replaced with the fully timed 60 km medium course for all riders — the 2026 edition was raced on a substantially modified (shortened) route
- Source: https://gfny.com/gfny-super-sunday-results-2/

## Course verification (supplementary codex pass, 2026-07-24)
- Long course: best-supported official figures are 151 km / 4,172 m, per GFNY's 2025 pre-race announcement, 2025 post-race report, and June 18 2026 preview (all consistent). No official support found for the on-file 170.6 km / 3,962 m — that figure looks stale or an alternate GPS measurement; replaced with 151 km / 4,172 m. (One GFNY registration listing gave 151 km / 3,900 m — minor rounding noise; the dated race releases consistently use 4,172 m, use that.)
  - https://gfny.com/lalpe-dhuez-this-sunday-at-gfny-la-vaujany/ (2025 pre-race, 151km/4172m)
  - https://gfny.com/alleman-king-of-lalpe-dhuez-with-fourth-victory-at-gfny-la-vaujany/ (2025 post-race, confirms 151km/4172m)
  - https://gfny.com/gfny-super-sunday-indonesia-and-the-french-alps-set-the-stage-for-a-global-weekend-of-racing/ (2026 preview, 151km/4172m, mentions Alpe d'Huez 21 switchbacks)
- Climb sequence CONFIRMED accurate for the normal long course: Col de la Morte (15.1km at 6.7% avg, 10% max) → Col d'Ornon → Alpe d'Huez (21 switchbacks, confirmed by official 2026 preview) → descent through Villard-Reculas/Pas de la Confession → final ~5km climb to Vaujany with pitches up to 14%. Source: https://lavaujany.gfny.com/course/?lang=en
- IMPORTANT: the 2026 heat-shortened race (60km replacement) did NOT follow this sequence — it retained Alpe d'Huez and the Vaujany finish but omitted Col de la Morte and Col d'Ornon.
- Medium course status: NORMALLY timed but non-competitive — 60.8 km, riders get a time but results are listed alphabetically, not ranked (official course page). The 2026 REPLACEMENT course was an exception: due to the Orange Heat Alert, GFNY ran the ~60km/~2,000m substitute course fully timed AND competitively classified (overall winners/podiums awarded) — see https://gfny.com/gfny-super-sunday-results-2/
- Founding year CONFIRMED: 2021 is correct for GFNY La Vaujany–L'Alpe d'Huez — official 2021 race guide (https://gfny.com/pdfs/lavaujany2021.pdf) explicitly calls it the "1st edition." 5th edition label in 2025 and 6th edition label in 2026 are consistent with a 2021 founding.
- Naming caveat: Vaujany separately hosted a differently-routed "GFNY Alpes Vaujany" event in August 2020 (predates and is distinct from La Vaujany–L'Alpe d'Huez) — same lineage as "GFNY Alpes Vaujany – Croix de Fer" referenced above (2026 status unresolved, see sol corrections below). Two historically separate GFNY Vaujany-area events; this profile (la-vaujany.json / GFNY La Vaujany-L'Alpe d'Huez) covers only the one that ran 21 June 2026.

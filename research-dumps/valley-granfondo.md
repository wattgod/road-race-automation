## Identity / naming history — same event, three sponsor-era names
Same race as "Prospera Valley GranFondo" (historic sponsor) and "Applewood Valley
GranFondo" (2022-2024 title sponsor, matches the existing youtube_data in this
profile) and occasionally "Fraser Valley GranFondo" (BBBS Langley's wording, same
date/Race Roster link). All the same event at Eagle Acres Dairy Farm, Fort Langley.
NOT: Ride for Water (separate Chilliwack gravel event), Cowichan Crusher/Burnt
Bridge (Vancouver Island gravel fondos), or the Okanagan Granfondo (Penticton,
discontinued after 2025 following a fatal collision — that incident belongs to
Okanagan Granfondo, not Valley GranFondo).

## Course character
Paved, predominantly agricultural Fraser Valley circuit, NOT a mountain gran
fondo — leaves Fort Langley beside the Fraser River, crosses the Langley/
Abbotsford farm-road grid (Bradner, Mt. Lehman), reaches near the Canada-US
border roads, returns to Eagle Acres. 990m accumulated via repeated rollers/
low foothill climbs (longest sections only 5.8-7.9km at 61-79m gain each), not
one alpine ascent. Traffic-managed, NOT fully closed roads — riders share
railway crossings, vehicle lanes, bike-lane dividers per the official rider guide.

## Organizer & history
Conceived by Jeffrey Hansen-Carlson, developed ~14 months with a volunteer team
led operationally by Bruce Dayton, debuted July 2012 with just under 1,200
riders; won Tourism Langley's event-of-the-year Tourism Excellence Award.
Recent editions operated by M1 Sports Management.

## Credible criticisms
No substantiated major controversy. Rider reports (Reddit r/vancouvercycling,
2025) describe the event as comparatively "bare-bones" next to Whistler
GranFondo, with normal open-road traffic exposure, aggressive vehicle passing,
sketchy corners, and grass/debris in parking areas — anecdotal, not official
findings. Official 2026 rider guide independently confirms open-road hazards
(railway crossings, limited-sightline corners, descents, vehicle lanes). Refund
terms are strict: non-refundable/non-deferrable without paid third-party
protection, including for weather cancellations.

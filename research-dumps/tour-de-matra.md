# Research Dump: Tour de Mátra

## Quick Facts
- Location: Gyöngyös, Mátra Mountains, Hungary. Road races start in Gyöngyös and finish at Kékestető, Hungary's highest point (1,014m).
- Distances (2026): 19 km individual time trial, 16 km Mini Fondo, 94 km Medio Fondo, 161 km Gran Fondo
- 161 km Gran Fondo elevation: 2,550m / ~8,366ft
- Surface: paved road ("excellent quality asphalt" per organizer)
- Course structure: road race begins south of Gyöngyös, crosses the city, reaches a ~67 km circuit — Medio Fondo riders complete it once, Gran Fondo riders twice — then both turn toward the Kékestető summit finish
- Founded 2021 as a local road event (nonprofit Mátra Biker Sport Club). 2026 was the sixth edition and became Hungary's first UCI Gran Fondo World Series round and a World Championship qualifier.
- UCI affiliation: 2026 designated the 161 km distance as the UCI qualifier for men aged 19-59; the 94 km race was the qualifier for women and men 60+.
- Field: official 2026 roster numbered through 801 entries across all distances (registration-list total, not verified starter/finisher count)
- Broadcast: livestreamed on the Tour de Mátra YouTube channel with Eurosport Hungary (ES Bringa) commentary — this is a YouTube stream carrying Eurosport-branded commentary, not a broadcast on the Eurosport TV channel itself
- International field: organizer/UCI materials describe riders from more than 15 countries in 2026
- Route not fully closed to traffic — some sections under full closure, others under police/marshal control; riders behind the sweep vehicle lose protected-race status
- No private team support vehicles or spare-wheel cars allowed — only organizer-run neutral assistance
- Time limits: 8 hours (161 km), 6 hours (94 km)

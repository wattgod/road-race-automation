# Research Dump: Tour de Romandie Cyclosportive (AUTHENTICITY INVESTIGATION)

Research date: 2026-07-24 (codex gpt-5.6-sol web-search session, ed4 editorial batch)

## Verdict: FABRICATED / DOES NOT EXIST

The exact database event — "Tour de Romandie Cyclosportive," 160.9 km / 2,591 m, Lausanne, 28 April 2026, founded 1947 — does not appear to be real. Classified as a fabricated composite record, high confidence.

## Evidence it is fabricated
1. **28 April 2026 was the professional Tour's opening day, not a location match.** The official 2026 pro route ran 28 April - 3 May 2026, beginning with a prologue in Villars-sur-Glâne, NOT Lausanne. Official host locations: Villars-sur-Glâne, Martigny, Rue/Vucherens, Orbe, Broc/Charmey, Lucens/Leysin — no Lausanne stage. https://www.tourderomandie.ch/en/tour-de-romandie-2026/
2. **Public entry was never permitted.** Article 2 of the official 2026 regulations restricts the race to Elite Men and Under-23 Men as part of the UCI WorldTour. No companion adult sportive or public-registration section exists in the programme. https://www.tourderomandie.ch/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Programme_TDR2026_web.pdf
3. **1947 belongs only to the professional race.** Official history: conceived 1946 for the Swiss cycling federation's 50th anniversary; first edition (10 invited pro teams of 4 riders) in 1947. No indication of a parallel amateur/citizen event. https://www.tourderomandie.ch/en/about-tdr/
4. **The vitals look synthetically generated.** 100.0 miles converts to exactly 160.9 km; 8,500 ft converts to exactly 2,591 m — a suspiciously tidy imperial "century ride" figure converted to metric, not a documented Swiss course. No official route, results, registration page, or historical listing matches this combination.
5. **Every citation previously in the profile concerns the professional race only** (Wikipedia, tourderomandie.ch, Cyclingnews, 2025 Tour de Romandie Wikipedia page) — none supports a public cyclosportive, course, Lausanne location, or "amateur origin story."

## What DID historically exist (related but distinct — do not conflate)
A "Tour de Romandie populaire" was a real, separate, now-defunct amateur cyclosportive series:
- Proposed by Tour director Richard Chassot in 2011 as a multi-event series. https://doc.rero.ch/record/77104/files/2011-03-26.pdf
- Operating by 2013, documented through 2016. 2013: "Classement Tour de Romandie Populaire 2013" tied to the Verbier-La Romandie Classic (https://services.datasport.com/2013/velo/tdrclassic/regionch_ge.htm). 2014 series: events at Lucens, Aigle-Leysin, Charmey (https://www.mso.swiss/events?filterEndDate=2014-12-31&filterStartDate=2014-1-1).
- 2015: organized by Chassot Concept, grouping three cyclosportives — Tour du Lac de Neuchâtel, Romandie Classic (Aigle-Leysin), Gruyère Cycling Tour (Charmey). Registrations hosted under the Tour de Romandie site's "cyclosportive" section. https://www.neuchatelville.ch/fileadmin/sites/ne_ville/fichiers/presse/Journal_N_/imported/2015/No18_03_06_2015_LOW_20_3_.pdf
- 2016: MSO still lists a "Tour de Romandie populaire 2016" series at Neuchâtel and Charmey. https://www.mso.swiss/events?filterEndDate=2016-12-31&filterStartDate=2016-1-1
- A later Chassot Concept retrospective describes the series in the past tense (three events, May-September), confirming it is now defunct. https://fr.readkong.com/page/presentation-chassot-concept-8270750
- NO evidence this historical series ever had a Lausanne edition, a 160.9 km course, a 2,591 m elevation figure, or a 1947 founding date. It is a genuinely different thing from the database record under review, and should NOT be used to relabel this profile as "the historical Tour de Romandie populaire" — that was an umbrella series of separately-named events at Neuchâtel/Aigle-Leysin/Charmey, not a single Lausanne century ride.

## Other real things easily confused with this profile (all ruled out)
- **P'tit Tour de Romandie**: current official kids' activity, ~15 invited children per stage, ages 10-14, untimed. Not an adult mass-participation sportive. https://www.tourderomandie.ch/en/le-ptit-tour-de-romandie/
- **Vélosophe Cool Rides**: sponsor-organized social rides that have occasionally accompanied Tour stages (e.g. Aigle-Torgon before the pros). Not a 100-mile Tour de Romandie sportive. https://www.cycliste.ch/alain-rumpf/lalpe-dhuez-du-tour-de-romandie/
- **Cyclotour du Léman**: the genuine large Lausanne-based cyclosportive (176 km full circuit) — separate history and branding from Tour de Romandie entirely. See research-dumps/cyclotour-du-leman.md. https://www.lausanne-tourisme.ch/en/event/cyclotour-du-leman/

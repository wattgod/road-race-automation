# Research Dump: Eroica South Africa

## Quick Facts
- **Status: DEFUNCT.** No 2026 (or any future) edition exists. Bicycling South Africa stated outright on 2025-01-27: "Eroica South Africa is no more," introducing "il Classico" as a substitute event.
- Absent entirely from eroica.cc's current official event roster (which already carries dates into 2027 for Japan, Dolomiti, Germania, Gaiole, Montalcino, Valkenburg).
- Last verified edition ran 12-14 April 2024 in Montagu, Western Cape.
- Founded 2016 by local organizer Stan Engelbrecht, as part of Eroica's official 2016 international expansion (alongside Eroica Limburg and Eroica Punta del Este). Eroica founder Giancarlo Brocci attended the inaugural edition and publicly praised it.
- Ran for roughly eight conventional editions plus one modified/special edition, 2016-2024 (2016, 2018, 2019, 2020, 2021-special, 2022, 2023, 2024).

## Identity/sanctioning check
This was a genuine, officially licensed Eroica-series edition — NOT a fabricated or unofficial name-borrowing ride. Confirmed via Eroica's own published history (naming South Africa explicitly as a 2016 expansion market), contemporary trade press (Velo/Outside) listing it alongside California, Japan, Hispania, Britannia, and Limburg as the same wave of official 2016 additions, founder Brocci's attendance, and Eroica's own archived event journal/poster shop carrying separate South Africa 2018/2019 artwork. The precise commercial license contract was not located, but the sanctioning is conclusively established by the organizer relationship, not merely inferred.

One material rule variance: South Africa relaxed the vintage-bike cutoff to **pre-1999 steel road bikes** (vs. the mainline Eroica pre-1987 standard) due to a smaller local vintage-bike pool. The 2024 Hero format still required toe clips/traditional pedals, downtube shifters, drop bars; wool/period-style clothing was encouraged, not enforced.

## Course history (no single canonical course — changed materially by edition)
| Edition | Formats/distances |
|---|---|
| 2016 (inaugural) | Kingna 50 km, Kogman 90 km, Keisie 140 km (~100 km gravel + ~40 km paved passes); 142 entrants, 41 on the long route |
| 2018 | Kingna 45 km, Kogman 90 km, Keisie Classic 135 km, + new Nova Eroica 155 km (modern gravel bikes) |
| 2019 | Kingna 45 km, Kogman 91 km, Keisie 135 km, Nova 155 km |
| 2020 | Kingna 45 km/478 m, Kogman 90 km/1,165 m, Keisie 135 km; ~1/3 tar, rest rough gravel; €45 entry |
| 2021 (special format) | Modified "choose-your-own-adventure" ride, 24 April, picnic collection points + 4 timed Strava sections for Nova riders — not a fixed-distance course |
| 2022 | ~90 km Classic day + 170 km Hero/Nova day via Ouberg Pass, Hoek-om, Nouga Farm, Lettas Kraal |
| 2023 | ~40/90/170 km options; longest ~2,000 m climbing |
| **2024 (final edition)** | Classic 68/70 km, Hero 170/173 km, Nova 170 km; a detailed rider account recorded the Hero long route as **172 km / 1,978 m**; ZAR 1,200+ entry (Classic/Hero); roughly 150 total riders observed (not an organizer-published field count) |

Surface throughout was predominantly gravel/dirt farm roads and Karoo terrain, with paved sections (roughly one-third tar on the longer routes in later editions) — NOT "Paved roads" as previously recorded. The prior database vitals (130.4 km / 2,499 m) match no verified edition; the profile now uses the last-verified 2024 Hero figures (172 km / 1,978 m) as the most complete final-course record, with the historical variance flagged in `catalog_flags`.

## Duplicate/rebrand/successor notes
"Eroica Classic South Africa," "Eroica South Africa Hero," and "Nova Eroica South Africa" were route/format options within the same Montagu weekend, not separate duplicate events. The practical successor event is **il Classico** (Riebeek Kasteel, organized by Dirty South Events/Ronde Series) — explicitly created as a substitute after Eroica South Africa ended, but it is NOT an official Eroica-licensed rebrand or continuation.

## Dedupe determination
No duplicate slug in the existing 425-race corpus. `eroica-hispania` (owned by another batch this wave) and `eroica-germania` (this same batch) are confirmed genuinely distinct licensed editions — no conflict.

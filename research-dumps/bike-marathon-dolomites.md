# Research Dump: HERO Sudtirol Dolomites (MTB Marathon)

## IMPORTANT: Disambiguation

The name "Bike Marathon Dles Dolomites" / "Dolomites Marathon" does NOT correspond to a distinct, separate cycling event. Extensive research confirms:

- **"Maratona dles Dolomites"** (Ladin) literally translates to **"Marathon of the Dolomites"** — the Ladin word *maratona* = marathon, *dles* = of the. "Bike Marathon dles Dolomites" is simply an English/Ladin hybrid translation of the same event.
- The Maratona dles Dolomites - Enel (road gran fondo, 138km/4,230m, 7 passes, La Villa start) already has a full research dump at `maratona-dles-dolomites.md`.
- There is NO separate event officially called "Bike Marathon Dles Dolomites."

The closest **distinct** event to that name is the **HERO Sudtirol Dolomites**, an MTB marathon in the same Dolomites region. This research dump covers that event, as the most likely intended target if the user wanted something different from the Maratona.

Other Dolomite MTB marathons considered and ruled out:
- **Dolomiti Superbike** (Villabassa, 123km/3,400m, est. 1994) — different name pattern
- **KronplatzKing** (Plan de Corones, 72km/3,150m) — different name pattern
- **Marathon Dolomiti Friulane** (Friuli, separate region) — different name pattern

## Course Profile

### Marathon Route (86 km / 4,500 m)

The marathon route circumnavigates the Sella massif, traversing four Ladin valleys (Val Gardena, Val Badia, Arabba/Livinallongo, Val di Fassa) and crossing the Seiser Alm (Alpe di Siusi), Europe's largest high-altitude alpine meadow.

**Key Passes (in order):**

1. **Passo Gardena / Dantercepies** (2,121 m) — First major climb from Selva. Off-road ascent via MTB trails, not the standard road route. Mixed forest roads and singletrack.

2. **Passo Campolongo** (1,875 m) — Descent into Corvara then climb toward Arabba. Technical sections through forest trails.

3. **Passo Pordoi** (2,239 m) — Major climb from Arabba. Off-road variant using mountain trails rather than the paved road. Steep pitches.

4. **Passo Sella** (2,244 m) — Final major pass. The approach from Lupo Bianco to Passo Sella covers 3.6 km with 510 m elevation gain.

5. **Sourasass** — The "roof" of the race. Gradients reaching **30%** on certain pitches. Many riders forced to push bikes on foot. This is the signature crux of the HERO.

**Terrain Breakdown:**
- Mix of forest roads, gravel double-track, singletrack, and rock gardens
- Partially paved sections (brief linking roads between off-road segments)
- Technical descents on singletrack with rock gardens requiring advanced bike handling
- Sections above 2,000 m with exposed alpine terrain
- Altitude range: ~1,200 m (valley floor) to ~2,400 m (high points)

**Surface Quality:**
- Forest roads: generally well-maintained gravel/hardpack, typical South Tyrolean quality
- Singletrack: rooty, rocky, technical — rated intermediate to advanced
- Some sections may be muddy depending on recent precipitation
- Road crossings on well-paved Dolomite pass roads (brief)
- Rock gardens on descents demand full-suspension or high skill on hardtail

### Classic Route (60 km / 3,200 m)

Same start/finish in Selva. Shares the core Sellaronda loop but shortcuts certain sections. Still features Gardena, Campolongo, Pordoi, and Sella passes. Same terrain mix. Described as "still extremely challenging" — this is not a beginner-friendly alternative.

**Featured Climb (Classic):** Lupo Bianco to Passo Sella — 3.6 km / 510 m D+.

### Performance Data
- Fastest finishers (86 km): approximately 4 hours 30 minutes
- 2011 winner Mirko Celestino: 4h 26m 07s
- Women's winner (2011) Katrin Schwing: 5h 59m 09s
- Average recreational finisher: 7-10 hours

### Getting to Val Gardena
- **By car:** A22 Brenner Motorway, exit Chiusa/Klausen or Bolzano Nord, then ~20-30 min on mountain roads to Ortisei/S. Cristina/Selva
- **By train:** Nearest stations are Chiusa (Klausen) or Bolzano; Bus 350 runs every 30-60 min into Val Gardena (~1 hour)
- **Val Gardena Mobil Card:** Hotel guests receive free public transit throughout South Tyrol for duration of stay

### Accommodation
- Three villages in Val Gardena: **Ortisei** (1,236 m), **Santa Cristina** (1,428 m), **Selva** (1,563 m)
- Range from budget garni/B&Bs to 5-star hotels
- Notable cycling-oriented: Linder Cycling Hotel, Hotel Granbaita Dolomites, Boutique Hotel Nives, Hotel Freina
- **Book early** — mid-June is peak season and the HERO weekend fills accommodation quickly
- Typical hotel rates: EUR 80-250/night depending on class

### Race-Day Logistics
- Bib pickup at race expo (dates TBC, typically 2 days before race)
- Registration includes: personal race number, mechanical assistance during race (spare parts extra), refreshment stations, event shirt, pasta party voucher, SMS/email race classification, printable certificate
- Mechanical support on course (spare parts not included in fee)
- Refreshment stations at multiple points along course
- Start at Selva Val Gardena, finish at same location

### Feed Zones
- Multiple refreshment stations along both routes
- Sports nutrition and hydration provided
- Pasta party at finish included in registration

## History

### Origin Story
- **2010:** First edition held June 26, 2010, as the "Sellaronda Hero." 400 riders from Selva Val Gardena. Conceived as the ultimate MTB endurance challenge in the Dolomites.
- **Winner (2010):** Klaus Fontana (men), Anna Ferrari (women)
- Named for the heroic challenge of circumnavigating the Sella massif entirely off-road

### Growth Milestones
| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 2010 | First edition, 400 participants |
| 2011 | Growth continues; Mirko Celestino wins in 4h26m07s |
| 2014 | 5th edition; Colombian Leonardo Paez and German Elisabeth Brandau win |
| ~2018 | Participant cap reaches ~4,000; 45+ countries represented |
| 2019 | Cumulative: 17,669 riders across 9 editions, 59 countries |
| 2024 | Record 4,014 participants from 40+ nations; receives UCI Climate Action Award |
| 2025 | Becomes namesake of the HERO UCI Cross-country Marathon World Cup — global circuit of 6 rounds |
| 2026 | 16th edition planned for June 13; part of UCI Marathon World Cup calendar |

### Notable Developments
- Evolved from local South Tyrolean event to flagship of global UCI marathon circuit
- Val Gardena bidding to host UCI MTB Marathon World Championships 2030
- Consistent growth capped only by logistical limits of the mountain terrain
- Awarded "Green Event" status for sustainability practices
- Has attracted elite MTB professionals alongside ~4,000 amateur riders

## Climate (Mid-June, Val Gardena / Selva at 1,564 m)

### Temperature
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average daily high | 18C / 64F |
| Average daily low | 7C / 44F |
| Summit temperatures (2,200+ m) | 5-12C / 41-54F |
| Pre-dawn start conditions | Potentially near freezing at altitude |

### Precipitation
- June is the **wettest month** in Val Gardena: ~149 mm over ~19 rainy days
- **61% chance of rain** on any given day
- Typical pattern: clear mornings with clouds building in early afternoon; thunderstorms likely by mid-to-late afternoon
- Mountain weather highly variable — sunny valleys can coexist with storms on passes
- Snow possible above 2,500 m in exceptional cold snaps (rare but documented)

### Other Conditions
- Humidity: ~90% average in June
- Daylight: ~15.8 hours (sunrise ~5:20 AM, sunset ~9:05 PM)
- UV intensity: HIGH at altitude — sunscreen essential
- Wind: Generally light in valleys, can be strong and gusty on exposed passes

### Race Implications
- Riders should prepare for temperatures ranging from 5C (passes, early morning) to 25C+ (sunny valley sections mid-day)
- Rain gear and arm/leg warmers essential even if forecast looks clear
- Mud significantly increases course difficulty on singletrack sections
- Lightning risk on exposed high-altitude sections during afternoon storms

## Community & Culture

### Atmosphere
- Described as "the world's most spectacular mountain bike marathon"
- Strong Ladin cultural identity — Val Gardena is one of the five Ladin valleys in the Dolomites
- Festival atmosphere with race expo, pasta party, live music
- Mix of elite professionals (UCI World Cup points at stake) and passionate amateurs on same course
- South Tyrolean hospitality: local food, wine, and culture prominently featured

### International Participation
- 45+ countries represented at peak editions
- ~4,000 participants per edition (hard cap)
- Growing amateur category draws international recreational riders
- Strong contingents from Italy, Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and increasingly Colombia, Belgium, Spain

### Pro Field / Field Depth
- UCI Cross-country Marathon World Cup round since 2025
- Notable elite riders: Hector Leonardo Paez (COL, double UCI World Champion), Fabian Rabensteiner (ITA, 2024 overall winner), Wout Alleman (BEL), Casey South (SUI), Samuele Porro (ITA), Gioele De Cosmo (ITA), Marc Stutzmann (SUI)
- Women's field includes international marathon specialists
- Amateurs race same course as elites — shared experience is a major draw

### Media Coverage
- UCI official coverage via uci.org
- HERO World Series media hub
- DMT Cycling, Cycloworld, Battistrada coverage
- Italian cycling media (QUI MTB Magazine, sportnews.bz)
- Social media: @herodolomites (Instagram)
- Growing global visibility since UCI World Cup naming rights

### Sustainability
- UCI Climate Action Award (2024)
- Green Event certified
- Focus on waste reduction, carbon offsetting, resource management
- Aligns with UNESCO World Heritage site stewardship ethos

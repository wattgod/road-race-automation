# Research Dump: Tour o' the Borders

## Quick Facts
- Location: Peebles, Scottish Borders, Scotland. Event HQ / rider-pack collection at Tweed Green, Peebles, EH45 8AP.
- 2026 edition: confirmed for Sunday, September 6, 2026. Event HQ open Saturday, September 5, 10:00-19:00 (no Sunday-morning pack collection).
- Two route options: Full 120 km / 1,200 m; Challenge 88 km / 940 m (organizer's detailed page also gives 88.8 km; registration rounds to 88 km).
- Field size: 2025 council-commissioned report records 2,234 participants; organizer states capacity up to 2,500.
- NOT literally "fully closed roads." It is marketed and legally managed as closed-road, but: Moffat town centre is an untimed, neutralized open-road section on the Full route; agricultural/local-service vehicles may operate around Talla and Megget on the Challenge route; the final section east of Peebles uses traffic-management measures/local access; riders behind the 16 km/h minimum may continue after roads reopen. The 2025 council report itself identifies these shared-access points while still calling the event closed-road overall — "predominantly closed-road, with controlled-access and neutralized exceptions" is the accurate framing.
- Organizer: Hillside Outside Ltd (local). 2026 entry: GBP 84 standard, GBP 89 with postal rider-pack delivery. Official partner of Sir Chris Hoy's Tour de 4 charity initiative; 2026 beneficiary charities: Macmillan, Prostate Cancer UK, Maggie's, Breast Cancer Now, Cure Leukaemia. Hillside Outside also funds a local community fund, the Touro Trust.
- Course highlights — Full route: source of the River Tweed, Devil's Beeftub, descent to Moffat, Moffat Dale, Grey Mare's Tail, St Mary's Loch, Paddy Slacks. Challenge route: the Wall of Talla, Talla Reservoir, Megget Reservoir, St Mary's Loch.
- IMPORTANT organizational history: the event was NOT held in 2024, following prolonged resident/business objections to road closures (documented by road.cc reporting from June 2023 and the organizer's own February 2024 update). It returned in 2025. The 2025 survey's main participant complaint themes were food/drink queue/setup (12%), parking/access delays (11%), and closure/local disruption (4%) — no evidence of a weather cancellation in 2025; that edition ran successfully.
- URLs: official_site https://tourotheborders.com/event-info/; registration https://www.sientries.co.uk/event.php?event_id=16249; homepage https://tourotheborders.com/

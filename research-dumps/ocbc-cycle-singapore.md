## Route options (2026)
- Sportive Ride ~40km — standard mass ride, closed roads
- Foldie Ride by Brompton ~40km — same route concept, any folding-bike brand
- The Straits Times Ride ~20km — shorter closed-road category
- Brompton World Championship — competitive 800m circuit, up to 5 laps
- Kids' rides, plus 100km virtual and Round Island virtual categories (latter
  requires 120km+ in one attempt)
All three mass categories sold out.

## Course character
Fully paved, urban/highway-style closed-road ride, NOT a climbing gran fondo.
2026 route used the CBD, Benjamin Sheares Bridge, Singapore Flyer, West Coast
Highway, Port of Singapore, finishing in the National Stadium. Generally flat
to gently rolling; principal effort is Benjamin Sheares Bridge and elevated
highway ramps. Crowd management among thousands of mixed-ability riders is
arguably a bigger factor than terrain.
Source: https://ocbccycle.com/routes/

## Organizer & history
Inaugural event 22 Feb 2009, F1 Pit Building start, 5,400 cyclists. Spectrum
Worldwide originally organized; OCBC Bank was title sponsor. Grew to 9,100
(2010), 11,500 (2014). OCBC changed organizer and moved to Singapore Sports
Hub in 2015. For 2026, OCBC is event owner, PICH Event Management is
organizer/manager; sanctioned by Singapore Cycling Federation, supported by
Sport Singapore.
Source: https://www.ocbc.com/group/media/release/2014/ocbc-cycle-my-change-organiser

## Credible criticisms — two real, documented incidents
1. 2014 fatality: a 24-year-old participant died from head injuries after
   crashing during the former 59km Super Challenge category. Reporting
   highlighted Singapore's lack of standardized mass-endurance-event safety
   requirements at the time. The following edition added ~S$300,000 in safety
   measures (experienced safety riders) and eliminated the 59km category.
   Source: https://www.todayonline.com/sports/safety-mass-events-under-scrutiny-after-cyclists-death
2. 2025 physical-event cancellation: shortly after Singapore's general
   election date was announced for the same weekend, organizers converted the
   physical event to virtual-only because security resources for road
   closures were unavailable. Full refunds issued, but overseas riders and
   racing teams publicly criticized non-refundable travel costs and the late
   notice. Physical event returned in 2026.
   Source: https://www.straitstimes.com/sport/ocbc-cycle-on-may-3-4-will-not-proceed-event-to-offer-virtual-rides-instead

## Verdict values used

- Distance: 48km (organizer-advertised; no decimal-precision GPX published). Source: https://event.thaimtb.com/V3/event-detail.php?e=736&lang=EN
- Elevation: ~2,580m route-estimate climbing gain. The DB's prior 2,565m figure is Doi Inthanon's summit elevation above sea level (source: https://botany.dnp.go.th/detail_eng.html?menu=flora_Eng), NOT a verified ascent total — the organizer does not publish one. Best route-specific GPX estimate: https://cyclingchiangmai.cc/doi-inthanon (48km/2,580m).
- Field size 2026: 870 registered (679 Challenge + 191 Race) against a 1,500-rider cap. Source: https://event.thaimtb.com/V3/event-detail.php?e=736&lang=EN
- Historical field size: 5,939 registrations in 2019 (https://event.thaimtb.com/event.php?e=355), ~7,500 advertised in 2018 (https://event.thaimtb.com/event.php?e=209). The prior "thousands" label described this older, larger version of the event.
- Founded: 2008, per a January 2010 contemporary report calling that year's race the "third annual." Source: https://crouchingtigertours.blogspot.com/2010/01/. Not ~2009-2010 as previously estimated.
- Organizer: สมาคมจักรยานเพื่อสุขภาพจอมทอง (Chom Thong Cycling for Health Association).
- Edition #17 in 2026 is consistent with the documented numbering sequence (2017=#10, 2018=#11, 2019=#12, 2020=#13, COVID-delayed #14 held 2023, then #15-17 in 2024-2026).

## Course character

Gradient breakdown from https://cyclingchiangmai.cc/doi-inthanon:
- First 8km: flat warm-up
- Park gate: 1.3km at 7% (touching 13%)
- ~10km rolling/false-flat at ~3%
- km 20: 6km at 7% (ramps to 14%) through gorge past Wachirathan waterfall
- 3km respite, then 3km at 9% to Ban Khun Klang/km 31
- 2 easier km, then 3.3km at 10% (incl. ~800m at 16%)
- From ~km 39: final 9km averages ~9%, incl. 3.5km at 14%, max pitches ~25%
- SBS 2017 race report independently confirms gentle opening kilometers and final-km pitches touching 23% above 2,000m: https://www.sbs.com.au/sport/article/racing-to-the-top-of-thailand-the-doi-inthanon-challenge/0w4rg5k6u

## Weather

TMD race-day forecast for 8 Feb 2026: Chiang Mai 18-34°C, northern mountaintops 5-14°C, morning fog. Source: https://www.tmd.go.th/media/forecast/daily-forecast/2026-02-08_06_thai_1.pdf (forecast, not observed reading).

## Documented weaknesses/criticisms (all historical, not necessarily 2026-specific)

- Roads only semi-closed as of 2017 (SBS Sport report above) — traffic built up behind front riders.
- 2023 edition's full two-lane closure drew complaints from locals/visitors over short (4-day) advance notice: https://mgronline.com/sport/detail/9660000012519
- 2018 fatality: a rider descending near km 40-41 collided with an uphill-traveling truck and later died. Source: https://www.thairath.co.th/news/local/north/1202255. This is a documented, real safety incident — included in the profile as an honest fact, not sensationalized, and not implied to be a yearly occurrence.
- Heat/cold contrast between valley and summit is well documented across multiple years.

No solidly sourced 2026-specific complaint was found regarding timing, aid stations, packet pickup, pavement, or medical coverage.

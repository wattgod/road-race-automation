# Research Dump: Raúl Alcalá Challenge Oaxaca

## Quick Facts
- Parent series: Raúl Alcalá Challenge, a multi-city Mexican gran fondo/sportive series honoring retired Mexican pro Raúl Alcalá Gallegos (two-time Tour de France stage winner, 1989 and 1990), founded 2012
- Oaxaca leg specifically: debuted 2022 (billed at the time as the series' 10th-anniversary edition and Oaxaca's first hosting), originally styled "Raúl Alcalá Challenge Oaxaca Mayordomo" after a local title sponsor — not a separate race
- **2026 Oaxaca status: CANCELLED.** The only specific 2026 Oaxaca calendar listing (19 April 2026) explicitly says "Fecha anulada" (date cancelled)
- The wider series is NOT defunct — it continues in other Mexican cities. The April 2025 series date that would have been Oaxaca's slot instead ran in San Luis Potosí (2,000+ riders), with Morelos and Nuevo León rounding out the 2025 circuit — Oaxaca dropped off the 2025/2026 rotation
- Last confirmed COMPLETED Oaxaca edition: 2023 (Diario Marca post-event coverage); a 2024 registration/regulations page also exists (marcate.com.mx) but this research did not confirm 2024 actually ran
- Distances varied by year: 70/100 km (2022 and per the 2024 registration page), 70/110 km (2023 completed edition) — not distance-stable year to year. The previously-filed 109.4 km/68 mi figure has no direct source; the organizer's own 2022 Strava route measures 103.45 km/64.3 mi with 703 m/2,306 ft climbing — the strongest sourced distance/elevation pair available
- Field size: 300+ riders (2022 inaugural), 500 riders (2023) — modest, regional-scale event, not a large fondo
- Surface: paved road/sportive — Federal Highway 190 and city streets, open to both road and MTB bikes but not a gravel course

## Course Profile (historical, 2022-2023 editions)
- Start: central Oaxaca de Juárez, via Avenida Independencia/Boulevard Eduardo Vasconcelos to Federal Highway 190
- Short course turned near Yaguar Xoo/Tlacolula; long course continued through Tlacolula and Santiago Matatlán to Nueve Puntas, returning via Highway 190 to the Eduardo Vasconcelos baseball stadium finish
- 2023 route added a passage through Mitla
- Competitive/timed segment centered on Nueve Puntas (2022)

## Identity Check
Confirmed legitimate: this is the genuine mass-participation event tied to Raúl Alcalá Gallegos, a real two-time Tour de France stage winner (1989, 1990). It is a recreational gran fondo/sportive with timed competitive segments (age 15+, beginners through semi-professionals, in-kind prizes) — not a professional UCI race, not a different Alcalá-named event, and not a virtual/Zwift event (physical highway routing, mass start, timing chips, mechanical support, finisher medals all confirmed).

## Dedupe determination
No separate duplicate profile exists in the 425-race corpus for another city leg of this series (Morelos, León, Monterrey, San Luis Potosí, CDMX) — this profile is the sole record for the Oaxaca leg specifically. No dedupe action needed; this is a status/eligibility correction (cancelled leg within a still-active series), not a duplicate-record problem.

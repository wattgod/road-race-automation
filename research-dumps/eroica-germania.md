# Research Dump: Eroica Germania

## Quick Facts
- Official series event page: https://www.eroica.cc/en/events/germania/ — confirmed, active, 2026 edition scheduled Saturday, August 22, 2026.
- German licensee/producer: die wellenmaschine, holding the German Eroica license since 2017; first official edition ran August 2018 (test/prologue in 2017).
- Format: a non-competitive historical ride on period-correct bicycles (1987-or-earlier road-racing bikes, typically steel frames, downtube shifters, external brake cables, toe clips), not a timed race. Classically inspired clothing encouraged (wool jerseys archetypal, not an absolute requirement).
- Host: start on the central square in Erbach, finish/festival ~850m away at Weingut Baron Knyphausen (Draiser Hof). Erbach is a district of the municipality of Eltville am Rhein — "Eltville," "Eltville-Erbach," and "Erbach" all describe the same host area.

## Course Profile (2026)
- **Long route: 122 km / 2,150 m D+**, 3 feed stops, 07:00 start. (Prior database figure of 130.4 km / 2,148 m is stale — that was the ~2019/2023-era long-course distance; the organizer's current structured route field says 122 km.)
- **Medium route: 80 km / 1,290 m D+**, 2 feed stops, 08:00 start.
- **Short route: 42.8 km / 600 m D+**, 1 feed stop, 08:30 start.
- Note: the organizer's own page is internally inconsistent — narrative copy states "~2,400-2,500 m" of climbing while the structured route field says 2,150 m for the long route. Using the explicit route-field figure per the official long-route/medium-route pages.
- Surface: mixed asphalt and gravel/unpaved ("white road") sectors per official regulations — NOT "Paved roads" as previously recorded. The organizer does not publish a gravel-vs-asphalt km breakdown.
- Route character: leaves Erbach through Rheingau vineyards, past the Schloss Johannisberg landscape, descends on asphalt through forest before harder climbing; long route continues into the Rhine-Taunus Nature Park. No named climbs or gradients are officially published.
- A 2023 participant ride report (RTC Stuttgart) describes coarse, washed-out gravel climbs reaching ~16% and narrow muddy forest sectors — useful color for rider-experience framing but not a guaranteed 2026 course feature (not independently verified against the current route).
- Entry fee (as of 2026-07-24): €90 regular, rising to €99 Aug 1-15; Eroica Cycling Club members €45 (€49.50 late).
- Field size: 1,000 riders is the advertised 2026 target per a regional Rheingau event listing — not a documented hard registration cap on the official registration page.
- Founding year: 2018 (official premiere), not left blank.

## Identity check
Confirmed real, distinct, officially licensed Eroica edition — die wellenmaschine states it has held the German license since 2017; the event has its own official page on eroica.cc and appears in Eroica's current "Classic" event catalog alongside Dolomiti, Japan, Gaiole, Montalcino, and Valkenburg.

Two mislabeled YouTube entries were found under this profile and must be purged:
1. "Eroica Dolomiti 2023 – Official Video" — confirmed footage of the separate Eroica Dolomiti event (San Candido/Tre Cime, Italy), not Germania.
2. Euronews "Radklassiker L'Eroica: Technik von heute ist verpönt" — covers the original L'Eroica in Tuscany (Gaiole in Chianti, founded 1997), not Germania.

After removing both, this profile has zero verified Germania-specific video content.

Not to be confused with: Eroica Dolomiti (San Candido, Italy), the original L'Eroica (Gaiole in Chianti, Tuscany), Eroica Japan (Ibaraki Prefecture), Eroica Hispania (batch-owned by another wave crew — not touched here), or Eroica South Africa (separate profile in this same batch, found defunct — see eroica-south-africa.md).

## Dedupe determination
No duplicate-slug issue: eroica-germania is a distinct, existing slug in the corpus. `eroica-hispania` also exists as a separate slug (owned by a different batch this wave) — confirmed as a genuinely distinct licensed Eroica edition (Ureña/Spain), no conflict, not edited here.

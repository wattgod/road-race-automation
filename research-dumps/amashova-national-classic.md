## 4. "National" naming and governing-body status
- Confirmed official branding: homepage says "Welcome to the Amashova National Classic"; official CTCTT entry portal titled "Amashova National Classic 2026."
  - https://www.shova.co.za/ (updated 6 July 2026)
  - https://online.entries.co.za/entries/findperson.aspx?eid=5235&pa=se (accessed 24 July 2026)
- "National Classic" naming is historical — inaugural 1986 name was "Natal Mercury/Pick n Pay National Classic"; a 1993 name also ended in "Pietermaritzburg to Durban National Classic." https://www.shova.co.za/history/ (updated 12 Nov 2025)
- KZN Cycling's official 2026 calendar lists the event under the exact name "Amashova National Classic": https://kzncycling.co.za/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/KZN-Cycling-Calendar-2026-29-May-2026.pdf
- Organizer requires CSA membership or an R70 day licence, per Cycling South Africa rules — supports "CSA-rule-compliant/federation-calendar event" framing.
- **No primary CSA or UCI document confers a formal "national road classic/championship" designation.** The actual 2026 South African National Road Championships were a separate UCI-listed event, 5-8 February 2026: https://www.uci.org/competition-details/2026/ROA/78478
- **Editorial guidance**: safe framing is "the organizer brands it as South Africa's oldest road classic; it appears on the KZN Cycling calendar and operates under CSA licensing rules" — do NOT call it the officially designated national championship.

## 6. Correct course features and climbs (replacing fabricated/garbled labels)
- **"Fox Hill Grind" was not an official feature name.** Real feature: the **Alexander Road/Fox Hill climb** — organizer's history describes "the challenging 14km Alexander Road climb ... up Fox Hill to Thornville," introduced after 1993; the 2026 route description says riders turn into Alexander Road and "continue up Fox Hill."
  - https://www.shova.co.za/history/ (updated 12 Nov 2025); https://www.shova.co.za/route-description/ (updated 3 June 2026)
- **"Hills of a Thousand" should be deleted** — proper regional name is the **Valley of a Thousand Hills**; organizer directions use junction wording "Inchanga/Drummond/1000 Hills."
  - https://www.shova.co.za/route-description/ (updated 3 June 2026); https://www.bicycling.co.za/race-news/amashova-2025-draws-thousands/ (22 July 2025)
- **Cato Ridge** is a route locality/water-point, not a documented climb name — route passes through via Doonrug Drive and Selby Road toward Harrisons Flats.
- **Inchanga and the approach toward Botha's Hill** are genuine hilly sectors (per a 2022 rider report), but the official 2026 directions describe the section after Kearsney College as a **fast descent down Botha's Hill**, and **Fields Hill** is explicitly a descent on the M13 toward Durban, not a climb in race direction.
  - Berea Mail rider report: https://www.citizen.co.za/berea-mail/news-headlines/local-news/2022/11/04/grinding-the-gears-saint-giles-cyclist-beats-the-odds-at-amashova/ (4 Nov 2022)
  - Official route description: https://www.shova.co.za/route-description/ (updated 3 June 2026)
- Other correctly named landmarks: Comrades Marathon Wall of Honour, Kearsney College, Harrisons Flats, Inchanga, Drummond, Hillcrest, Jan Smuts Highway, the final crest beneath Tollgate Bridge, Monty Naicker Road, Masabalala Yengwa Avenue (finish area).

Suggested course copy: "Alexander Road/Fox Hill: the historic 14 km climb from Pietermaritzburg toward Thornville. Rolling R103 terrain follows through Cato Ridge, Harrisons Flats, Inchanga and the Valley of a Thousand Hills, passing the Comrades Wall of Honour before the fast Botha's Hill and Fields Hill descents. The last rise crests beneath Tollgate Bridge before the run into Durban."

## 1. Status and 2026 dates

Active/scheduled. Organizer has live registration, pricing and regulations. Competitive events run

Broader event/expo weekend runs December 17-19. The Dec 11-12 listing found in a prior automated check

Sources: https://gran-fondo.shvoong.co.il/, https://regi.shvoong.co.il/shvoong/page/17909?langid=0

## 2. Course — 140 km / 1,393-1,400 m

Current official route page: 140 km, 1,393 m climbing; regulations round to 1,400 m. Only 116.8 km /
1,350 m is competitively timed (descent back to Eilat is neutralized). Feed zones at Neot Smadar, Camp
Shagor (Route 12), and the finish — not "chase cars." Team cars are prohibited; neutral support runs
front and rear. Source: https://gran-fondo.shvoong.co.il/?page_id=3108, ?page_id=13

## 3. Course character

Real desert terrain, mixed rather than alpine. ~52 km flat on Arava Road/Route 90, then the principal
climb Mt. Ayit/Ketura toward Neot Smadar (4.2 km at 6.5% avg, max 14%), then ~60 km of exposed rolling
hills on Route 12 (locally "bathtubs"), finishing around Netafim Pass/Mount Yoash with a neutral descent.

## 4. Field, organizer, UCI, history

Organizer: Shvoong Sport Events, with Israel Cycling Federation, Eilat Municipality, Astral Hotels.
Shvoong took over production in 2025. Eilat edition began 2021, ran again 2022, paused, returned 2025.
2023 was intended as a UCI Gran Fondo World Series qualifier debut but was cancelled due to the war.
2026 event is NOT on the current UCI Gran Fondo World Series calendar
(https://ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/calendar/). Recent participation ~500 in both 2022 and 2025
(https://sports.walla.co.il/item/3546564); no support for "1000+".

## 5. Prestige

Serious Israeli national/regional road event — closed roads, electronic timing, prize money, federation
involvement, leading Israeli racers. Not yet an internationally established marquee gran fondo: ~500-rider
field, three completed editions, a long interruption, no current UCI series affiliation.

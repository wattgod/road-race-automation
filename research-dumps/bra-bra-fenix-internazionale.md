# Research Dump: Bra-Bra Fenix Internazionale

## Quick Facts
- Official name family: "Granfondo Bra Bra," commonly styled "Bra-Bra Fenix" or "Fenix Bra-Bra Specialized" under current title/main sponsor split
- Organizer: A.S.D. Gran Fondo Bra Bra, based in Pollenzo, Bra, Piedmont, Italy
- Official site: https://brabra.org/
- Founded 1992 — first edition June 28, 1992, 560 entrants, won by Michele Pepino
- 33rd edition held May 17, 2026 (registration/Gravel Experience Sat May 16, competitive road event Sun May 17), start/finish in Pollenzo
- 2026 courses: Granfondo ~125-126 km / ~2,390 m; Mediofondo ~105 km / ~1,900 m — official materials are internally inconsistent by 1-2 km / ~40 m across pages (regulations say 125 km/105 km; timing table says 125.5 km; homepage says 124.72 km/2,375 m and 103.98 km/1,906 m; race-week press says ~126 km/2,390 m and ~105 km/1,900 m)
- The profile's prior 149.7 km figure is stale — matches the 2023-24 course (149 km/2,250 m), not 2026
- 2026 field: 2,000+ starters (nominal bib allocation up to 2,500 per official materials, which are internally contradictory on the cap)
- 2026 entry fee: tiered, €50 (through Dec 31 2025) rising to €70 on-site; Gold €120/Silver €90 packages; +€10 one-day license where needed
- Surface: paved road cycling (ENDU classifies it "Ciclismo su strada"); the unpaved "strade bianche" riding belongs to the separate Saturday Bra Bra Gravel Experience, not the Sunday Granfondo

## Critical safety incident — 2026 edition
During the May 17, 2026 edition, a participant died in a crash near Neviglie. Organizers suspended celebratory/collateral activities and stated there would be no further sporting communications; one local report describes the race itself as immediately suspended, while the organizer's quoted statement refers specifically to associated activities. Source: TargatoCN incident report (names the rider; profile does not repeat the name out of respect, but the fact and its sourcing are load-bearing for eligibility.notes and biased_opinion).

## Course Profile (2026)
Both routes start/finish in Pollenzo, cross the Roero, skirt the Monferrato, enter the Langhe/Alta Langa, split near Sinio, and reunite for a shared finale through Serralunga d'Alba, the Cedro del Libano, La Morra, Verduno, and back to Pollenzo. Named selection points: Bra/Orti, Monticello d'Alba, Guarene, Neive Borgo Alto, Neviglie, Trezzo Tinella, Benevello, the Dorsale delle Langhe, Tre Cunei, Albaretto Torre; Granfondo-only extension through Coste Pomo, Pedaggera, Serravalle Langhe, Cissone, the SP56/Serralunga sector, then Strada Paciarini to the Cedro del Libano (the shared signature/GPM segment) and La Morra. The formerly prominent Mur d'la Mura climb was removed for 2026 — its "up to 20%" gradient and short gravel-sector description from older profiles/editions should not be reused as current course fact. No reliable official 2026 per-climb gradient data exists; climb_profile stays unenriched rather than inventing figures.

## Name and sponsor history
One continuing event under evolving sponsor-heavy names, not separate races: "Granfondo Bra-Bra" historically, "Granfondo Internazionale Bra Bra Specialized"/"Bra-Bra Specialized" by 2017, "Bra-Bra Specialized – FENIX Gran Prix" from 2021 (28th edition), then in 2022 FENIX and Specialized swapped commercial positions (FENIX became title sponsor, Specialized became main sponsor) — a configuration the official site still reflects. Current aliases: "Bra Bra Fenix – Langhe Monferrato Roero," "Fenix Bra-Bra Specialized," legacy "Internazionale" form.

## Identity check
Confirmed: this is the real, long-running amateur road Granfondo based in Bra/Pollenzo, organized by A.S.D. Gran Fondo Bra Bra. Not a motorcycle race, not a Zwift/virtual event. Possible confusion sources noted: (1) the concurrent Saturday Bra Bra Gravel Experience is a separate, distinct ride; (2) a separate Bra Bra Running Fun footrace also happens race weekend; (3) "Fenix" is a commercial title sponsor, not a different event; (4) the official route page still uses a legacy filename (percorsi2025.aspx) despite a 2026 heading, which could confuse scrapers but does not indicate an identity problem.

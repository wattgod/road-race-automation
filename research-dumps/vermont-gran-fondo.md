# Research Dump: Vermont Gran Fondo

## Quick Facts
- Official site: https://vermontgranfondo.com/ — currently states "We're Taking a Break in 2026 — See You in 2027." This is a one-year organizer hiatus, NOT a permanent discontinuation.
- Last completed edition: June 28, 2025, in **Bristol, Vermont** (not Stowe — see location correction below).
- Founded 2014 (inaugural edition started at Middlebury College Snow Bowl in Hancock, 181 registrants/160 starters), not 2016 as previously recorded.
- Current organizer: **Ride It If You Can LLC** (Todd Warnock, event director), who took over from original producer Cycle Addison County, Inc. in 2018 and moved the event to Bristol in 2019.
- Partner event of the (Suarez) Gran Fondo National Series since 2022 — a partner relationship, not a rebrand/new event.
- Non-competitive supported ride overall; competition is limited to timed gap-climb segments. No racing license required.

## Location correction
Existing profile had `location: "Stowe, Vermont, USA"`. This is wrong. Current start/finish is **Bristol Rec Park/Club, 110 Airport Drive, Bristol, VT**, confirmed by the official 2025 event-info page and a Town of Bristol road-use permit letter. A likely source of the Stowe contamination: a third-party calendar mirror ("Avid Cyclist") misspells the event "Vermont Grand Fondo" and places it in Stowe while copying the official hiatus/LAMB copy verbatim — almost certainly a mislocated duplicate listing, not a second real event.

## Course Profile (last completed 2025 edition; four route options)
- **Gran (flagship)**: ~109 mi / 175 km, ~9,680 ft / 2,950 m climbing. Clockwise over Appalachian, Lincoln, Middlebury, and Brandon Gaps. Organizer's own page is internally inconsistent (also states "9,850+ ft"), so figures should carry an approximate qualifier.
- **Gran-X**: ~148.5 mi / 239 km, ~15,090 ft / 4,599 m. Adds Roxbury and Rochester Gaps to the Gran route.
- **Medio**: ~80 mi / 129 km, ~5,500 ft / 1,676 m. Crosses Middlebury and Brandon Gaps.
- **Piccolo**: ~40 mi / 64 km, ~2,650 ft / 808 m. No gap climb.
- **Surface**: mixed paved road with hard-packed dirt sectors — roughly 18 dirt miles on the Gran, 21.5 on Gran-X, 10 each on Medio/Piccolo (organizer's detailed figures); official FAQ recommends road or gravel bikes with 28-30mm+ tires. The prior "Paved roads" surface tag is incorrect.
- **Field size**: 371 registrations across all four routes in 2025 (BikeReg roster). Municipal planning docs describe a normal 300-500 participant event; an 850-person cap appears on some calendar mirrors but was not the actual 2025 field.
- **Entry fees (2025, last published)**: $85 Piccolo / $140 Medio / $160 Gran / $260 Gran-X.
- The previously stored 99.8 km / 1,463 m figure closely matches a stale 2018 "Medio Facile" course (62 mi / ~4,626 ft) — not the current flagship Gran.
- Existing suffering_zones text (7-mile Lincoln Gap, 23-mile Middlebury cutoff, "over 50 miles" Brandon finale) is not supported by official course data: Appalachian Gap comes first on the Gran, Lincoln Gap's steep east side is ~1.4 mi, and 7 miles is actually the listed length of Middlebury Gap on the Gran-X climb table.

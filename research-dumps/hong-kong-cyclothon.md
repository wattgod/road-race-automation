# Research Dump — Hong Kong Cyclothon (editorial wave 8, 2026-07-24)

Researched via codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, live web search) for GOLD-register editorial pass.

## Key verified facts

- **2026 date**: Sunday, October 11, 2026, but HKTB still labels it tentative. HKTB's sponsorship page: "Event Date (Tentative): 11 Oct 2026." The UCI calendar lists the professional Sun Hung Kai Properties Hong Kong Cyclothon as a class 1.1 race on 11 October 2026. The prior "November TBD" listing appears carried forward from the cancelled Nov 30, 2025 edition.
- **Distances**: Latest verified mass-participation program (2024, planned-then-cancelled 2025) was a 50km ride ("four tunnels, three bridges") and a 32km ride ("two tunnels, two bridges"). No official HKTB route document, FAQ, government traffic notice, GPX file, or elevation profile publishes 49.9km or 366m/1,200ft — those figures traced back only to this database's own prior content, not an independent source.
- **Route verification** (via Hong Kong Transport Department traffic notices for the 2024 and 2025 editions): 50km route runs Salisbury Road/Empire Centre start (Tsim Sha Tsui East) → West Kowloon Highway → Cheung Tsing/Cheung Ching Tunnel → Tsing Ma Bridge → Ting Kau Bridge → Nam Wan Tunnel → Stonecutters Bridge → Eagle's Nest Tunnel → Sha Tin Heights Tunnel → return to West Kowloon, finish at Jordan Road flyover. The 32km route uses Cheung Ching Tunnel, Nam Wan Tunnel, Ting Kau Bridge, and Stonecutters Bridge only (no Tsing Ma, Eagle's Nest, or Sha Tin Heights). Cheung Tsing Tunnel is part of Route 3; Nam Wan, Stonecutters, Eagle's Nest, and Sha Tin Heights belong to the Route 8/Tsing Sha system.
- **Organizer**: Hong Kong Tourism Board (HKTB), title/charity sponsor Sun Hung Kai Properties, technical cycling involvement from the Cycling Association of Hong Kong, China.
- **Founded**: 2015 (not 2014) — HKTB's own 2015-16 annual report calls the 2015 event the first-ever/debut Cyclothon, drawing 3,600+ participants from 17 countries/regions.
- **Cancellation/disruption history**: 2019 cancelled amid social unrest (Hong Kong government record); 2020-21 no edition during COVID; January 16, 2022 planned edition cancelled after government-ordered suspension of large-scale events; December 18, 2022 the event returned but strong winds (35+ km/h) forced removal of Tsing Ma and Ting Kau bridges mid-cycle, shortening the 50km route to roughly 36km; November 30, 2025 the entire edition was cancelled following the Wang Fuk Court fire in Tai Po, with HKTB/SHKP redirecting HK$15 million to the fire relief fund instead.
- **Field size / prestige**: 2024 (most recent completed edition) drew a record 6,000 riders and 20,000 spectators, with four-time Tour de France winner Chris Froome in the 50km mass-ride field. The event also has a genuine elite-racing component: the 2023 UCI 1.1 race drew nearly 100 professionals across 14 teams, including WorldTour and ProTeams, and the professional race is back on the 2026 UCI calendar as class 1.1.
- **Wind-rerouting rule**: Official rules allow rerouting/shortening the course based on measured bridge wind speeds — this is a real, documented operational policy, not editorializing, evidenced by the December 2022 shortening precedent.
- **Eligibility**: ACTIVE/PLANNED as of 2026-07-24 — HKTB lists a 2026 edition, the UCI lists an Oct 11 race, and HKTB is procuring venue setup/decorations for the 2026 Cyclothon per its own procurement page. Registration, final mass-ride date confirmation, 2026 route options, and elevation remain unpublished/TBD at research time.

## Fabrication audit of prior course-description content (all removed/rewritten this pass)

- "Tsing Ma Bridge Wind Tunnel" nickname, "headwinds on the 2km span," and the "1,200ft total gain" linkage: not supported by any event source. Tsing Ma's actual main span is 1.377km per Hong Kong Transport Department data, not 2km.
- "Back-to-back Cheung Ching, Nam Wan, and Eagle's Nest passages": misleading — these tunnels are not encountered in immediate succession on the actual route (Cheung Tsing comes before the western bridge section; Nam Wan and Eagle's Nest come later), and the copy omitted Sha Tin Heights Tunnel, which is also on the 50km route. Claims of "dim lighting," "echoing packs," and "heat buildup spikes" were not found in any official or credible rider source.
- "Final 10km through Kowloon traffic closures... rolling finishes": only the general Kowloon/Jordan Road flyover finish location is supported; no source confirms a "rolling" profile or humidity as the defining late-race factor.

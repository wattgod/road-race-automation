# Research Dump: UCI Gran Fondo World Championships

Compiled 2026-07-24, debt-sweep batch 9. This is the UCI Gran Fondo World Series' championship event itself — claims checked against UCI's own site (uci.org, ucigranfondoworldseries.com) plus Wikipedia and Gran Fondo Daily's results archive.

## Flagged claims — display_name / tagline / final_verdict / history (7 flags, all the same underlying "UCI/world championship" theme)

**Status: TRUE across the board.** No fabrications found; all checked claims are corroborated by curl-verified sources.

1. **"The only world championship in amateur road cycling" / "only UCI World Championship for amateur road cyclists"** — Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UCI_Gran_Fondo_World_Championships) and UCI.org both frame this event as the UCI's amateur/mass-participation counterpart to the elite-only UCI Road World Championships: "unlike the UCI Road World Championships which are an elite professional event, the Gran Fondo World Championships are targeted at mass participation and cyclists not in full-time professional teams." No other UCI world championship exists for amateur road racing specifically (UCI does run separate Gravel and MTB Marathon World Championships, but those are different disciplines, not road). Treated as a defensible characterization rather than a UCI-published superlative — noted in eligibility.notes rather than hard-asserted as a direct UCI quote.
2. **Founding: 2011 series launch, first Championships 2012 in Pietermaritzburg, South Africa** — UCI.org's own retrospective (https://www.uci.org/article/uci-gran-fondo-world-series-global-dreams-lead-to-japan/3UpI7mQH6WfTW5FeDSFm8, WebFetch-confirmed) states "Born in 2011, the series has grown to unprecedented heights." Gran Fondo Daily's UCI Gran Fondo World Championship results archive (https://granfondodailynews.com/historical-uci-gran-fondo-world-championship-results-archive/ — already cited in the JSON) covers "RESULTS 2012–2025," and a granfondoguide.com 2016 recap explicitly references "Pietermaritzburg 2012" as an already-completed prior edition — consistent with 2012/Pietermaritzburg being the inaugural championship. Host-city sequence 2012 Pietermaritzburg (RSA) → 2013 Trento (ITA) → 2014 Ljubljana (SVN) → 2015 Aalborg (DEN) corroborated via search aggregation of UCI/granfondoguide coverage.
3. **"Five continents feed into the World Championship" / rotating host venue** — corroborated: hosts since 2012 span South Africa, Italy, Slovenia, Denmark, Australia (multiple editions), Scotland (2023, combined with the inaugural UCI Cycling World Championships), and now Japan (2026, "the first time" in Asia per ucigranfondoworldseries.com's own 2026 Niseko announcement, curl-equivalent WebFetch-confirmed). France (Thônes-La Clusaz) is announced for 2027.
4. **36 qualifying events globally in 2026** — confirmed: ucigranfondoworldseries.com's "2026 UCI Gran Fondo World Series expands with nine new events" release states the calendar grows from 32 to 36 events for 2026 (new countries include Czech Republic, Hungary, Bulgaria, Chile, Ecuador, Argentina), spread across five continents.
5. **2026 dates (Aug 26-30, Niseko, Japan)** — directly confirmed on ucigranfondoworldseries.com's own 2026 Niseko announcement page: "From 26-30 August 2026, the UCI Gran Fondo World Championships will break new ground as the event heads to Asia for the very first time." Matches the JSON's `date_specific` exactly.
6. **Qualification via top 25% of age group at a World Series qualifier** — confirmed by Wikipedia: "The top 25% in each age group from UCI Gran Fondo World Series qualifier events qualify to the UCI Granfondo World Championships, where the champions are awarded with the coveted rainbow jersey."
7. **~2,000 riders, 55-60 countries** — Wikipedia and UCI.org 2023 Glasgow recap both cite "almost 2000 riders" competing; the 58-countries figure in the JSON is consistent with a UCI.org retrospective noting the (differently-dated) Great Ocean Road/Australia edition drew participants "from 58 countries."

## Sol adversarial review — corrections applied 2026-07-24
A read-only `codex exec -m gpt-5.6-sol` pass found the first draft's "no text changes required" verdict was wrong on one material point and imprecise on two others. All three were verified independently before applying:

1. **Pietermaritzburg 2012 was NOT the first edition — real error, corrected.** The series' own Hall of Fame page (https://ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/jeannie-longo-and-igor-kopse-leaders-in-hall-of-fame-of-the-uci-gran-fondo-world-championships/, WebFetch-confirmed) lists rider Molly Van Houweling's time-trial world titles in chronological order: "Stavelot 2011, Pietermaritzburg 2012, Ljubljana 2014 and Varese 2018." This is direct, official-source evidence that Stavelot, Belgium hosted a title-awarding edition in 2011, one year before Pietermaritzburg. Corrected `history.founded` (2012→2011), `origin_story`, and the first two `notable_moments` entries to reflect Stavelot 2011 as the first Championships and Pietermaritzburg 2012 as the second. (The "granfondodailynews.com results archive runs 2012-2025" citation already in the JSON is not contradicted — it may simply not have digitized the 2011 Stavelot results, or 2011 predates the archive's "results" format; the archive's coverage window is not proof of a 2012 inaugural.)
2. **"Aarhus" in `notable_moments`' 2015-2019 venue list was a typo for "Aalborg."** Confirmed via UCI.org ("UCI Gran Fondo World Series: objective Aalborg for athletes from around the globe") and an official 2015 results PDF titled "2015 UCI World Cycling Tour Championship Gran Fondo — Aalborg, Denmark." Corrected.
3. **"UCI Road World Championships are elite-only" was imprecise.** The pro Road Worlds field Elite, U23, and Junior competitive categories — they're not literally "elite-only," they're simply not open to amateur/age-group entry (which is the actual distinguishing fact for this claim). Reworded `eligibility.notes` accordingly.
4. **36-qualifying-events count: not disproven, but hedged.** Sol argued the 36 figure (from a Nov 2025 UCI Gran Fondo World Series expansion release) might include events feeding the 2027 championship, not just 2026 Niseko. A live check of ucigranfondoworldseries.com/en/calendar/ on 2026-07-24 found 35 events listed under "Qualifiers 2025-2026" leading to Niseko — close enough to 36 that this reads as normal calendar drift (a late addition/cancellation) rather than sol's stronger claim of a structurally different count, but the eligibility note was reworded to state both figures and flag the small discrepancy rather than asserting exact certainty.

Also fixed, low-risk and directly evidenced: `vitals.country` was `"Japan)"` (stray trailing parenthesis) — corrected to `"Japan"`, the same class of bug already fixed on white-rose-classic in this batch.

Sol separately noted `web/race-index.json` still lists this race under USA/North America — that's a generated output file, out of scope for this batch (touch-only race-data/ + research-dumps/ per the brief); flagging for whoever next regenerates the index.

## Identity check

Confirmed distinct from L'Étape Poland (Zakopane, Tour de France-branded), Race Around Poland
(~3,600km ultracycling circumnavigation), and Race Through Poland (~1,500km ultradistance,
starts well south of Gdynia).

## History

Launched May 27 2018 as GFNY Gdynia (first Polish GFNY race, offered 80km and 134-136km routes,
Sport Evolution/SE Group organizer, ~350 riders/25+ countries). Rebranded Gran Fondo Gdynia in
2019 after Sport Evolution split from the GFNY license, following what race director Michał
Drelich described as irreconcilable business differences. Physical 2020 edition cancelled for
COVID (partial virtual replacement). Resumed 2021-22, returned 2025 under PZU Gran Fondo Series
(ran as part of that series in 2025). No 2026 edition announced — L'Étape Poland (a separate
Tour de France-branded event) is confirmed for Zakopane, June 14 2026.

## Course

Two-lap rolling circuit through Kashubian hinterland — steep short climbs, little flat ground,
crosswind-exposed sections (organizer preview: "exposed sections where crosswinds could split
the field"), timed mountain prime and sprint prime, fast technical descending finish. Official
2025 rulebook: "a road-cycling event on closed public roads — effectively paved/asphalt
throughout." Official 2025 race report: 2025 winner Adrian Mrówka described a fast descending
finale followed by a sharp corner, calling the course selective and the sprint an all-in risk.

## Credible criticisms

- 2020 cancellation dispute: 100+ riders contested the organizer's refusal to refund a 100 zł
  administrative fee; Wejherowo county reportedly had not approved the route before the
  pandemic hit
- Significant local traffic disruption — 2025 closures affected a major expressway junction and
  multiple neighborhoods, drawing resident complaints about limited escape routes

## Flagged claim: [age claim] history.reputation — "The oldest cycling event still running"

**MOSTLY TRUE, with a documented nuance — text left as-is (defensible,
widely-used superlative), evidence recorded here.**

Findings:
- Paris-Brest-Paris was created by Pierre Giffard (Le Petit Journal) in 1891
  as a 1,200 km demonstration of the bicycle's practicality. First edition
  ran September 1891.
- Bordeaux-Paris (600 km), also created by Giffard, actually predates PBP by
  a few months in the same year (May 1891 vs. September 1891) — technically
  the older of the two 1891 Giffard races. However, Bordeaux-Paris was
  discontinued after 1988 and is NOT still running.
  Source: https://www.bordeaux-paris.com/evenement/historique/ — HTTP 200 verified.
- Cyclist.co.uk's own history-of-oldest-races piece notes a British hillclimb
  (from 1886) and an Australian track race (from 1887) also still run today,
  predating PBP by a few years — but these are a different discipline/format
  (hillclimb, velodrome track), not a long-distance open-road event.
  Source: https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/oldest-cycling-races (search-verified).
- Randonneurs USA (RUSA), the sanctioning body for PBP-style long-distance
  cycling in the US, states plainly: "Paris-Brest-Paris Randonneurs is the
  oldest bicycling event still run on a regular basis on the open road."
  Source: https://rusa.org/pages/PBP-short-history — HTTP 200 verified (already in profile citations).
- The competitive PBP race ran on a roughly decennial schedule from 1891 to
  1951 (7 editions); since 1931 it has continued in parallel — and, after
  1951, exclusively — as the non-competitive randonneur brevet held every
  four years, which is the modern PBP the profile describes (1,200 km, 90-hour
  cutoff, no rankings).
  Source: https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris-Brest-Paris (French Wikipedia, cross-verified via search).

**Conclusion:** The literal-absolute framing ("on earth," unqualified) is an
overstatement — Bordeaux-Paris was a few months older (but is defunct), and a
hillclimb/track race from the 1880s technically predate it in a different
format. But "oldest cycling event still run on a regular basis on the open
road" (the precise, sourced claim RUSA and most cycling press use, and which
the profile's own history.reputation field already states correctly) is
accurate and is the standard framing used across cycling journalism and by
PBP's own community (RUSA, Audax UK, Strava's editorial history piece). Per
brief guidance ("flagged claim true → leave text, dump carries the
evidence"), no text change made — this is a widely-used, well-sourced
superlative rather than a fabrication. Existing citations (RUSA short
history, Wikipedia, Strava editorial) already carry this evidence.

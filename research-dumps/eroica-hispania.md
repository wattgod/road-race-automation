# Research Dump: Eroica Hispania

## Quick Facts
- The Spanish member of the international Eroica family, run by Club Eroica Hispania (organizer Willy Mulonia) as an Eroica partner event
- Founded 2015 in Cenicero, La Rioja — inaugural June 2015 with ~600 riders; 2016 explicitly documented as the second edition
- Ran in Cenicero 2015-2019 (five editions); moved to Haro for a planned 2020 debut
- 2020 cancelled (COVID-19); an October 2021 attempt was cancelled immediately before the event over a permit/authorization dispute between the Riojan cycling federation and the DGT (accounts conflict on fault — organizer says paperwork was filed in time and it was a late-notified unpaid fee; Haro's government denies responsibility)
- First completed Haro edition: 2022. Last documented edition: June 4, 2023 (~619 entered, 607 the prior Haro year)
- Haro removed the event's budget/support for 2024 (organizer says a ~6-month negative response left no time to redesign four mixed-surface routes and get permits; Haro says the event "hadn't consolidated"; local reporting notes a ~€336,000 four-year sponsorship agreement)
- Organizer publicly described "hibernating" the event in Jan-Mar 2024, seeking a new host, hoping to return 2025 — no return materialized. A third-party 2025 listing marked its date explicitly "not confirmed."
- As of 2026-07-24: no 2024, 2025, or 2026 edition confirmed. No formal discontinuation announcement found either — Club Eroica Hispania remains listed by Riojan cycling authorities, but the event is absent from Eroica's own current event calendar.
- **Status verdict: dormant/indefinite hiatus, not confirmed active or confirmed defunct.** Do not use "active" (no current listing anywhere); do not use blanket "cancelled" (only the specific 2024 edition was explicitly cancelled); do not claim "confirmed defunct" (no shutdown announcement exists).

## Course Profile (last real edition, Haro, 2023)
Four routes, contemporaneously advertised:
- Paseo Eroico: 24-25 km / ~250 m
- Short: 74 km / ~960-1,000 m
- Medium: 122 km / ~1,655-1,700 m
- Long: 184 km / ~3,076-3,700 m (two conflicting source figures for elevation — preserved as a range, not averaged)

Long route: start/finish Haro, through San Vicente de la Sonsierra, Laguardia, Labraza, Logroño, Navarrete, Nájera, San Millán de la Cogolla, Santo Domingo de la Calzada — incorporating sections of the Camino Francés and Camino de Santiago Vasco del Interior.

**Surface**: NOT paved-only. The organizer described four mixed routes with a substantial gravel/track component; contemporary coverage explicitly describes riding on both gravel and asphalt; one participant's route report estimated roughly 30-35% dirt/stone track (rider testimony, not an official measurement). The profile's prior "Paved roads" / road_surface: 1 classification materially understates the unpaved component.

**Vintage-bike requirement**: participants rode steel bicycles manufactured through 1987 in period clothing, consistent with Eroica's standard "heroic bike" definition across the whole Eroica family.

**Format**: noncompetitive — times were not ranked; every finisher was treated as a finisher, not a placed competitor.

**Field size**: ~600, more precisely 619 entered for the final 2023 edition (607 the year before).

**Entry fee**: a specialist third-party listing cites €78 for June 2023, not independently corroborated in surviving official material — treat as an unverified historical figure, not a current fee (there is no current edition to have a fee).

## Identity check
Confirmed: this is the genuine Spanish Eroica event (Cenicero 2015-2019, Haro 2022-2023), not Eroica Dolomiti, Eroica California, or any other Eroica-family location. The 2023 Spanish national cycling calendar (RFEC) independently lists it: Haro, June 4 2023, organizer Club Eroica Hispania, classification "Ciclismo para todos." "Eroica Hispania, Haro Capital del Rioja" and government references to "Eroica España" are loose naming for the same club/event, not separate races. After the Cenicero exit, the town created an unrelated independent retro event, "Rioja y Pedal" — a local substitute, not an Eroica Hispania rebrand.

## Taxonomy note
Fits the database as a `sportive` given its road-racing-bicycle base, long road-style distances, and noncompetitive mass-participation format — but it is fundamentally a mixed-surface vintage event (gravel/track sectors on every route), not a pure paved gran fondo. `terrain.surface`, `road_surface`, and `technical_rating` on the prior profile materially understated this.

## Dedupe determination
No duplicate found in the 425-slug corpus. Distinct from `eroica-california`, `eroica-cuba`, `eroica-dolomiti`, `eroica-germania`, `eroica-japan`, `eroica-south-africa`, and `leroica` — all separate Eroica-family locations, not the same event.

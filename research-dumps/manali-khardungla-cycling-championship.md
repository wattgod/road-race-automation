# Research Dump: Manali-Khardung La Cycling Championship

## Quick Facts
- Real organizer: Northern Escapes Outdoors Pvt. Ltd. (promoter Gaurav Schimar); Cycling 4 Change/Anil Uchil co-organized the last verified edition
- Last independently verified finish: **3 August 2015** — 63-rider field, named men's and women's winners (Laxmi Pradhan/Magar reported as a winner, described the event as a "nine-day MTB marathon")
- Official site (manalikhardungla.in) remains frozen on **7-16 August 2024** dates; no independent source confirms that edition occurred
- Promoter alone claims a 2022 edition (~100+ riders); unverified by any independent source
- Distance: promoter materials conflict — 500 km vs 510 km; our prior file's 558.4 km figure is unsupported by any source found
- Elevation: 35,000 ft / 10,668 m climbing matches file, but is itself an unverified promoter figure
- "Seven days" in file's cutoff_time was a misreading — the 2015 edition used 9 stages; this is a multi-stage event, not a fixed-cutoff endurance race
- Format: competitive timed stage race run in parallel with an untimed "adventure tour" option covering the same route — not a conventional one-day mass-start road championship
- Surface: predominantly paved high-altitude highway, interrupted by sand, gravel and rocky stretches
- Route: Manali (Himachal Pradesh) over the Great Himalaya into Trans-Himalayan Ladakh, through Leh, finishing atop Khardung La (~5,359-5,375 m)
- Rohtang Pass (~13,000 ft) marks the initial Manali-to-Lahaul crossing; four later passes reportedly exceed 15,000 ft
- The "world's highest motorable road/pass" framing attached to Khardung La in promoter marketing is outdated — Guinness World Records now recognizes Umling La (5,798.251 m) for that title

## Verdict
Status set to `unknown` (not `active`, not confirmed `defunct`) — there is no evidence a current edition has run since 2015, but no explicit cancellation announcement either. This is a live-database judgment call flagged for human review: the promoter continues light marketing (a frozen 2024 event page) without any verifiable recent finish line.

# Cappadocia Gran Fondo — Editorial Wave 10 Research Notes

Source: codex exec (gpt-5.6-sol, web search), 2026-07-24.

## Identity/status resolution (defunct)
No separate October race exists. The competitive Cappadocia Gran Fondo was historically
staged inside the Cappadocia Bike Festival (launched by Argeus in 2015, with 90 km/2,000m+
and 47 km/600m+ courses; Argeus itself founded in Cappadocia in 1993 by Aydın Ayhan Güney,
Muammer Sak and Veli Tören). The Turkish Cycling Federation briefly called the 2025
festival "Kapadokya Gran Fondo," but the organizer's actual road component is now the
**Büyük Sürüş** — and the 2026 edition's own terms explicitly state it is "not a race."
Cappadocia is absent from the federation's current 2026 Gran Fondo calendar. The festival
itself is alive; the competitive gran fondo format is not.

## Eligibility
**Status: defunct.** Official 2026 event terms (curl-verified 200):

closed-course competitive gran fondo).

## Course character
Not an off-road fairy-chimney trail, not a closed-road fondo. The current Büyük Sürüş
route links Ürgüp with Ortahisar, Göreme, Çavuşin, Karacaören, Akköy, Başdere,
Şahinefendi, Taşkınpaşa and Cemil before returning to Ürgüp. The historical competitive
Gran Fondo (pre-2019) was Uçhisar-based: 108 km long / 41 km short course; the short route
passed the iconic fairy chimneys, descended to Ürgüp, climbed toward Mustafapaşa,
continued through Ayvalı and Bahçeli, and returned to Uçhisar.

## Criticism / safety
A rider died during the 2018 short-course race after missing a bend and striking a tree
and road sign. Contemporary rider discussion (participant testimony, not a formal
investigation finding) criticized late publication of the risk report and specifically
called for the km-11 section to be removed. The event's shift to an open-traffic,
noncompetitive format is itself a material downgrade for riders expecting a protected
gran fondo experience.
